00 — Orientation

Start Here

Blood Magic is a progression mod about spending your own life — and other lives — as a crafting resource called Life Points (LP). You bleed LP into a Blood Altar, store it in a Soul Network via Blood Orbs, and spend it on runes, sigils, rituals, demonic tooling, living armour, and alchemy.

Version reality check

The CurseForge page “Ragnaroks Unofficial Blood Magic” is not a custom mod — it is the classic Blood Magic by WayofTime, repackaged for the Ragnarok Reincarnated server as BloodMagic-1.19.2-3.3.0-42.jar.

One honest caveat: WayofTime's mod jumped 1.18.2 → 1.20.1, and the “3.3.0” version tag belongs to the 1.20.1 line — there is no official 1.19.2 build. The Ragnarok jar is a backport/repackage of that same 3.3.x codebase, so all of the mechanics and numbers in this guide (read straight from the 3.3.x source and the in-game Sanguine Scientum guidebook) apply. If a recipe in your pack disagrees, trust the in-game book.

What this version actually includes

3.3.x is the modern rewrite, not the old 1.7.10 altar-and-rituals mod. Expect all of:

core

Blood Altar

Six tiers, runes, blood orbs and the Soul Network. The spine of everything.

utility

Slates & Sigils

Imbued slates and a toolbox of toggle/use sigils — water, lava, mining, flight, magnetism.

automation

Rituals

Master Ritual Stone multiblocks like the Well of Suffering that auto-generate LP.

combat

Demon Will

Hellfire Forge, Tartaric Gems, five Will aspects and sentient weapons.

defense

Living Armor

Armour that trains as you play and spends a shared pool of upgrade points.

crafting

Alchemy

Alchemy Table, Arcane-Ash arrays, and the Incense Altar that supercharges self-sacrifice.

The danger framing

Self-sacrifice really drains your health. Early on you will be one careless tap away from death, with no LP to show for it. Build a safe, well-lit altar room, keep food and regeneration handy, and never sacrifice at low health. This guide will tell you when each shortcut becomes safe.

01 — The opening

First Blood

Everything starts with a bare altar and a knife. Here is the exact opening sequence.

  1. Craft a Blood Altar. A standalone altar (no runes around it) is Tier 1 with a base capacity of 10,000 LP.
  2. Craft a Sacrificial Dagger. Right-clicking near the altar with it adds +200 LP to the altar and costs you about one heart of health 100 LP / HP, ×2 = 200. Eat, wait to regen, repeat.
  3. Drop a Diamond in the altar and bleed until it has absorbed 2,000 LP. The diamond transmutes into a Weak Blood Orb.
  4. Bind the orb — hold right-click with it in an empty hand. It now represents your personal Soul Network (a Weak orb holds 5,000 LP).
  5. Make Blank Slates in the altar from Stone (1,000 LP each).
  6. Craft Blood Runes (Blank Runes) from a Blank Slate + your bound orb + stone, and ring the altar with 8 of them to reach Tier 2.
First sigil first

Your very first craft should arguably be the Divination Sigil (free to use). Right-click it to read your network LP; right-click an altar to read its tier, contents and capacity. You are flying blind without it.

06 — Automation

Rituals

A ritual is a Master Ritual Stone (MRS) surrounded by painted Ritual Stones. Hold a Ritual Diviner, pick a ritual, then right-click the MRS to ghost-build every stone from your inventory; activate by right-clicking the MRS with an Activation Crystal. The activation LP is pulled from your network at once, so the network must already hold the cost.

Two big version gotchas

The Well of Suffering activation is 40,000 in 3.3.x (old wikis say 50,000), and the “25” you'll read is the LP each mob is worth, not a flat rate. Build it above the altar with a mob spawner/grinder; it is the headline hands-off LP engine.

Gathering of the Forsaken Souls changed meaning: in 3.3.x it's a crystal-growth ritual (it applies Will catalysts from a chest), not the old “kill mobs for Will” ritual. Get raw Will from Snares and Sentient tools instead (see §07).

The base Ritual Diviner only builds basic rituals; advanced (Dusk) rituals need a Dusk-upgraded Diviner (Tier-4 altar + Dusk Elemental Inscription Tools, 2,000 LP each). The Weak Activation Crystal handles every staple above; Awakened is for higher tiers.

Getting & growing Will

  • Soul Snare — throw at a mob; when it sparkles white, kill it inside the window to drop a Demonic Will chunk.
  • Sentient Sword (and Axe, Pickaxe, Shovel, Scythe, Bow) — kills drop Will far more reliably and auto-fill the gem you carry. The standard farm once you can craft one.
  • Demon Crucible burns Will Crystals of an aspect into the chunk's Demon Will Aura; clusters in that aura grow up to 7 spires. Will Catalysts (applied via Gathering of the Forsaken Souls) speed growth ~10× and cut the per-spire cost from 45 to 25.

A Sentient Gem lets you pick the dominant aspect; sentient gear pulls Will from a held/equipped Tartaric Gem and gets stronger the more Will you carry.

The Demon Will Aura & its machines

Every loaded chunk holds an invisible, per-aspect Demon Will Aura — a pool of each of the five Wills. Tartaric Gems are your portable Will; the aura is the world's. Three blocks move Will between the two:

Demon Crucible
Hellfire Forge build · holds a gem/crystal

Burns the Will out of a held Tartaric Gem or Will Crystal and pumps it into the chunk's aura. The pressure valve that feeds everything below.

Demon Crystallizer
draws from aura

Once the chunk's aura clears a threshold, it grows a Demon Will Crystal cluster on top of itself, slowly converting aura into solid, harvestable Will.

Demon Pylon
balances neighbours

Equalises aura with adjacent chunks — place a line of them to spread one rich chunk's Will out to a row of crystallizers.

Demon Will Crystal Cluster
harvest with any pickaxe

Grows up to 7 spires. Break the outer spires for raw Will crystals and leave one to regrow. Aspect it (Corrosive/Destructive/Vengeful/Steadfast) with catalysts.

The hands-off endgame Will farm is a single chunk: one Crucible raising the aura, several Crystallizers harvesting it back out, optionally a Gathering of the Forsaken Souls ritual applying catalysts to aspect and speed the crystals. Lay it out like this:

Bootstrapping the aura

You face a chicken-and-egg: crystallizers need a full aura, but the aura starts empty. Prime it by feeding the Crucible a few full Tartaric Gems (charged from Soul-Snare/Sentient-Sword kills) until the aura clears the crystallizer threshold, then let the loop run. A Sentient Sword on a mob farm is the easiest Will income to get there.

08 — The carapace

Living Armor

Living Armour is a full set that trains as you play and spends a shared pool of upgrade points. Craft each piece from iron armour + Binding Reagent + Arcane Ashes (place them and wait for the transform).

  • Point cap: the set starts with a 100-point budget. The Draft of Angelus raises it to 200; the Ritual of Living Evolution pushes it to 300. You choose a build inside that budget.
  • Training: upgrades level up by doing the matching activity — mining, taking hits, sprinting, eating.
  • Re-spec: the Sound of the Cleansing Soul ritual strips all upgrades into Upgrade Tomes (one per ability, level retained). Re-apply a tome in hand, or combine two identical tomes in an anvil.

The upgrade roster

Teleport, honestly

A true teleport array is limited/experimental in 3.3.x. For reliable teleportation use the Teleposer + Sigil of Teleposition (1,000 LP/use): bind the sigil to one Teleposer, stand on a second, and swap places (and any blocks/entities on the pads).

The Incense Altar multiblock supercharges self-sacrifice. 10 — The arsenal

Bound & Sentient Tools

Blood Magic gives you two parallel weapon lines, each powered by one of the mod's two currencies. Bound tools run on LP from your Soul Network; Sentient tools run on Demon Will from a carried gem. Both are fast and strong; which you favour depends on which economy you've built out.

Bound tools — LP-powered

Forged in the altar around a high-tier orb, a bound tool sits inert until you hold right-click to “activate” it, at which point it draws LP from your network and becomes brutally efficient. Let go and it costs nothing.

Bound Blade

While active, a fast, high-damage sword. Drains LP per swing/second held.

Bound Pickaxe

Near-instant mining of stone/ores while active; great with a fat network.

Bound Axe

Felling and combat in one; chews wood while active.

Bound Shovel

Wide, instant dirt/gravel clearing for big digs.

Altar tier · T4+ Cost · LP per use while active Upkeep · none when idle

Because cost is paid only while the trigger is held, bound tools are effectively free to carry — the danger is draining your whole network mid-fight. Keep a Well of Suffering topping you up.

Sentient tools — Will-powered

Forged in the Hellfire Forge (see §07), a sentient tool pulls Will from a Tartaric Gem in your inventory and scales with the Will you carry and the aspect you've locked with the Sentient Gem:

  • Raw — straight damage & attack speed.
  • Corrosive — applies Poison/Wither on hit.
  • Destructive — highest raw hit, best for filling gems fast.
  • Vengeful — fast swings + a movement buff that scales with Will held.
  • Steadfast — Absorption after a kill for a defensive farm loop.

The full line is Sentient Sword, Bow, Pickaxe, Axe, Shovel and Scythe (the Scythe is the AoE mob-farm star). A sentient weapon also refills the gem it draws from on kills, so a single Greater gem + Sentient Sword is a self-sustaining Will engine.

The Lava Crystal

Not a weapon, but the everyday LP tool you'll use most: a Lava Crystal is a furnace fuel that burns on LP from your network instead of coal. Drop one in a furnace's fuel slot and it smelts indefinitely as long as your network has LP. Altar-forged; a quiet quality-of-life win once you have a Well running.

Bound vs Sentient — the honest call

If your LP income (a Well) is strong, bound tools are simpler — no gem juggling. If you've invested in a Will garden, sentient gear hits harder and feeds your Will economy as you fight. Most players end up carrying a Sentient Sword for kills (it makes Will) and a bound or sentient pickaxe for digging. Exact recipes and costs are pack-tunable — check JEI.

11 — Logistics

Automation & Routing

Once the Well, the forge and the Will garden are all running, the late game is plumbing — moving items, fluids and LP between them without you standing there. Blood Magic ships its own logistics network and treats LP itself as a pumpable fluid.

The Routing Node network

A self-contained item network built from three node types plus a linking tool. Place a chain of nodes, link each to the master with the Node Router, attach inventories, and items flow on a tick.

Master Routing Node
one per network

The brain. Every input/output node must be linked back to a single master, and the master is the heart you build the rest around.

Output Routing Node
pulls from its inventory

Takes items out of the chest/machine it touches and pushes them into the network. Filterable by item, with a priority number.

Input Routing Node
inserts into its inventory

Receives items from the network and places them into the chest/machine it touches. Filter + priority decide what lands where.

Node Router
linking tool

Right-click the master, then a node, to wire them together. Also used to read/clear links.

A clean Well loop

Output node on your mob-drop chest → Input node on a sorting array, or → Input node feeding a furnace bank fuelled by a Lava Crystal. Set filters (whitelist the drops you want) and priority (negative numbers fill last) so overflow routes to a void chest instead of clogging the master.

LP as a fluid — Life Essence

LP exists in-world as a fluid called Life Essence. The altar can be drained or filled with it, which means you can:

  • Pump LP out of one altar into a tank and across to a far-off crafting altar.
  • Buffer a Well's overflow into storage so nothing is wasted when the altar caps.
  • Bridge to other mods' fluid logistics (pipes, pumps) since Life Essence behaves like any Forge fluid.
Mind the tier wall

You can pipe LP around, but a craft still needs the destination altar at the right tier and with enough capacity for that single recipe. Plumbing solves transport, not the tier requirement — keep a fully-runed crafting altar at the end of the pipe.

Teleposer logistics

The Teleposer (driven by a Telepositional Focus) swaps the blocks and entities standing on two linked pads. Beyond travel it's a blunt automation tool — shuttle a stack of mobs into a kill room, or relocate a block of ore on a timer. 1,000 LP per activation.

13 — The path

Progression Roadmap

Everything above, in the order that actually works. Each phase unlocks the tools that make the next one painless — chase them roughly in this sequence and you'll never be stuck bleeding for an hour with nothing to show for it.

  1. Survive the bleeding

    Bootstrap (Tier 1)

    • Craft the Blood Altar + Sacrificial Dagger + Divination Sigil.
    • Bleed a diamond into a Weak Blood Orb; bind it.
    • Make Blank Slates and 8 Blood Runes → reach Tier 2.
  2. Stop hurting yourself

    The Alchemy bench & first sigils (T2–T3)

    • Build the Alchemy Table; make Arcane Ash and your first array-bound sigils (Water, Fast Miner, Magnetism).
    • Add Speed + Self-Sacrifice runes; ring out to Tier 3 with pillars + glowstone.
    • Upgrade to the Apprentice, then Magician's orb.
  3. Make LP without dying

    Automate the blood (T3–T4)

    • Build a Ritual Diviner + Weak Activation Crystal.
    • Run a Well of Suffering over a mob farm above the altar — the headline hands-off LP engine.
    • Reach Tier 4, craft the Master Blood Orb, and build an Incense Altar for burst self-sacrifice.
  4. Open the second economy

    Demon Will (T4)

    • Craft the Hellfire Forge; gather Will with Soul Snares → a Sentient Sword.
    • Climb the Tartaric Gem ladder; build a Crucible + Crystallizer garden.
    • Use Gathering of the Forsaken Souls to aspect & grow crystals.
  5. Become unkillable

    Living Armor & the arsenal (T4–T5)

    • Transmute iron armour into Living Armor; train a themed build.
    • Drink the Draft of Angelus (cap 200), then run Ritual of Living Evolution (cap 300).
    • Forge Bound or Sentient tools; carry a Lava Crystal.
  6. Industrialise & beautify

    Endgame (T5)

    • Push the altar to Tier 5 with four Beacons; craft the Archmage Orb.
    • Wire Routing Nodes and pipe Life Essence between altars.
    • Sink it all into The Sanguine Crypt (§14) and stand back.

14 — The sanctum

Build It Beautiful — The Sanguine Crypt

A functional altar is a pile of runes in a hole. The Sanguine Crypt sinks that altar into a circular ritual pit at the base of your spire, open to a vertical skylight, ringed by a balcony of ritual stones. Darker and more gothic than the coastal spruce-and-grey above it — deepslate, blackstone, crimson, and cold soul-fire blue against the altar's warm glow.

Each module below is a small, symmetric, tileable piece with its own palette. Step through the Y-layers (bottom → top); the rail tallies the full-build block list for that piece.

1 · Blood-Channel Floor

The first thing anyone sees: red veins fanning from the altar across a deepslate-brick floor.

2 · Ribbed Vault Bay

A repeatable wall section — polished blackstone with dark-oak ribs that arch into a lantern-lit vault. Tile it around the pit's circumference.

3 · Pillar & Sconce

A column that lines up with the altar's own corner pillars, carrying a crimson banner and a cold soul-fire sconce.

4 · Skylight Shaft

The vertical light-well punched up through the spire — crying obsidian weeping faint purple, glass-quoined corners, a ring of soul-lantern light partway up.

5 · Balcony Ring

The upper gallery overlooking the pit, where your real ritual stones live and you stand to watch the altar work.

Putting it together

Sink the altar (§02) at the centre of the pit; lay the Blood-Channel Floor around it; ring the pit wall with Ribbed Vault Bays punctuated by Pillar & Sconce modules aligned to the altar's corners; punch the Skylight Shaft straight up the middle; and crown the whole pit with the Balcony Ring. Keep the functional ritual pad (Well of Suffering, §06) on the balcony level or just off the pit so mobs don't fall onto your head.
