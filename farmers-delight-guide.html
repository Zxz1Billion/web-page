Pull up a chair by the stove. Farmer's Delight turns Minecraft's thin diet of porkchops and bread into a proper country kitchen — new crops in the garden, a Cooking Pot bubbling over the fire, a Cutting Board by the window, and hearty home-cooked meals that keep you full and warm for the long road ahead. This guide walks the whole homestead, from first seed to a feast big enough for the whole base.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Farmer's Delight expands farming and cooking with hearty meals, a handful of new crops, and a set of kitchen workstations. Its food shines because so many dishes hand you long, gentle buffs — Nourishment from hearty plates and Comfort from soups and drinks — and because bowls and plates stack neatly in your pack instead of clogging it one steak at a time.

If you remember three things from this whole cookbook, remember these. Everything else is seasoning:

the heart

Cooking Pot

Set it over a heat source, drop in a few ingredients and a container item (usually a Bowl), and it simmers stews, soups and meals.

the prep

Cutting Board

Right-click it with a tool to process an item — a Knife chops, an axe splits logs, shears snip. Many recipes start here.

the harvest

New crops

Tomatoes, Onions, Cabbage and Rice join your garden, foraged first as wild plants out in the world.

Why bother, when steak exists?

  • Bigger meals. A pot of stew refills far more hunger and saturation than a single grilled chop.
  • Lasting buffs. Soups grant Comfort and hearty plates grant Nourishment — slow-burning effects that keep you topped up on long trips.
  • Tidy packing. Bowls and plates stack, so a day's food is a single slot, not a column of meat.
  • It uses the whole garden. Odds and ends — a carrot here, an onion there — all find a place in the pot.
Your first afternoon

Forage some wild crops for seeds, lay out a Cooking Pot over a campfire or stove, keep a Knife and a Cutting Board by the counter, and cook your first Vegetable Soup. That tiny kitchen already feeds a base — the rest of this guide is just refining the recipe.

01 — The workstations

The Kitchen

A Farmer's Delight kitchen is a little cluster of blocks, each with one honest job. Lay them out the way you'd arrange a real counter — heat in the middle, a board for prep, a basket to catch the scraps — and the whole thing hums along while you do other work.

heat + container

Cooking Pot

The cornerstone. Place it over a heat source, fill its ingredient slots, and supply a container item (a Bowl for stews) so the finished meal has something to ladle into. Outputs collect for you to pull.

right-click prep

Cutting Board

Hold an item, right-click the board to set it down, then right-click with a tool to process it. Knives chop crops and meat, axes split logs into planks, shears and pickaxes have their own recipes.

heat source

Stove

Cook food directly on top — no fuel-in-a-furnace-slot needed; it burns its own fuel below. It also counts as a heat source for a Cooking Pot placed on it. The tidy heart of a built-in kitchen.

pan + weapon

Skillet

Cook food held in-hand, or place it as a block to pan-fry over heat. In a pinch it doubles as a heavy weapon — a hot skillet to the face is a real attack.

storage

Basket

A hopper-like container that pulls and pushes items. Use it to catch a farm's harvest or to feed ingredients into your pot and clear finished meals.

campfire

Campfires

The humble starter heat source. A lit Campfire (or Soul Campfire) under a Cooking Pot gets you simmering before you've built a proper stove.

The heat-source rule

The Cooking Pot does not cook on its own — it needs heat underneath it. That heat can come from a Campfire, a Soul Campfire, a Farmer's Delight Stove, or other blocks the pack flags as heat sources. No heat, no simmer; the pot just sits there with cold ingredients.

Build it once, cook forever

Set a Stove in the counter, drop a Cooking Pot on top, and tuck a Basket beside it to catch finished bowls. That three-block kitchen will feed your homestead from now until the end of the modpack.

02 — The blade

Knives & Tools

The Knife is Farmer's Delight's signature new tool type. On the Cutting Board it's your everyday prep blade — slicing meat into cuts, chopping crops, breaking foods down into ingredients — and out in the field it's a light, quick weapon with its own swing.

Knife materials

Knives come in the familiar tiers plus a couple of Delight-specific ones. Higher tiers last longer and cut a touch better, but even a humble Flint Knife handles nearly every recipe on the board.

Right-click quality-of-life add-ons

This pack ships two small mods that make farming and ranching feel wonderfully hands-on. They're not strictly part of Farmer's Delight, but they pair so naturally with a kitchen homestead that they belong here.

RightClickHarvest

Right-click to harvest

Right-click a mature crop to harvest it and automatically replant the seed. Walk your rows tapping each plant — no breaking and re-sowing by hand. Some setups let it cost a little hunger or a tool.

Right Click & Milk

Right-click to milk

Right-click a cow with a bucket (and goats/other animals where supported) to milk them with a satisfying directness, instead of fumbling the vanilla interaction.

The two-tap garden

With RightClickHarvest active, a long bed of wheat, carrots, potatoes — and your new Tomatoes, Onions and Cabbages — becomes a stroll: tap, tap, tap, and the rows replant themselves behind you. Keep a Knife in the other hand for whatever wanders into the field.

03 — The garden

Crops & Farming

Before you can cook, you need a garden. Farmer's Delight adds several crops, each first found in the wild and then domesticated into neat rows. There's no tree-tapping or sap here — just honest dirt, water, and patience.

Foraging the wild crops

You won't find a tomato in your starting inventory — you find its ancestor growing wild. Scout the biomes for wild plants and break them for seeds and starter food:

  • 🍅 Wild Tomatoes
  • 🥕 Wild Carrots
  • 🥔 Wild Potatoes
  • 🧅 Wild Onions
  • 🫐 Wild Beetroots
  • 🌾 Wild Rice
  • 🥬 Wild Cabbages

Each wild patch drops the seeds or roots you need to start a proper bed. Gather a few of each, bring them home, and your garden plan writes itself.

Soil, compost & straw

Farmer's Delight closes the loop between kitchen scraps and the garden. Spare vegetables, seeds and plant matter become fertiliser, and fertiliser becomes faster-growing soil:

compost

Organic Compost

Toss surplus crops and plant scraps onto a compost heap to break them down into Organic Compost — the garden's reward for a tidy kitchen.

soil

Rich Soil

Compost enriches dirt into Rich Soil, which helps nearby crops grow faster. The Rich Soil ↔ Compost cycle keeps a serious farm humming.

byproduct

Straw

Straw (from wheat/grain) is a handy building and crafting material — bales, blocks, and the odd recipe. Nothing from the harvest goes to waste.

No sap, no tapping

Unlike some farming mods, Farmer's Delight doesn't add tree-tapping or syrup. Its farming is grounded and familiar: water for the rice, farmland for the rest, compost to speed it all up.

04 — At the stove

Cooking in the Pot

Here's where the homestead earns its keep. The Cooking Pot takes a small handful of ingredients plus a container item and, given heat and a little time, turns them into a finished meal. Get the ingredients right and the container is the spoon: a Bowl for stews and soups, sometimes nothing for dry dishes.

How a recipe works

  1. Place the Cooking Pot over a heat source (campfire or stove).
  2. Fill the pot's ingredient slots with the recipe's items — often prepped first on the Cutting Board.
  3. Add the container item (usually a Bowl) so the meal has something to be served in.
  4. Wait while it simmers; the finished meal appears in the output slot.
  5. Pull it out by hand or with a Basket/hopper — and start the next pot.

05 — The big table

Feasts & Sharing

Some dishes are too grand for a bowl. Feasts are food blocks you place down on a table and eat from several times — each serving carved off until the dish is gone. They're the centrepiece of a base kitchen and a gift to multiplayer servers: cook once, feed the whole crew.

poultry

Roast Chicken Block

A whole roasted bird set on the table; carve servings until it's picked clean. A satisfying, hearty feast.

harvest

Stuffed Pumpkin

Pumpkin packed with rice and vegetables — an autumn feast that serves a small party.

holiday

Honey Glazed Ham

Sweet-glazed pork, sliced from the block. The showpiece of a celebration spread.

baked

Shepherd's Pie

Mutton and potato baked into a shareable pie; scoop out portions as needed.

medley

Rice Roll Medley

An assorted platter of rice rolls — light, pretty, and great for grazing during a build session.

Why a feast beats a stack of bowls

  • Shared on the spot. Anyone walking past the table can take a serving — no handing out food.
  • Set-and-forget. Place it once at base; it sits ready until eaten, like a pie or cake.
  • Throughput. One cook fills many bellies, which is exactly what a busy modpack base wants.
A standing feast at base

Keep at least one feast block on a table near your spawn point or your main work area. Between dungeon runs and rocket prep, a quick serving off the Roast Chicken or Shepherd's Pie tops everyone up without breaking stride.

06 — The long buff

Comfort & Nourishment

This is the real reason a Farmer's Delight kitchen pays for itself. Beyond filling your hunger bar, the right meals grant slow, long-duration buffs that vanilla steak simply can't match — and they're what make home-cooked food the right fuel for exploration.

soups & drinks

Comfort

Granted by many soups and warm drinks. While it's active it keeps refreshing your saturation over time, so you stay topped up far longer than a single meal would suggest.

hearty meals

Nourishment

Granted by hearty, substantial plates. It similarly extends how long a meal carries you, rewarding you for cooking the bigger dishes rather than nibbling.

Why this matters on a long trip

Out in a dungeon or on the way to the Moon, your hunger ticks down with every sprint and jump. A pile of grilled meat refills the bar but burns off fast. A pot of soup, by contrast, leaves a buff working quietly in the background — you eat once and keep coasting on it, freeing up inventory slots and attention for the actual adventure.

Durations kept approximate

The exact length and strength of Comfort and Nourishment depend on the dish and the pack's configuration, so this guide doesn't quote numbers. Watch the buff icons and your saturation in game to learn which of your favourite meals carry you furthest. approx · in-game truth

Pack a soup, not a steak

Before a big expedition, cook a few stackable soup bowls. They take one inventory slot, refill more than steak, and leave Comfort running — the closest thing to a packed lunch this modpack offers.

07 — The extended pantry

The Add-ons in this Pack

To the Moon 2.0 doesn't stop at vanilla Farmer's Delight — it stacks several add-ons on top, each widening the menu and tying the kitchen into the rest of the modpack. Here's what each one brings to the counter.

fishing

Aquaculture Delight

Bridges Farmer's Delight with Aquaculture 2: the new fish you reel in can be sliced on the Cutting Board and cooked into fish dishes, so your catch becomes proper meals instead of just raw filets.

tinkers

Tinkers Delight

Lets your Tinkers' Construct tools work on the Cutting Board (and adds dishes/integration), so a custom-forged knife or cleaver earns its place in the kitchen as well as the mine.

huge menu

Extra Delight

The big expansion: many more foods, baking and ovens, candy, drinks, a watering can for the garden, and a long list of new recipes. If the base menu felt short, this fills the larder.

twilight forest

Twilight's Flavors & Delight

Turns Twilight Forest ingredients into Farmer's Delight dishes, so the loot and produce of that dimension finds its way into your cooking pot.

QoL harvest

RightClickHarvest

Right-click mature crops to harvest and replant in one motion — the backbone of a low-effort garden, covered back in §02.

QoL ranch

Right Click and Milk

Right-click cows and other animals with a bucket to milk them directly — small, but it makes the dairy side of the kitchen feel natural.

One ecosystem

Together these add-ons mean almost everything you grow, catch, forge or loot in the pack has a home in your kitchen. The Cooking Pot and Cutting Board become the common endpoint for a dozen different mods.

08 — Putting it together

Tips & Synergy

You've got the stations, the crops, the recipes and the buffs. Here's how to wire them into a kitchen that runs itself and feeds the pack's long, hungry adventures.

The self-running kitchen

  1. Set a Stove in the counter and a Cooking Pot on top for fuel-free, always-hot cooking.
  2. Feed ingredients in and pull finished meals out with Baskets or hoppers so the pot never sits idle.
  3. Run a small farm with RightClickHarvest into a Basket, dropping straight into your prep chests.
  4. Keep a standing feast on a table at base for instant, shareable food.
  5. Compost the scraps into Rich Soil and loop it back to the farm.

Cross-mod pairings

tools

Knives ✕ Tinkers

With Tinkers Delight, forge a custom blade and use it on the Cutting Board — one tool for prep, combat and mining.

fish

Pot ✕ Aquaculture

Turn the day's catch into Baked Cod Stew and other fish dishes instead of letting filets pile up.

expeditions

Soups ✕ the void

Stack soup bowls for space trips and dungeon dives — Comfort keeps you fed through the long haul.

garden

Watering can ✕ compost

Extra Delight's watering can plus Rich Soil makes a fast, renewable garden that never runs the pantry dry.

Feeding the long road

This is the payoff. The pack sends you on space trips, dungeon dives and dimension hops that chew through hunger fast. A pocketful of stackable soups — each leaving Comfort running — turns food from a constant chore into a single slot you barely think about. Cook the kitchen up once, and it quietly feeds every expedition that follows.

The cook's whole philosophy

Grow it, prep it on the board, simmer it in the pot, share it from a feast, and let the buffs carry you. A warm kitchen at base is worth more on a long journey than another chest of raw meat.

★ — The path

Progression — from cutting board to a feast hall

Every homestead in To the Moon 2.0 starts the same way: a hungry traveller, a handful of wild seeds, and one good blade. Farmer's Delight has no tech tree to grind — its progression is the natural arc of a kitchen growing up, from a single soup over a campfire to an automated feast hall that fuels the whole pack. Here's that arc, start to finish, with the add-ons folded in where they belong.

  1. Forage and arm yourself. On day one, scout the biomes for wild crops — wild tomatoes, onions, carrots, cabbages and rice — and gather their seeds and roots. Craft your first Knife (a Flint Knife is plenty) so you have a prep blade and a light weapon in one.
  2. Lay the first kitchen. Build a Cutting Board by the counter, a Cooking Pot, and set them over a campfire or Stove for your first heat. Cook a humble Vegetable Soup — that tiny three-block kitchen already feeds a base.
  3. Plant the garden and close the loop. Sow the foraged crops into proper rows — tomato vines, onions, cabbage on farmland and rice along the waterline. Start a compost heap for surplus scraps, and let the Rich Soil ↔ Organic Compost cycle speed every bed. With RightClickHarvest, the rows replant themselves as you stroll them.
  4. Cook the meals that carry you. Graduate from starter soups to hearty plates — stews, pasta, roasts — and learn which dishes hand you Comfort (soups and warm drinks) and Nourishment (substantial plates). These long, gentle buffs are what make home-cooked food the right fuel for The Ascent and its long expeditions.
  5. Raise a feast hall. Once the garden overflows, cook feast blocks — Roast Chicken, Stuffed Pumpkin, Honey Glazed Ham, Shepherd's Pie — and stand one on a table at base. Cook once, feed the whole crew or server; perfect between dungeon runs and rocket prep.
  6. Integrate the add-ons. Widen the menu as the pack opens up: Aquaculture Delight turns your catch into fish stews, Extra Delight adds ovens, baking, drinks and a watering can, Tinkers Delight lets a forged blade work the Cutting Board, and Twilight's Flavors & Delight brings that dimension's loot into the pot.
  7. Automate the whole kitchen. Wire Baskets and hoppers to feed ingredients into the pot and pull finished meals out, catch the farm's harvest into prep chests, and loop compost back to the beds. The kitchen now runs itself while you adventure.
Good food fuels the pack

The whole arc pays off the moment you leave base. A pocketful of stackable soups — each leaving Comfort quietly running — turns hunger from a constant chore into a single inventory slot, carrying the pack through space trips, dungeon dives and dimension hops. Build the kitchen up once, and it feeds every long expedition that follows.
