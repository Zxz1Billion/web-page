00 — Orientation

Start Here

Apotheosis is not one mod but a bundle of modules stitched onto Minecraft's loot, mobs and enchanting. It rewards killing things and opening chests — every drop is a lottery ticket for an item rolled with random affixes, gems and stats. There is no tech tree, only the hunt for better numbers.

Apotheosis is authored by Shadows_of_Fire and depends on the library mod Placebo (also by Shadows). Everything it does is broken into self-contained modules, each toggle-able and deeply configurable. Learn what each one touches and the whole grimoire reads cleanly:

loot

Adventure

The flagship: affixed gear, six rarity tiers, gems & socketing, the salvaging loop and the randomized apothic bosses that drop it all.

arcana

Enchantment

A full enchanting rework — new enchantments, raised max levels, and the Eterna/Quanta/Arcana table powered by library blocks.

utility

Spawner · Potion · Village

Editable mob spawners, supercharged potions & the Potion Charm, and new villager trades. The quality-of-life half of the mod.

The three currencies of power

  • Rarity — every affixed item, gem and boss reward sits on a tier ladder (Common → Ancient). Higher rarity means more/stronger affixes and bigger rolls.
  • Luck — the vanilla stat, now load-bearing. Luck biases loot toward higher rarity and better affix rolls. Build it up before you farm.
  • Rarity / Gem materials — the crafting "ink" (gem dust, sigils, rarity materials) used to reroll affixes, cut gems, and socket gear.
First hour

Kill mobs and crack chests until an affixed item drops (it has a coloured name and bonus tooltip lines). Build a Salvaging Table early so junk affix gear becomes materials, and start drinking Luck potions before any serious farming run. Everything compounds from there.

01 — Affix loot

The Adventure Module

The Adventure module is what most people mean by "Apotheosis." It sprinkles affixes — bonus modifiers — onto weapons, armour, tools and bows as they drop from mobs and chests. Each item rolls a rarity, and the rarity decides how many affixes it carries and how generous the rolls are.

Rarity tiers

Rarity is colour-coded on the item's name. The ladder below is the default Apotheosis order; the higher you climb, the more affixes and the larger the stat ranges (exact counts and colours are configurable):

Numbers are approximate

Apotheosis rolls affixes, rarity and stat ranges randomly, and every value is config-tunable. Treat tier names and the direction of each effect as reliable; read the live item tooltip and JEI for the actual numbers in your pack.

What affixes do

Affixes come in two flavours and attach to a "loot category" (weapon, armour, tool, bow/ranged, shield):

  • Stat affixes raise numbers — extra damage, armour, attack speed, draw speed, movement, mining speed, crit, life-on-hit, and so on.
  • Effect (named) affixes add behaviour — chaining/explosive arrows, leeching, thorns-like retaliation, on-hit potions, miner's fortune, and similar. These are the ones that flip how the item feels to use.

Rerolling & salvaging

Two stations close the loop on affix gear:

reroll

Rarity Materials

Spend rarity materials (and gem dust) to reroll an item's affixes or nudge its rarity, hunting for a better combination on a base you like.

recycle

Salvaging Table

Feed unwanted affix gear in and break it down into materials & gem dust. Every bad drop becomes fuel for improving a good one.

Luck is the dial

Drops scale with the Luck stat of whoever lands the kill or opens the chest. Higher Luck shifts the rarity roll upward and tightens affix rolls toward the good end. Luck potions, beacons and gear that grant Luck pay for themselves on a farm.

02 — The hunt

Bosses & Invaders

Scattered through the world, Apotheosis spawns apothic bosses — randomized mini-bosses built from ordinary mob types but kitted out with a name, a boss bar, buffed stats, and a full set of high-rarity affixed gear they actually use against you. They are the richest source of top-tier loot in the mod.

Read them before you swing

A boss's danger and its reward both come from the affixes on its gear. The more (and rarer) the modifiers it visibly carries, the deadlier the fight — and the better the drop. A boss bristling with effect-affixes can chain attacks, reflect damage or apply nasty potions, so scout the boss bar's tooltip and gear before committing.

How a boss is built

  • Random base mob — a zombie, skeleton, etc., promoted to boss status with a generated epithet ("X the Brutal").
  • Affix gear — weapon, armour and sometimes a bow/shield, each rolled at high rarity; the boss inherits those affixes' powers.
  • Stat buffs — extra health, damage, knockback resistance and speed, shown on the boss bar.
  • Guaranteed loot — on death it drops its gear plus rarity materials and gems, weighted high by its tier.

Fighting tips

Bring Luck here too

Because boss drops are rolled like any other loot, fighting bosses with high Luck active raises the rarity of what they drop. A Luck potion before a boss fight is one of the highest-value buffs in the mod.

03 — Socketry

Gems & Socketing

Alongside affixed gear, Apotheosis drops gems — modular power chips you slot into socketed equipment. A gem has its own rarity and a type (the effect it grants), and the same gem behaves differently depending on which item category it goes into.

Anatomy of a gem

rarity

Quality tier

Gems roll on the same rarity ladder as gear. A higher-rarity gem gives a stronger bonus when socketed (and is worth more to fuse up).

type

Effect type

Each gem is a flavour — damage, life steal, armour, mining speed, attack speed, movement, and more — with values that depend on the slot it fills.

state

Unnamed vs Named

Raw "unnamed" gems must be processed before use; a finished, named gem is the socket-ready version with its rolled stats locked in.

From rough stone to socket

  1. Find a gem as loot from mobs, chests or bosses — often as a raw, unnamed gem.
  2. Gemcutting turns the rough gem into a finished, named gem with a rolled value, using gem dust (salvaged from gear and gems).
  3. Find or roll socketed gear — items carry a number of empty sockets based on their rarity.
  4. Socket the gem into an open slot using a sigil / gem-dust flow at the smithing/salvaging station; the gem's bonus now rides on the item.
  5. Gem Fusion combines duplicate gems to upgrade their rarity — the path to a single, very strong gem.
Match gem to slot

A "damage" gem in a sword and the same gem in a chestplate read differently — Apotheosis applies the gem's per-category bonus. Plan your gems around what each piece is for: offence gems in weapons, defence and utility gems in armour.

Sockets are precious

Socket count is set by an item's rarity and is hard to add later. Don't burn a good gem into a low-rarity base you'll replace — save your best fusions for Mythic/Ancient gear you intend to keep.

04 — The arcana

The Enchantment Module

Apotheosis tears the lid off vanilla enchanting. It raises max levels on many vanilla enchantments, adds a roster of new enchantments, and replaces the table's "0–30 levels" mystery with three readable stats you control: Eterna, Quanta and Arcana (see §05).

05 — Three stats

The Enchanting Table, Reworked

Vanilla bookshelves only ever pushed your table toward level 30. Apotheosis replaces that with three independent stats the surrounding blocks feed. Tune them and you control not just how high your enchants roll, but how consistent and how rare they are.

power

Eterna

Raw enchanting power — the effective level. More Eterna = higher-level enchantments are possible. This is the headline stat.

spread

Quanta

Randomness / variance in the result. High Quanta widens the range of outcomes; low Quanta makes results predictable.

treasure

Arcana

The chance of rolling rare / treasure enchantments. Crank Arcana when you're fishing for the hard-to-get ones.

Blocks that tune the stats

Different "library" blocks placed around the table contribute to different stats (and some come with diminishing returns or penalties). Plan the room around the stat you want:

Hover the table

The reworked table's GUI shows your current Eterna / Quanta / Arcana totals and each enchant slot's preview live. Build, place a block, and watch the numbers — it's the fastest way to learn what every block is worth in your pack. in-game ground truth

High & safe

For reliable top-tier enchants, push Eterna to the ceiling, keep Quanta low enough that you don't roll garbage, and raise Arcana only when specifically hunting treasure enchantments. Max Quanta + low Eterna is how people get disappointing books.

06 — Bookkeeping

Enchantment Library & Infusion

Rolling great enchantments is only half the battle — you also need to store, sort and apply them. Apotheosis adds the Enchantment Library, a bulk store for enchanted books that turns a chest full of random books into a searchable bank of exactly the enchant you want.

The Enchantment Library

  • Deposit books — feed it your enchanted-book overflow; it absorbs and catalogues every enchantment by level.
  • Extract / "draw" books — withdraw a book of a specific enchantment and level on demand, paying experience to print it.
  • Tiered libraries — bigger library tiers hold more total points and higher per-enchant levels, scaling with your needs.
Stop hoarding loose books

Treat the library as your enchant economy: dump every junk book in, and draw clean, exact-level books out when it's time to gear up. It also lets you combine levels — many low books become one high book.

Applying it all

Finished books reach your gear two ways:

anvil

Anvil

The familiar route — combine a book onto an item. Apotheosis-raised levels and new enchantments apply here like any other.

infusion

Infusion flow

Use the table/library workflow to push high-level and treasure enchantments onto gear that the vanilla anvil would balk at, respecting the new caps.

Watch the prior-work cost

Stacking many high-level enchantments still racks up anvil cost. Plan the order — apply the expensive, high-level enchants to a fresh item early, and use books drawn from the library to skip intermediate combines.

07 — Mob engineering

Spawners & the Garden

The Spawner module makes vanilla Mob Spawners capturable and editable — the classic enabler for high-yield mob farms feeding your affix-loot hunt. Pick a spawner up (with the right tool) and reconfigure how, when and how fast it spits out mobs.

Build the engine of the hunt

A modified spawner stacked with "ignore conditions" + faster delay + higher count, drained by a kill mechanism while you stand by with Luck and Scavenger, is the canonical Apotheosis affix-loot farm. Apply the modifiers in a crafting/anvil flow as the recipes allow.

The Chronicle / reference tome

Apotheosis ships an in-game reference book (the Chronicle of Shadows / Apotheosic reference) describing each module's items and recipes. When a number or recipe here is marked approximate, that tome — plus JEI — is the authoritative source for your pack.

Spawners can overload a server

"Ignore spawn cap" + minimal delay can flood chunks with entities and tank performance. Pair aggressive spawner settings with an instant kill chamber and reasonable counts, especially on multiplayer.

08 — The remaining modules

Potions, Villages & More

Beyond loot and enchanting, Apotheosis carries smaller modules that round out the RPG feel. Each is independently configurable and may be tuned or disabled by the pack.

The Potion module

  • Stronger & longer potions — extended brewing options push effects to higher amplifiers and durations than vanilla allows.
  • The Potion Charm — a carried item that keeps a potion effect topped up from a stored supply, so your Luck / Strength / Night Vision never lapses mid-run. Ideal for sustaining Luck while farming.

The Village module

  • New villager trades — additional and rebalanced trades, including ways to acquire enchanting and loot-relevant goods, deepening villager economies.

The Garden module

  • World & plant additions — where enabled, the Garden module adds flora/decor and minor world content; treat its specifics as pack-dependent and check JEI for what's present.
Charm your Luck

A Potion Charm loaded with Luck is arguably the single best quality-of-life item for an affix-loot grinder — it turns "remember to re-drink" into a permanent passive while you farm bosses and spawners.

09 — Putting it together

Progression & Synergy

Apotheosis has no quest line — your "progression" is a feedback loop: raise Luck, farm affix loot & gems, salvage the rejects, reroll & fuse toward Mythic/Ancient gear, and use that gear to farm faster. Here's the efficient path through that loop.

The power loop

  1. Stack Luck — Luck potions (via the module), a Potion Charm, Luck-granting affixes/gems, and a beacon. Luck multiplies every drop you're about to farm.
  2. Farm volume — a modified spawner farm and apothic bosses generate affix gear and gems en masse while your Luck is high.
  3. Salvage the chaff at the Salvaging Table into rarity materials and gem dust.
  4. Reroll & cut — spend materials to reroll affixes on good bases and gemcut/fuse gems toward higher rarity.
  5. Socket the keepers — slot your best fused gems into Mythic/Ancient gear with the sockets to hold them.
  6. Enchant on top — finish each piece with high-Eterna table enchants and treasure books drawn from the Enchantment Library.

Pairing with the rest of the pack

Pick a base, then perfect it

Don't chase a perfect random drop — find a base item you like at decent rarity, then make it perfect: reroll affixes, fuse and socket the right gems, and crown it with library enchantments. That deliberate path beats waiting on the Ancient lottery.

Power creep is real

Fully kitted Apotheosis gear can trivialise normal content — that's the point, but it can also unbalance a pack. If fights feel pointless, the pack likely intends you to seek out its boss mods next. Let the difficulty pull you forward.
