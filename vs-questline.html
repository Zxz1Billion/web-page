The Questline

Vintage Story ships no quest book, so here's one. Ten chapters take you from empty hands to steel, with the storms to survive along the way. Every quest tells you what to do, how to do it, and what it earns you. Tick the boxes as you play — they save here.

10 chapters ~30 quests Now with the how Browser-saved

★ — Read me first

How This Works

This is a companion checklist, not an in-game mod. It works like an FTB-Quests book: chapters unlock roughly in order, and each quest has tasks to finish.

  • Tick a task when you've done it in-game. The meter below counts every task on the page and saves to this browser, so you can leave and come back.
  • Each quest has a "How" line with the method and a link to the chapter that explains it in full — that's where the detail lives.
  • Chapters gate each other the way the tech does: copper before bronze, bronze before iron, iron before steel.

Chapter 1

Stone age · no prerequisite

Awakening

You start with nothing. Make the first tools, make fire, and live to see Day Two. Full walkthrough: The First Day.

Quest · Tools

🔪 First Edges

Knap your first knife and axe.

How Right-click loose stones (flint is best) and pick up sticks. Hold Shift and click flat ground to lay a knapping surface, left-click away the stone until the blade shape is left, then combine blade + stick in the crafting grid (E). Repeat for the axe. Knapping →

Reward You can now harvest grass, reeds, food and wood.

Quest · Survival

🔥 Light Against the Dark

Make fire and a place to hide before nightfall.

How Cut dry grass with the knife. Craft a Firestarter (sticks + dry grass), drop 4 firewood on the ground to form a fire pit, and hold right-click with the Firestarter until it lights (keep trying). Wall a small box around it. Fire →

Reward A safe camp and your first full night survived.

Quest · Food

🍖 First Forage

Eat — without poisoning yourself.

How Right-click berry bushes to pick them; cut cattails for roots; check unknown mushrooms in the Handbook (H) before eating. Throw a spear (hold and release right-click) or stab a hare, then put the raw meat in the fire pit to roast. Food →

Reward A full belly and your first taste of the nutrition system (The Larder).

Chapter 2

Stone age · requires Ch.1

Earth & Fire

No metal happens without pottery. Dig clay, shape it, and fire it into the crucible and molds the rest of the tech tree needs. How-to: The Four Crafts → Clay Forming.

Quest · Pottery

🏺 The Potter

Turn raw clay into fired vessels.

How Dig clay from grey patches near water. Hold the clay and right-click a surface to open the clay-forming grid; add voxels layer by layer to match the item. Stand the pieces on the ground, stack ~8 firewood over each to build a pit kiln, and light it (~10–20h to fire). Clay forming →

Reward Food storage, water-carrying, and a working pit kiln.

Quest · Metal prep

⚗️ The Crucible

Make the gear that handles molten metal.

How Clay-form a crucible and your molds from fire clay specifically (ordinary clay can't take the heat), then fire them in the pit kiln with the rest of your pottery. Clay forming →

Reward You can now smelt and cast metal — the door to the Copper Age.

Quest · Comfort

🍲 The First Pot

Cook meals, not just roasts.

How Fire a cooking pot, set it on a lit fire pit, and fill its four slots — the first two slots decide the meal type. Craft a bedroll (from cattail/flax fibre) and right-click it to sleep through the night. (Beds don't set respawn in vanilla — a temporal gear does.) Cooking →

Reward Better nutrition and safe nights away from base.

Chapter 3

Copper age · requires Ch.2

The Red Metal

Copper is the real start of the game — stone can't mine ore, so a cast copper pickaxe unlocks mining itself. Deep-dive: Fire & Metal and casting.

Quest · Prospect

🪙 First Strike

Find your first copper.

How Pick up copper nuggets lying on the surface — they mark a vein below. Look for green malachite or orange native copper in exposed stone and cliff faces. Prospecting →

Reward Enough copper to pour your first tools.

Quest · Cast

🌋 The First Pour

Smelt and cast copper.

How Fill the crucible with copper nuggets and heat it in a fire pit worked with a bellows until it passes 1084°C. Pick up the hot crucible and right-click a mold to pour; let it cool, then take the piece (20 nuggets = 1 ingot). Smelting →

Reward A pickaxe — you can finally mine ore veins.

Quest · Smith

🛠️ The Smith Awakens

Stand up your first metalworking station.

How Cast an anvil from copper (it takes ~900 units, so smelt plenty) and place it. Build a forge beside it to reheat metal to working temperature. The anvil →

Reward An anvil and forge — the base for all smithing to come.

Chapter 4

Bronze age · requires Ch.3

The Alloy Age

Copper alone won't reach iron. You need bronze twice over — a bronze pickaxe to mine iron ore, and a bronze anvil to work it. Recipes: Fire & Metal → Alloying.

Quest · Gather

⛏️ Gather the Alloys

Find what copper needs to become bronze.

How With your copper pick, mine cassiterite (tin) for tin bronze — it's scarce. If you can't find tin, gather zinc + bismuth (bismuth bronze) or gold + silver (black bronze) instead. Prospecting →

Reward The makings of whichever bronze your map offers.

Quest · Alloy

🔶 Bronze Casting

Mix and pour bronze.

How Put both metals in the crucible to an exact ratio (tin bronze = 88–92% copper, 8–12% tin); even 1% off fails the mix. Heat, then pour a bronze anvil and a bronze pickaxe + tools. Alloying →

Reward A bronze anvil and pick — the gate into iron.

Quest · Defend

🛡️ First Armor

Stop dying to wolves.

How Soak hides in a barrel of tannin (from bark) for a few days to make leather, then craft a leather or gambeson set. Or smith metal plates onto a leather jerkin for a brigandine set. Aim for tier-2 protection before facing packs. Armor →

Reward Real protection — see Warmth & Wear.

Chapter 5

Iron age · requires Ch.4

The Bloom

Iron isn't cast — it's smelted into a spongy bloom and hammered solid. First you need charcoal. Deep-dive: Fire & Metal → Bloomery.

Quest · Fuel

🪵 Charcoal Burner

Make the high-heat fuel iron needs.

How Stack firewood, bury it completely in soil, light it, and seal the last block within ~30 seconds so it smoulders instead of burning. Dig out the charcoal after ~18 hours. Charcoal →

Reward A charcoal stockpile — fuel for bloomery, forge and steel.

Quest · Smelt

🔩 The Bloomery

Pull iron out of the earth.

How Build a bloomery, load it with iron ore (bog-iron limonite is the easy first source) and a fuel that reaches ≥1200°C (charcoal). Fire it ~10h, break it open, and hammer the bloom on a bronze-or-better anvil to drive out slag into ingots. Bloomery →

Reward Wrought iron — a tier above bronze.

Quest · Equip

⚒️ Iron Outfit

Re-tool in iron.

How Reheat an iron ingot in the forge until it glows, set it on the anvil, and hammer to the Handbook blueprint (it cools fast — reheat as needed). Smith yourself an iron anvil too; you'll need it for steel. Smithing →

Reward An iron anvil — the prerequisite for steel.

Chapter 6

Steel age · requires Ch.5

The Crucible of Steel

The endgame metal. You carburize iron into blister steel in a cementation furnace, then hammer it into steel. Deep-dive: Fire & Metal → Steel.

Quest · Industry

🏭 Industrial Heat

Scale up your iron pipeline.

How Build a pulverizer (mechanical) to crush ore for extra yield, and run the bloomery hard for iron in bulk — both feed the steel process. (A blast furnace is a popular mod addition, not vanilla.) Bloomery →

Reward Faster iron and the crushing step steelmaking needs.

Quest · Carburize

🔥 Cementation

Make steel.

How Build a cementation furnace, pack up to 16 iron ingots surrounded by charcoal (the carbon source, mostly reusable) over a fuel bed, and keep it hot for ~6.67 in-game days → 16 blister steel. Hammer the blister steel on an iron anvil into steel ingots. Steel →

Reward Steel — the top tier of tools and armor.

Quest · Master

🗡️ Steel-Clad

Wear the top of the tech tree.

How Reheat steel in the forge and smith a full tool set plus plate (or scale) armor on the iron anvil. A helve hammer (Ch.7) makes the heavy hammering far quicker. Steel →

Reward The strongest gear in the game.

Chapter 7

Industry · requires Ch.3+ (best with Ch.5)

The Turning World

Stop grinding by hand — build a mechanical network and let wind or water do the work. Deep-dive: The Turning World.

Quest · Grind

🌾 First Motion

Mechanize a single chore.

How Build a quern and put grain in it (hand-crank, or hook it to power) to make flour; build a grindstone to sharpen and repair tools. Mechanical power →

Reward Flour (→ bread) and sharper tools.

Quest · Generate

🌬️ Harness Nature

Build a power source.

How Raise a windmill — sails on a rotor make ~20 kN per sail set, more up high — or a waterwheel on rapidly-flowing water. Carry the power along axles, turning corners with angled gears. Power budget →

Reward A turning network you can hang machines off.

Quest · Automate

🔨 The Helve Hammer

Automate the anvil.

How Connect a helve hammer to your network — it's heavy, needing ~2–3 sail sets to run. Place hot blooms or blister steel under it and let it hammer them for you. Power budget →

Reward Hands-free iron and steel working.

Chapter 8

Homestead · runs alongside Ch.2+

Hearth & Harvest

A fed, warm, well-stocked home is what carries you through winter and the late game. Deep-dives: The Larder, The Homestead, Warmth & Wear.

Quest · Farm

🌱 The Farmer

Grow your own food.

How Till grassy soil with a hoe, plant seeds, and keep them watered and warm. Rotate crops by the nutrient they drain (N → P → K) and top up with fertilizer: saltpeter (N), bone meal (P), potash (K), or compost (all). Farming →

Reward Renewable food across all five nutrition groups.

Quest · Preserve

🧊 The Cellar

Stop your food rotting.

How Wall a small, fully enclosed stone/cob room with a door and no skylight, kept cool — line it with sealed crocks and storage vessels. Salt meat in a barrel and dry fish on racks for the longest-lasting stores. The cellar →

Reward Food that lasts a whole winter.

Quest · Endure

🧥 Survive a Winter

Beat the season that kills new players.

How Before winter, make a warm fur and leather set for every clothing slot (aim ~+10 total warmth) and stockpile preserved food. Stay near a heat source, keep dry, and don't travel far in the cold. Surviving winter →

Reward Proof your homestead is self-sufficient year-round.

Quest · Husbandry

🐑 The Farmstead

Domesticate animals.

How Build a trough and feed a male + female pair to breed them. Offspring tame down over generations (pigs go fully passive by the 3rd). Then collect eggs, milk goats and sheep, and butcher for meat and hide. Husbandry →

Reward Renewable hides, milk and food — see The Bestiary.

Chapter 9

Temporal · escalates all game

Against the Storm

Reality here is unstable. Learn to manage your stability and weather the temporal storms. Deep-dive: The Rust & The Gear.

Quest · Stability

⚙️ The Gear

Get a temporal gear.

How Kill drifters (or buy from a trader) to get a temporal gear. Hold the gear in your offhand and a knife in your main hand, then use it to restore about +30% stability — it costs the gear and ~2 HP. Temporal →

Reward An emergency stability reset — and a translocator key.

Quest · Survive

🌀 Weather the Storm

Live through a temporal storm.

How When the "storm approaching" warning shows, get to a sealed, lit base with a one-wide chokepoint to fight from. Stay inside until it passes — during a storm, drifters spawn everywhere, even in daylight. Storms →

Reward Confidence that storms can't wipe your run.

Quest · Delve

🕯️ Into the Deep

Mine deep without dying to it.

How Mining down lowers your stability gauge. Keep a temporal gear handy, watch the gauge, and head back up before it drops near 25% (where bowtorn start spawning) and well before 12% (where it damages you). Stability →

Reward Access to the deep ores — and the nerve to use it.

Chapter 10

Endgame · requires good gear

The Wayfarer

The world is full of ruins, traders, teleporters and a buried story. Go find it. Deep-dive: Ruins & Wayfarers.

Quest · Loot

🏚️ Ruins & Relics

Plunder the past.

How Seek out stone ruins on the surface and underground. Smash the cracked clay vessels inside for loot — including the rare seeds (like cabbage) you can't get any other way. Bring light; ruins draw drifters. Locations →

Reward Rare seeds (cabbage!), tools and gears.

Quest · Trade

💰 The Trader

Do business with a caravan.

How Find a trader tent/caravan on the surface. Sell your surplus for gears and coins, and buy what you can't make yourself — rare seeds, extra temporal gears, special goods. Stock restocks over time. Traders →

Reward A money economy and access to scarce goods.

Quest · Travel

🌌 The Translocator

Bend distance to your will.

How Find a broken translocator in a ruin and repair/power it with temporal gears. Step through to see where its partner drops you, then build a safe outpost at both ends. Translocators →

Reward Fast travel across the map — and a thread of the story.

Quest · Conquer

🗝️ The Dynamic Dungeon

Clear 1.22's marquee challenge.

How The dynamic dungeon generates in fresh terrain, so travel to unexplored land to find one. Go in with full steel armor, plenty of light and food, and clear it room by room. Dungeon →

Reward Endgame loot and bragging rights. The Almanac is complete.
