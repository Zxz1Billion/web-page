Ground rules

Everything here was filtered so it's an easy call for you.

No world reset · 1.19.2 Forge only · no broken tech spine · performance-aware

Every mod is confirmed for 1.19.2 Forge and safe to bolt onto the live save. Mods that add structures/mobs/ores/crops are tagged new chunks — that content appears only in newly-generated chunks, which needs no regen; already-explored terrain is untouched. Anything that would actually require a fresh world (e.g. Tectonic, Terralith-style terrain seams at scale) is excluded and explained at the bottom. Library dependencies (GeckoLib, Citadel, Placebo, Balm, TerraBlender, Curios, etc.) are noted per mod; CurseForge pulls most automatically. Always confirm the exact 1.19.2 Forge file before installing, and test on a backup.

Storage & taming AE2

Straight talk on AE2

AE2's stock progression is a grind — meteor hunting for presses, manual inscriber spam, slow crystal growth, and storage that stays clunky until hours in. The mods below either cut that busywork or give players a less painful storage path. You don't need all of them; the AE2 add-ons alone fix most of the tedium.

Compatibility, libraries & exclusions

Excluded — would need a fresh world

Tectonic (overhauls terrain shape → severe cliffs/seams at the old/new chunk border) and Tinkers' Reforged (adds ores only reachable in new chunks) are left out under the no-reset rule. Applied Flux and Bountiful have no 1.19.2 Forge build at all.

Pick-one decisions

• Animated combat: Better Combat OR Epic Fight, never both. • Biome mod: BYG or Biomes O' Plenty (running both dilutes biome distribution). • Food mega-pack: Croptopia or Pam's HarvestCraft 2. • Base storage: pick a primary among Drawers / Functional Storage / Sophisticated Storage. • Mapping: JourneyMap (installed) vs Xaero's — let players choose, don't mandate both.

Tech-spine cautions

The pack runs AE2 + IC2 Classic. Mekanism has historical IC2-crash reports — test on a backup. Multiple ore-adding tech mods (IE/Thermal/Mekanism) overlap, but the pack's Almost Unified dedupes tagged ores/ingots. Create is the biggest Distant Horizons performance risk.

Shared libraries you'll pull in

GeckoLib (Iron's Spells, Forbidden & Arcanus, Occultism, Cataclysm, Mowzie's, Bosses of Mass Destruction, Naturalist, Creatures and Beasts), Citadel (Alex's Mobs), Placebo (Apotheosis, FastWorkbench), TerraBlender (BYG/BOP), Balm (Waystones, TrashSlot), Curios (installed), McJtyLib (XNet, Modular Routers), Titanium (Industrial Foregoing, Functional Storage), CoFH Core (Thermal), FTB Library (FTB Chunks/Quests), Architectury (FTB). CurseForge resolves most automatically — keep lib versions matched to each mod's 1.19.2 build.

Before installing

Confirm each mod's exact 1.19.2 Forge file (a few above are marked "verify ver"), test on a backup of the live save, and remember: every pick here is regen-safe — worldgen mods simply populate new chunks as players explore outward.
