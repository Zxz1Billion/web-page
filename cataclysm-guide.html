The Cataclysm
Bestiary

There are doors in this world that should have stayed shut. Behind each one waits a thing that was bound, not buried — a citadel-king of molten bronze, a serpent coiled in a drowned city, a guardian throned at the world's edge. L_Ender's Cataclysm is endgame combat content: a handful of monstrous structure-bosses scattered across the dimensions, each guarding gear worth the scars. This manual marks the lairs, names the wardens, and describes how they fight. Stat numbers are deliberately left to the in-game guide — what you'll find here is how to survive them.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Cataclysm is not a progression ladder you climb for the early game — it is a gauntlet of optional, brutal boss fights you walk into after you are already strong. Treat every entry in this book as a tombstone-in-waiting until you have the gear, the consumables, and ideally the company to face it.

The mod adds challenging structure-bosses spread across the Overworld, the Nether and the End, each lairing in its own purpose-built dungeon and each guarding powerful, often unique gear. It leans on two libraries to do its work, and you must install them or nothing spawns:

dependency

Lionfish API

L_Ender's own shared library. Cataclysm will not load without it — it carries the common code the bosses are built on.

dependency

GeckoLib

The animation runtime that drives the bosses' elaborate, telegraphed movesets. The wind-up animations are your dodge cues.

posture

This is endgame

These fights are tuned to test maxed-out gear. Come back when you can already shrug off a Wither — not before.

Gear up with the rest of the pack first

"To the Moon 2.0" hands you many roads to power before you ever knock on a Cataclysm door. Walk at least one of them to the end first:

  • Tinkers' Construct — a forged-and-modified weapon with the right traits and modifiers carries many of these fights.
  • Mekanism MekaSuit — modular armour with damage absorption, flight and life-support trivialises the arena-positioning problem.
  • Apotheosis-affixed gear — rolled and socketed weapons/armour add the raw damage and defensive affixes that turn a loss into a win.
  • Iron's Spells 'n Spellbooks — ranged and healing magic lets you fight from outside a boss's reach and patch yourself between attacks.
Come prepared to die

These bosses hit like freight trains and many have phases that escalate. Bring stacked healing & resistance potions, a death-insurance plan (Corail Tombstone graves so a wipe doesn't scatter your kit across an arena you can't re-enter safely), totems if your pack provides them, and — wherever possible — friends. Solo runs are the hardest version of every fight in this book.

On accuracy — read this once

Exact health pools, damage values, spawn conditions, structure rarities and crafting recipes vary by mod version and pack config and are easy to get wrong. This guide describes mechanics qualitatively on purpose. When you need a precise number or recipe, trust the in-game guide book, the official wiki, and JEI over anything written here.

How to read a lair

Each structure is built around an arena — open floor, ringed walls, often a hazard (lava, water, drop-offs). The structure spawns the lesser mobs of its theme; the boss is summoned or awakened at the arena's heart. Clearing the trash first, then triggering the boss with healing and escape routes ready, is the universal opening move.

Names you can trust vs. names to confirm

The Ender Guardian, Netherite Monstrosity, Ignis, The Harbinger, Wadjet and The Ancient Remnant are the load-bearing, well-documented bosses. Listings flagged verify locally (Maledictus' exact arena, the Leviathan/Scylla deep-ocean particulars) should be confirmed against your installed version before you go hunting — the family is right even where a detail drifts.

02 — The parched dominion

The Overworld & the Desert

The sand keeps its dead poorly. Across the desert biomes Cataclysm raises tomb-structures whose guardians wake when you breach them — and the road there is patrolled by creatures of its own.

The Cursed Pyramid & Wadjet

The Cursed Pyramid is a desert dungeon crowned by a serpent-warden commonly known as Wadjet (the Maxilla). The structure funnels you inward through trapped, mob-laden halls toward a sealed chamber; awaken the warden and the room becomes the arena. Expect a melee-plus-projectile fighter that punishes standing still — it favours lunges and area-denial that reward circling and ranged pressure. The Pyramid's signature spoils feed the Cursed weapon family, including the Cursed Bow line.

The Sandstorm & The Maledictus

The desert also hosts The Maledictus, a sand-and-curse themed boss tied to a desert arena sometimes called the Sandstorm confirm in-game. Treat it as a high-mobility melee threat with sand/visibility hazards; keep open ground at your back and don't let the trash mobs sandwich you while you focus the boss.

The desert & abyssal mob roster

Cataclysm seeds its biomes with themed lesser mobs — useful for materials and dangerous in numbers:

overworld

Amethyst Crab

A skittering crystal-shelled mob; its drops feed cosmetic/utility crafts. More nuisance than threat alone, deadly in packs near a fight.

aquatic

Coralssus

A hulking coral brute of the warmer waters — a heavy hitter you'll meet on the way to the ocean bosses. Bait its swings, then punish.

abyssal

Deeplings

The Sunken City's drowned cult-mobs. They swarm, they hit hard underwater, and they soften you up before the Harbinger ever appears.

loot

Themed drops

Lesser mobs drop materials and trophies — check JEI for what each feeds before you sweep an arena clean.

Loot the lair, not just the boss

Cataclysm structures hide chests and crafting components alongside the boss reward. Sweep the Cursed Pyramid for its loot on the way in; you may find the materials that upgrade the very weapon the boss drops.

03 — The drowned dominion

The Drowned Deep — the Sunken City

Beneath the waves lies the Sunken City, a ruined metropolis claimed by the sea and its cult. It is the single most punishing environment in the book: the fight is hard, and the ocean is trying to kill you the whole time.

The Harbinger

The Harbinger is the Sunken City's warden — a towering aquatic boss attended by Deeplings. Expect heavy melee, summoned adds, and ranged or shockwave-style attacks that close the distance for it. Its defeat is the gateway to Black Steel and the abyssal gear tied to the city.

The Leviathan & The Scylla

Deeper still swim larger horrors — the serpentine Leviathan and the multi-limbed Scylla confirm details in-game. These are large-bodied ocean bosses; the same survival rules apply, magnified. Their exact arenas and triggers are the kind of detail that shifts between versions, so verify before you dive.

Surviving the water itself

The ocean is the third combatant

Underwater you mine slower, swing slower, sink, and drown. Before any ocean boss, secure: Water Breathing (potion, Respiration/Aqua Affinity armour, or a Mekanism scuba setup), vertical mobility (Depth Strider, Dolphin's Grace, a Riptide trident or jet boots), and night-vision so you can read the boss's telegraphs in the murk. Consider draining or air-pocketing a fight chamber if your kit lets you turn it into a dry arena.

Bring ranged pressure

Melee in deep water is a trap. Spells, tridents, and bows let you damage the boss while keeping your mobility — fight from the edge of its reach and retreat upward to breathe and heal.

04 — The burning dominion

The Burning Citadel — Netherite Monstrosity & Ignis

The Nether holds Cataclysm's forge-kings. Their arena is a ruined, fire-wreathed citadel where the floor is as likely to kill you as the boss — these are fights about positioning over fire as much as trading blows.

Netherite Monstrosity

The Netherite Monstrosity is a colossal brute of fused netherite — a slow, earth-shaking melee titan that fights with ground slams and shockwaves rather than finesse. Its attacks are heavily telegraphed (read the GeckoLib wind-ups) but devastating if they land, and many ripple outward across the arena floor, so jumping or breaking line-of-floor-contact at the right beat matters. It is associated with Monstrous-tier boss weapons and netherite-grade spoils.

Ignis

Ignis is a fiery knight-boss — faster and more aggressive than the Monstrosity, trading in fire pillars, ranged flame, and aggressive closing attacks. Defeating Ignis is the path to Ignitium, the mod's fire-tier ingot, which crafts the Ignitium armour & tools set (see §06).

Fire resistance is mandatory, not optional

Both fights take place over lava and amid flame, and several attacks set you alight. Stack Fire Resistance (potions, beacon, or Fire Protection armour) before you enter — without it the environment alone will end the run before the boss does. Bridge yourself a solid, lava-free platform to fight on so you control the footing.

Range the big one, dodge the fast one

Against the slow Monstrosity, kite at range and punish recovery frames after each slam. Against the faster Ignis, manage your spacing tightly and use cover/pillars to break its ranged fire while you close for melee windows.

05 — The dominion at the edge

The Ender Throne — The Ender Guardian

Past the central island, out across the End-stone, sits an arena and its sentinel. The Ender Guardian is widely regarded as one of Cataclysm's flagship fights and a fitting capstone to the End.

The fight

The Ender Guardian is a teleport-heavy duellist: it blinks around the arena, closes the gap unexpectedly, and chains aggressive melee combos with phase-shifting movement. The challenge is less about raw tanking and more about not losing track of it — every blink resets your spacing, and standing flat-footed waiting for it to reappear is how you get deleted. It is associated with End-tier rewards, including a powerful boss weapon / gauntlet-class drop.

Fighting a teleporter

Keep the camera moving and the feet moving. Pre-place light and clear the arena floor so adds and Endermen don't compound the chaos. Ranged options and movement abilities shine here — anything that lets you re-acquire and re-engage the moment it blinks back in. Don't over-commit to a combo you can't cancel; the Guardian will teleport out and punish your recovery.

Mind the ledge

The End is a void. Many Cataclysm End arenas have edges, and knockback over a drop is an instant, gear-on-the-ground death. Fight toward the centre and keep a feather-fall / slow-fall or ender-pearl bailout ready.

Forge order matters

Plan a route: an early boss whose drops let you craft gear that makes the next boss survivable. A common path is securing fire-tier (Ignitium) and an abyssal/Black Steel kit before attempting the hardest teleporting and super-boss fights, all assembled at the Infernal Forge.

07 — Beyond the bestiary

The Ancient Remnant & Superbosses

Some fights in Cataclysm are not gates on a path — they are dares. The Ancient Remnant is the mod's optional super-boss: a hidden, summoned challenge tuned above the standard wardens, fought for bragging rights and a unique reward rather than necessity.

Super-boss fights of this class share a profile you should expect:

  • Higher everything — health, damage and attack density all climb past the standard bosses. Margin for error shrinks to almost nothing.
  • Multi-phase escalation — expect the fight to change as it drops, adding new attacks or hazards rather than simply getting weaker.
  • A summon, not a spawn — these are usually triggered deliberately with a crafted/found item at a set place, so you fight on your terms — bring your full kit. confirm the exact summon in-game
  • A unique prize — the reward is something you cannot get any other way; it is the whole reason to take the dare.
Do this one last, and do it prepared

Attempt the Ancient Remnant only after you've cleared the standard wardens and crafted their gear. Stack every consumable you have, secure your death-recovery (graves/totems), bring allies, and pick a controlled arena. This is the fight that punishes "good enough" — exact stats and the summon ritual vary by version, so confirm them in-game before you commit.

08 — The survivor's kit

Strategy & Loadout

Every Cataclysm fight rewards the same fundamentals: out-heal the chip damage, out-position the big hits, and never enter a fight you can't leave. Here is the universal boss-hunter's kit, drawn from across the pack.

How to fight anything in this book

  1. Clear the lair first. Sweep the structure's trash mobs and grab its chest loot before you wake the boss — you don't want adds on you mid-fight.
  2. Build your arena. Floor over hazards, place light, and mark an escape route. You control the ground or the ground controls you.
  3. Buff before the trigger. Potions, resistance and your survival insurance go up before you summon/awaken the boss — never during.
  4. Read the wind-up. GeckoLib gives every attack a telegraph. Learn the tells and dodge the slam, the pillar, the lunge — don't trade into them.
  5. Punish recovery, not aggression. Strike in the gaps after a big attack, then disengage. Greed gets you hit by the follow-up.
  6. Sustain and reset. Retreat to heal and re-buff between phases. A patient hunter wins; an impatient one feeds the arena.

Where this slots into the pack's late game

Cataclysm is a combat capstone, not a tech one. Once your Mekanism, Tinkers, Apotheosis and Iron's Spells lines have given you a strong, self-sustaining kit, the wardens are the fights worth that kit — and their unique gear (gauntlets, Ignitium, Black Steel, Monstrous weapons) feeds back into your loadout for the next dominion. Hunt them in roughly ascending difficulty, forge their spoils at the Infernal Forge, and save the Ancient Remnant for when nothing else in the world can scare you.

One last word from the bestiary

Nothing in this book is a substitute for the in-game guide, the wiki and JEI when you need an exact number, recipe, or spawn rule — those drift between versions and configs. What survives every version is the discipline: prepare, position, punish, persist. Bring friends. Bring graves. Come home.
