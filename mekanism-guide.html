The Osmium
Codex

A working field manual for Mekanism — the tech mod that turns one block of ore into five ingots, then keeps going until you are electrolysing water, boiling sodium with a fission reactor, and firing a laser into a fusion core to print antimatter. This guide runs from your first Enrichment Chamber to a fully powered MekaSuit, with interactive, layer-by-layer schematics for every major multiblock.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Mekanism is a self-contained tech progression: power generation, a tiered ore-processing chain, a deep chemical system, logistics, and an endgame of reactors and powered armour. It is modular — most installs bundle the four official pieces below — and almost every number is configurable, so treat the values here as the defaults and let the in-game tooltips and JEI/EMI win when your pack disagrees.

core

Mekanism

Machines, the ore-processing chain, chemicals, logistics, QIO, and the multiblocks.

Mekanism Generators

Heat, Solar, Wind, Bio & Gas generators, plus the Fission and Fusion reactors and the Industrial Turbine.

gear

Mekanism Tools

Paxels and a full tool/armour set in every Mekanism metal (Bronze, Steel, Osmium, Refined Obsidian/Glowstone…).

qol

Mekanism Additions

MekaSuit add-ons, the Electric Bow, Balloons, Stairs/Slabs/Walls for the decorative blocks, and more.

The three numbers to learn first

Mekanism measures power in Joules (J), stores fluids in millibuckets (mB), and stores gases/chemicals also in mB. Energy interops with Forge Energy (FE) at a configurable rate (≈ 2.5 J per 1 FE by default); you can even flip the display to read FE in the config if you prefer.

The shape of the climb

  1. Get power. A Heat Generator next to lava (or burning fuel) is your bootstrap. Run a Universal Cable to your first machines.
  2. Double your ore. An Enrichment Chamber turns ore into 2 dust → 2 ingots in an Energized Smelter. That's the 2× tier; everything after deepens it.
  3. Make chemicals. An Electrolytic Separator splits water into Hydrogen + Oxygen and unlocks 3× and 4× processing.
  4. Go full 5×. The Dissolution → Washer → Crystallizer → Injection → Purification → Crusher → Enrichment → Smelt chain turns 1 ore into 5 ingots.
  5. Scale & automate. Factories, Logistical Transporters, the Digital Miner, and eventually QIO handle the volume.
  6. Go nuclear. A Fission Reactor → Boiler → Turbine loop is the endgame power spine; Fusion and the SPS push into antimatter and the MekaSuit.

01 — The raw stock

Resources & Ores

Mekanism adds its own ores and pulls vanilla ones into its processing chain. Its signature metal is Osmium — common, found high in the stone column, and the binder in most early recipes (it makes the all-important Osmium Compressor outputs and the casings/alloys you'll lean on).

Already-explored chunks

On an existing world, Mekanism ores only generate in newly-loaded chunks — no world reset needed, but you'll want to wander into fresh terrain (or deeper) to find Osmium, Fluorite, and Uranium. Salt appears as shallow surface basins; bring an Electric Pump or just dig the salt blocks.

Key crafted materials

  • Steel — Iron infused with Carbon (Compressed Charcoal) in a Metallurgic Infuser. The structural backbone of most machine frames.
  • Bronze — Copper + Tin via the Infuser or crafting; early tools/casings.
  • Osmium / Steel / Bronze / Glowstone / Obsidian alloys — Infused, Reinforced, and Atomic Alloy gate the Basic → Advanced → Elite → Ultimate tier ladder.
  • Refined Obsidian & Refined Glowstone — made in the Osmium Compressor (ingot + liquid Osmium); the premium tier and the Atomic Alloy ingredient.
  • HDPE / Polyethylene — plastic sheeting from the Pressurized Reaction Chamber; the MekaSuit's backbone material.
  • Control Circuits — Basic → Elite circuits (Osmium + Redstone, infused up the tiers) gate almost every machine recipe.

02 — The grid

Energy & Joules

Everything Mekanism runs on Joules. Machines store a buffer and draw from cables; generators fill the grid. Mekanism speaks Forge Energy too, so it slots into any FE setup — the wall is just the conversion ratio (≈ 2.5 J : 1 FE, configurable).

Cables & storage

Universal Cables carry Joules and buffer a little energy in-transit. Like every Mekanism transmitter they come in four tiers (Basic → Ultimate) with steeply rising throughput. Store power in Energy Cubes (portable, tiered) or, at scale, the Induction Matrix multiblock.

Build the Induction Matrix split right

In an Induction Matrix, Cells decide how much you can hold and Providers decide how fast it goes in/out. A reactor pushing huge bursts needs Providers; a buffer that just has to survive the night needs Cells. Most builds want both, sized to the load.

03 — The whole point

Ore Processing — 1× to 5×

This is why people install Mekanism. Each tier adds machines and chemicals to squeeze more ingots out of every ore block. You climb it in order; each tier reuses the machines below it.

The five processing tiers
TierYieldMachines addedChemical needed
1× Smelting1 ingotEnergized Smelter—
2× Enriching2 ingots+ Enrichment Chamber—
3× Purifying3 ingots+ Purification Chamber, CrusherOxygen
4× Injecting4 ingots+ Chemical Injection ChamberHydrogen Chloride
5× Dissolving5 ingots+ Dissolution, Washer, CrystallizerSulfuric Acid (+ water, HCl, O₂)

The full 5× chain

At 5×, a single ore block walks through eight machines. The intermediates rename per metal (Iron Crystal, Iron Shard, Iron Clump…), but the path is always the same:

  • Ore
  • Dissolution + H₂SO₄
  • Dirty Slurry
  • Washer + H₂O
  • Clean Slurry
  • Crystallizer
  • Crystal
  • Injection + HCl
  • Shard
  • Purification + O₂
  • Clump
  • Crusher
  • Dirty Dust
  • Enrichment
  • Dust
  • Smelter
  • Ingot ×5
Sane build order

Don't jump to 5× on day one. Run 2× (Enrichment + Smelter) until you have spare power and an Electrolytic Separator, then add 3× (Oxygen). Stand up 4× once you're making Hydrogen Chloride reliably, and only commit to 5× when you have the sulfuric-acid pipeline and enough Joules to feed eight machines per ore. Each tier above 2× also needs its chemical supplied continuously — build the gas plant before the ore plant.

Throughput, not just ratio

5× is the best ratio but the slowest per-ore unless you parallelise. The fix is Factories (§05): one Ultimate factory does the work of nine machines in a single block, and Speed Upgrades push each step. For raw stone & gravel, the Crusher and Enrichment Chamber also handle ordinary recipes (cobble→sand, flint, biofuel, enriched redstone/diamond/etc.).

05 — Scaling up

Factories & Upgrades

A Factory is a multi-slot version of a single machine — same recipe, many parallel inputs in one block. They come in three tiers and are built by combining the right Factory with a Tier Installer (or crafted directly).

Factory tiers & types
TierParallel slots
Basic Factory3
Advanced Factory5
Elite Factory7
Ultimate Factory9

Each factory is set to one type: Smelting, Enriching, Crushing, Compressing, Combining, Purifying, Injecting, Infusing, or Sawing. One Ultimate Purifying Factory + one Ultimate Injecting Factory + one Ultimate Smelting Factory will out-process a whole room of single machines.

Upgrades

Drop these into a machine's (or factory's) upgrade slot — they stack up to a cap per machine.

Machine upgrades
UpgradeEffect
Speed UpgradeFaster processing — at a steep energy cost. Pair with Energy Upgrades.
Energy UpgradeLower energy use & a bigger internal buffer.

Speed + Energy together

Speed Upgrades roughly square energy draw as they cut time. The standard trick is to run max Speed with several Energy Upgrades and feed the machine from a strong grid — you get the throughput without the buffer constantly emptying.

Chemicals & Gases

Mekanism's chemical system is what separates it from other tech mods. Gases, slurries, infusion types, and pigments all flow through Pressurized Tubes and are stored in Chemical Tanks. Here's how to make the ones that matter.

Sulfur & salt are the bottlenecks

3× and 4× are easy once you electrolyse water. 5× lives or dies on Sulfuric Acid, which needs Sulfur (often from ore-processing byproducts or a renewable source) and a two-step infusion chain. Stand up a dedicated Sulfuric Acid plant with buffer Chemical Tanks before you convert your ore line to 5×, or it will stall the instant the acid runs dry.

Infusion types & pigments

The Metallurgic Infuser uses solid infusion types rather than gases: Carbon (charcoal/coal), Redstone, Diamond, Tin, Fungi, Bio, Gold, and Refined Obsidian. Mekanism also has a pigment system (Pigment Extractor/Mixer + Painting Machine) for colouring blocks and dye production — niche, but handy for mass dye.

07 — Moving it around

Transmitters

Mekanism's five transmitter families each carry one thing, all tiered Basic → Ultimate, all configurable, and all able to form one shared network when connected. They're cheap, fast, and the reason a Mekanism base feels tidy.

The five transmitter networks
TransmitterCarriesTiers do
Universal CableEnergy (Joules / FE)Higher tier = more J/t throughput & in-line buffer.
Mechanical PipeFluids (mB)Higher tier = more mB/t & buffer.
Pressurized TubeGases / chemicals / slurries (mB)Higher tier = more mB/t & buffer.
Logistical TransporterItemsHigher tier = faster item travel & stack size in transit; supports filters & round-robin.
Thermodynamic ConductorHeatMoves heat between heat-producing/consuming blocks (boiler loops).

Logistical Transporters are the smart one: pull items out of a chest, route them by colour/filter to the right machine, and round-robin across destinations. Two variants tune routing:

  • Restrictive Transporter — only used as a last resort path (build dead-end overflow routes).
  • Diversion Transporter — toggled by redstone to open/close a path.
  • Logistical Sorter — the brain on a chest face: define filters (by item, tag, material, mod) and colour-code outputs.
Colour is your friend

Use the Configurator to colour transporter strands and sorter filters. Matching colours only deliver to matching-colour destinations, so one trunk line can serve a dozen machines without cross-talk.

09 — Reaching out

Mining & Teleports

Digital Miner

The Digital Miner is automated quarrying done right: set a radius and height range, add filters (mine only these ores/blocks, by item or tag), and it teleports matching blocks straight into its inventory — optionally replacing them with a chosen block (Stone Generator Upgrade) so you don't leave craters. Feed it power, pipe out the loot, and it runs a whole ore vein dry without a dropped quarry frame.

Power & chunk-loading

The Miner's reach and silk-touch options cost energy fast. Add an Anchor Upgrade (or a separate chunk-loader) if it's mining far from where you stand, or it'll pause when the chunk unloads.

Teleporter

The Teleporter is a frame-and-block multiblock-ish pad: build the frame, power it, and tune two or more Teleporters to the same frequency to step between them. It draws energy per jump scaled by distance and works across dimensions.

Quantum Entangloporter

The Quantum Entangloporter is wireless transfer of everything — items, fluids, gases, energy, even heat — between any two Entangloporters on the same frequency, across any distance or dimension. One per buffer type per frequency. It's the cleanest way to pull reactor output home or feed a remote miner.

10 — First multiblocks

Dynamic Tank & Thermal Evaporation

Mekanism's multiblocks are dynamic: you decide the size within limits, and the structure forms the moment the shell is sealed. The two gentlest are bulk storage and the evaporation tower.

Dynamic Tank

A hollow cube of Structural Casing (optionally windowed with Structural Glass) with one or more Dynamic Valves for I/O. The interior air volume is your capacity — it stores fluids or chemicals or slurries. Any cuboid from 3×3×3 up to 18×18×18. Step through the layers:

Thermal Evaporation Plant

A 4×4 tower, 3–18 blocks tall, that concentrates fluids using heat — most importantly Water → Brine → Lithium, the gateway to fusion fuel. Taller is faster, and stacking Solar Panels on the structure feeds it the heat it craves. Keep the top open to the sky.

11 — Steam power

Boiler & Turbine

The classic Mekanism power spine converts heat → steam → Joules. A Thermoelectric Boiler boils water into steam; an Industrial Turbine spins that steam into energy. Heat the boiler electrically (Resistive Heater), by burning fuel (Fuelwood Heater), or — at endgame — with the sodium loop off a Fission Reactor.

Thermoelectric Boiler

A casing shell split internally by a mandatory full layer of Pressure Dispersers: water and Superheating Elements below, the steam cavity above. More heating elements and more water volume = more steam per tick.

Industrial Turbine

A taller casing tank with a central rotor shaft (Rotors + Blades), a Rotational Complex ringed by Electromagnetic Coils, a Pressure Disperser layer, a Saturating Condenser bank, and Turbine Vents on the upper walls. Steam in the bottom, Joules out the energy port. Balance blades (steam consumed), coils (energy produced), and vents/condensers (max flow) for full output.

Sizing the pair

A turbine's max steam flow must be ≥ your boiler's steam output, or you waste boil rate. Mekanism shows both numbers in the multiblock GUIs — match the turbine's max flow to the boiler's boil capacity, then add condensers so spent steam recycles to water instead of venting away.

12 — Going critical

Fission Reactor

The Fission Reactor is the endgame heat source — and the one that can kill your base. It burns Fissile Fuel, produces enormous heat (drive a Boiler+Turbine with it) plus Nuclear Waste, and will melt down if you let it overheat or run dry of coolant.

The fuel chain

  • Uranium
  • Yellowcake
  • Injection + HF
  • Uranium Hexafluoride
  • Isotopic Centrifuge
  • Fissile Fuel

Feed Fissile Fuel and a coolant (water → steam directly, or sodium for a hotter loop) into the reactor's ports; pull heated coolant/steam to your boiler+turbine, and pull Nuclear Waste off for further processing.

The structure

A Structural Casing cube (3×3×4 up to 18³) with Reactor Glass windows, packed inside with Fission Fuel Assembly columns each capped by a Control Rod Assembly, served by Reactor Ports. Step through it:

Meltdown discipline — read this twice

Set the burn rate conservatively and watch the temperature and coolant bars. If heat climbs toward ~1,200 K the reactor takes damage and starts leaking radiation; a full meltdown irradiates a large area. Wire a Reactor Logic Adapter to SCRAM on low coolant or high temp, keep a Geiger Counter and Dosimeter on you, and build it walled-off / underground. Radiation is cleared slowly over time or with a Radioactive Waste Barrel + Hazmat/MekaSuit shielding — prevention beats cleanup.

13 — The summit

Fusion, SPS & Antimatter

The true endgame. The Fusion Reactor fuses Deuterium and Tritium for power that dwarfs fission; the SPS turns reactor leftovers into antimatter for the very best gear.

Fusion fuel

  • Water
  • Heavy Water (pump + filter)
  • Electrolysis
  • Deuterium
  • Brine → Lithium (evap, §10)
  • Solar Neutron Activator
  • Tritium

Combine Deuterium + Tritium in a Chemical Infuser into D-T Fuel, load a Hohlraum with it, drop the Hohlraum in the Reactor Controller, and fire a charged Laser (build up a Laser Amplifier) to ignite. Once lit and fed, it self-sustains and pours out heat/energy.

SPS — Supercritical Phase Shifter & antimatter

The SPS is a large multiblock that consumes Polonium (refined from Nuclear Waste via the Solar Neutron Activator) and colossal energy to produce Antimatter Pellets. Antimatter feeds the Antiprotonic Nucleosynthesizer for the top-tier crafts — including the components that turn a basic MekaSuit into a flying, self-charging exosuit.

Energy budget

The SPS is one of the most power-hungry blocks in the modpack. Don't even approach it without a fusion reactor or a multi-turbine fission setup and a fat Induction Matrix buffering the spikes.

14 — Wearing the tech

MekaSuit & Meka-Tool

Everything points here. The Meka-Tool is one tool that becomes every tool; the MekaSuit is armour that flies, feeds you, breathes for you, and shrugs off radiation. Both are built from HDPE + Atomic Alloy + a Modification Station, then customised with modules.

Building it

  1. Make the bases. Craft the Meka-Tool and the four MekaSuit pieces from HDPE, Atomic Alloy, and reinforced components. These are expensive — they assume a working ore & chemical plant.
  2. Build a Modification Station. This is where modules are installed into the gear (and removed/recovered).
  3. Craft modules (often via the Antiprotonic Nucleosynthesizer for the best ones) and install them. Many modules can be installed multiple times to stack their effect.
  4. Keep it charged. The suit runs on Joules — a Solar Recharging module trickles power, but a Chargepad or Energy Cube tops it up fast.

Save the Gravitational Modulating module (and the SPS that effectively funds it) for when power is genuinely a non-issue.

15 — The route

Progression — The Assembly Line

The reference above is the shelf; this is the route. Mekanism is wide, but the critical path is narrow: power → ore multiplication → chemistry → logistics → reactors → gear. Eight stages take you from a lava-fed Heat Generator to a flying, self-charging MekaSuit. Each stage stands on the one before it — don't skip ahead until a stage's goal is green.

  1. S0 · Bootstrap & 2×

    First power, first steel, and doubling every ore. The smallest setup that already pays for itself.

    UNLOCKS · Enrichment Chamber · Energized Smelter · Metallurgic Infuser · Heat Generator

  2. S1 · Chemistry I — 3× & 4×

    Electrolyse water for Oxygen & Hydrogen, brine for Chlorine; tier ore processing to 3× then 4×.

How to read a stage

Every stage below has a Goal (the milestone that means you're done), a Build checklist, the structures you raise during it (the interactive schematics live in §10–§13 above — each stage links to them), and the gate to the next stage. Recipe specifics live in §00–§14 and your in-game JEI/EMI.

Stage 00

Bootstrap & 2×

⚡ first power · double your ore
🎯Goal

An ore block in, two ingots out — powered, automatic, and not babysat. You should have steel and a small buffer of Joules to spare.

Build

  • Heat Generator — place it and ring it with lava source blocks for passive power (or feed it solid fuel). This is your bootstrap grid.
  • Universal Cable (Basic) — run a line from the generator to your machine wall.
  • Metallurgic Infuser — infuse Iron with Carbon (Compressed Charcoal) to make Steel; you'll need it for almost everything.
  • Enrichment Chamber — ore → 2 dust. The heart of 2× processing (see §03).
  • Energized Smelter — dust → ingot on Joules (faster, cheaper than a furnace).
  • Basic Energy Cube — a buffer so the grid survives a lull and a charger for your tools.
  • Configurator — set machine faces (input/output) and colour cables. Craft it early; you'll use it constantly.
Don't overthink power yet

A couple of lava-fed Heat Generators is plenty for a 2× line. Resist the urge to jump to solar/wind farms — Stage 4–5 power dwarfs anything you'd build now, so spend the iron on the ore line instead.

🚪Gate to S1

You have steady power, steel, and a working Enrichment → Smelter pair. Now make chemicals.

Stage 01

Chemistry I — 3× & 4×

🧪 electrolysis · triple & quadruple
🎯Goal

A continuous supply of Oxygen, Hydrogen, and Hydrogen Chloride, and an ore line upgraded to 4× (4 ingots per ore).

Build — the gas plant first

  • Electrolytic Separator on Water → Hydrogen + Oxygen. Pipe each gas into a Chemical Tank buffer with a Pressurized Tube.
  • Gather Salt (shallow surface basins) → make Brine; a second Electrolytic Separator on Brine yields Sodium + Chlorine.
  • Chemical Infuser — combine Hydrogen + Chlorine → Hydrogen Chloride (the 4× reagent).

Build — tier the ore line

  • Purification Chamber (ore + Oxygen → 3 clumps) + Crusher (clump → dirty dust) → feed your Enrichment Chamber. That's 3×.
  • Chemical Injection Chamber (ore/shard + Hydrogen Chloride → 4 shards) inserted ahead of the Purification step. That's 4×.
  • Buffer every gas with a Chemical Tank so a momentary shortfall doesn't stall the whole line.
Build the supply before the demand

Each tier above 2× consumes its chemical per ore, continuously. Size your electrolysis + buffers to comfortably outpace the ore line, or it'll choke the instant a tank empties.

🚪Gate to S2

4× ore is running and your gases never run dry. Time for the acid plant and 5×.

Stage 02

Full 5× & Factories

⚗️ sulfuric acid · slurry chain · scale
🎯Goal

1 ore → 5 ingots at volume: the full eight-machine chain, fed by a sulfuric-acid plant, parallelised with Factories.

Build — the sulfuric-acid plant

  • Sulfur (ore-processing byproduct or a renewable source) → Chemical Oxidizer → Sulfur Dioxide.
  • Chemical Infuser: SO₂ + Oxygen → Sulfur Trioxide; then SO₃ + Water → Sulfuric Acid.
  • Buffer the acid in a Chemical Tank — this is the single most stall-prone input in the whole base.

Build — the 5× slurry chain

  • Chemical Dissolution Chamber (ore + Sulfuric Acid → Dirty Slurry) → Chemical Washer (+ Water → Clean Slurry) → Chemical Crystallizer (→ Crystal).
  • Feed the Crystal into your existing Injection → Purification → Crusher → Enrichment → Smelter chain. Five ingots per ore.

Build — scale with Factories & storage

  • Replace single machines with Factories (Basic 3-slot → Ultimate 9-slot) via a Tier Installer (see §05). One Ultimate factory per chain step keeps up with a Digital Miner's output.
  • Drop Speed + Energy Upgrades into each factory; feed them from a strong cable run.
  • Raise a Dynamic Tank for bulk fluid/chemical storage, and a Thermal Evaporation Plant for Brine → Lithium (you'll need it at S6).
Structures

Build the interactive Dynamic Tank and Thermal Evaporation Plant from §10 above — step through their courses there.

🚪Gate to S3

5× ore at volume, with storage and lithium online. Now make it run itself.

Stage 03

Logistics & QIO

🔀 sorting · auto-mining · digital storage
🎯Goal

Raw materials flow in, products sort themselves, and you can search, withdraw, and craft your whole inventory from one terminal.

Build

  • Logistical Transporters + a Logistical Sorter on each source chest — filter by item/tag and colour-code routes so one trunk line serves the whole machine wall (see §07).
  • Restrictive / Diversion transporters for overflow and redstone-gated paths.
  • Digital Miner — set a radius, add ore filters, optionally a Stone Generator Upgrade to backfill. Pipe its output into your ore line (see §09).
  • QIO — a Drive Array + Drives for capacity, a Dashboard to search/craft, and Importers/Exporters to auto-stock (see §08).
  • Teleporter and/or Quantum Entangloporter — frequency-linked travel and wireless transfer from remote sites (e.g. that Digital Miner).
Colour is the whole trick

Use the Configurator to colour transporter strands and sorter filters. Matching colours only deliver to matching destinations — that's how a single line feeds a dozen machines with zero cross-talk.

🚪Gate to S4

The base runs hands-off and you have a searchable network. Now you need real power.

Stage 04

Steam Power

♨️ heat → steam → Joules
🎯Goal

A Boiler + Turbine pair producing far more power than your starter generators — enough to run a fully-upgraded base and prep for nuclear.

Build

  • Thermoelectric Boiler — casing shell split by a Pressure Disperser layer; Superheating Elements + water below, steam cavity above. Heat it with a Resistive Heater (electric) or fuel for now.
  • Industrial Turbine — central rotor shaft + Rotational Complex + Coils + Condensers + Vents. Steam in, Joules out.
  • Match the turbine's max steam flow to the boiler's boil rate; add Saturating Condensers so spent steam recycles to water.
  • Alternative / supplement: a Gas-Burning Generator burning Ethylene (Substrate + Bio Fuel + Hydrogen in a PRC) is a superb standalone power source.
Structures

Build the interactive Thermoelectric Boiler and Industrial Turbine from §11 above.

Why build this before fission?

A Fission Reactor doesn't make energy directly — it makes heat. You need a working Boiler + Turbine to turn that heat into Joules. Getting the steam loop right on an electric/fuel heater first means you're not also debugging the turbine when you light a reactor.

🚪Gate to S5

Your steam loop works on a tame heat source. Now swap in the dangerous one.

Stage 05

Fission

☢️ fissile fuel · meltdown discipline
🎯Goal

A Fission Reactor driving your Boiler + Turbine for enormous power — running stably, walled-off, with a SCRAM safety wired in and your waste being handled.

Build — the fuel chain

  • Mine Uranium → Yellowcake; Chemical Injection Chamber (Yellowcake + Hydrogen Fluoride) → Uranium Hexafluoride.
  • Isotopic Centrifuge: Uranium Hexafluoride → Fissile Fuel. (Hydrogen Fluoride comes from Fluorite.)

Build — the reactor & safety

  • Fission Reactor — casing cube with Reactor Glass, packed with Fuel Assembly columns capped by Control Rod Assemblies, served by Reactor Ports.
  • Cool it with water (direct steam) or a sodium loop into the boiler. Keep coolant supply ahead of burn rate.
  • Wire a Reactor Logic Adapter to SCRAM on high temp / low coolant. Carry a Geiger Counter + Dosimeter and wear radiation protection.
  • Induction Matrix (Cells = capacity, Providers = transfer rate) to buffer the turbine's output spikes.
  • Route Nuclear Waste into a Radioactive Waste Barrel or the S6 chains — never vent it.
Structure

Build the interactive Fission Reactor from §12 above.

It can melt down

Start with a low burn rate and watch the temperature/coolant bars. Past ~1,200 K the reactor damages itself and leaks radiation; a meltdown irradiates a wide area. Build it underground or behind thick walls, and never run it un-monitored until your SCRAM logic is proven.

🚪Gate to S6

Stable nuclear power and a waste pipeline. Now reach for fusion and antimatter.

Stage 06

Fusion & Antimatter

✦ D-T fuel · SPS · antimatter
🎯Goal

A self-sustaining Fusion Reactor for limitless power, and an SPS printing antimatter for the very best crafts.

Build — fusion fuel

  • Deuterium: pump Heavy Water (Electric Pump + Filter Upgrade) → electrolyse → Deuterium.
  • Tritium: Lithium (S2 evaporation) → Solar Neutron Activator → Tritium.
  • Chemical Infuser: Deuterium + Tritium → D-T Fuel; load a Hohlraum with it.

Build — ignite & print

  • Fusion Reactor (octahedral frame + glass + controller + ports + Laser Focus Matrix). Insert the fuelled Hohlraum, charge a Laser Amplifier, and fire to ignite. Once fed, it self-sustains.
  • SPS — feed it Polonium (refined from Nuclear Waste via the Solar Neutron Activator) and colossal energy → Antimatter Pellets.
  • Antiprotonic Nucleosynthesizer — antimatter + inputs → the top-tier components for S7.
Structure

Build the interactive Fusion Reactor from §13 above.

Power budget

The SPS is one of the most energy-hungry blocks in the pack. Don't approach it without fusion (or a multi-turbine fission array) and a fat Induction Matrix to soak the spikes.

🚪Gate to S7

Power is effectively unlimited and antimatter is flowing. Time to wear it.

Stage 07

The MekaSuit

🦾 the payoff
🎯Goal

A Meka-Tool that is every tool and a MekaSuit that flies, feeds you, and shrugs off radiation — modded to your playstyle.

Build

  • Make HDPE (Pressurized Reaction Chamber) and Atomic Alloy; craft the Meka-Tool and the four MekaSuit pieces (full module roster in §14).
  • Build a Modification Station — this is where modules are installed and recovered.
  • Craft modules (the best via the Antiprotonic Nucleosynthesizer) and install them. Start with Energy, Solar Recharging, Jetpack, Hydraulic Propulsion, Inhalation Purification, and Radiation Shielding.
  • For the Meka-Tool: Excavation Escalation, Vein Mining, Attack Amplification, and Teleportation first.
  • Save Gravitational Modulating (creative-style flight) for last — it's the most power-hungry module.
🏁Done

You've run the whole Assembly Line — from a lava-fed Heat Generator to a flying exosuit. For the pack-wide picture, see The Ascent and the Questline.
