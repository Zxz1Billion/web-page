The Descent

Somewhere beneath your feet, a staircase spirals down through floor after floor of brick, spawners and treasure. Dungeon Crawl drops a single sprawling, multi-level dungeon under the world — a proper "fight your way to the bottom" delve.

Multi-floor dungeon Procedural MC 1.19.2 · Forge

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Dungeon Crawl generates a large, procedural dungeon underground in most biomes: stacked floors linked by staircases, themed by the biome above, and getting nastier the deeper you go.

It's one of the headline dive sites in the Cartographer's Journal — a reliable, repeatable source of loot and XP between tech milestones.

01 — Getting in

Finding the Entrance

The dungeon generates underground, with a staircase running up toward the surface. To find one:

  • Explore caves and tunnels — you'll often break into the dungeon's brickwork or a staircase shaft.
  • Watch for the distinctive themed bricks (the palette matches the biome above).
  • If you've found the top staircase, follow it down — that's the intended route through every floor.
Use a map

A map mod helps you mark the entrance so you can return to a half-cleared dungeon. See the Operator's Handbook for JourneyMap/Map Atlases.

02 — The shape

Structure & Layout

The dungeon is built from multiple stacked floors connected by staircases. Each floor is a maze of themed rooms and corridors, with:

Themed rooms

Set-piece chambers — libraries, prisons, shrines — each with their own loot and dangers.

Spawner rooms

Mob spawners drive the difficulty; clustered rooms can overwhelm if you're careless.

Secret rooms

Hidden caches behind suspicious walls reward a careful eye (and a pick).

The deeper the floor, the harder it gets — treat each descent as a step up in difficulty and loot quality.

03 — The dangers

Hazards

  • Spawners pump out mobs continuously — disable or destroy them as you clear (a great use for Apotheosis spawner editing if you want to repurpose them).
  • Tight corridors funnel mobs onto you — fight in doorways and back into cleared rooms.
  • Scaling difficulty means the lower floors can spike past your gear; retreat and re-gear if needed.
  • Possible traps and ambush rooms — move deliberately, light as you go.

04 — The reward

Loot

Each floor holds chests, and the rule of thumb is simple: deeper = better. Expect resources, enchanted books, and mid-tier gear, with the richest caches near the bottom and in spawner/secret rooms. Combined with the steady mob kills, it's a strong XP and enchanting-material farm. Exact loot tables are config-driven verify in-game.

05 — The method

Clearing Strategy

  1. Gear up first. Bring mid-tier armour/weapons (Tinkers / Apotheosis), food (Farmer's Delight), and a grave net (Tombstone).
  2. Descend methodically. Clear and light each floor before taking the stairs down; mark the entrance.
  3. Kill the spawners as you go so a room stays cleared behind you.
  4. Loot on the way down, retreat to resupply, and push deeper when your gear can handle it.

06 — Field notes

Tips

  • Carry plenty of torches/blocks — lighting is your best crowd control.
  • A Comforts sleeping bag lets you skip the night without leaving the dungeon or moving spawn.
  • Use it as a renewable loot/XP run while you tech up elsewhere in the pack.

★ — The path

Progression — descending the floors

Treat the dungeon as a structured run rather than a single fight: gear up, secure an entrance, and peel away the floors from the top down, pushing deeper each trip as your kit improves.

  1. Gear up before you go. Bring mid-tier armour and a weapon, a stack of food, plenty of torches, and a grave net so a death deep underground isn't a total loss.
  2. Find an entrance and mark it. Break into the brickwork or follow a staircase shaft down to the top floor, then mark the entrance so you can return to a half-cleared dungeon — see the Exploration hub.
  3. Clear the top floors. Light and sweep each floor, destroying or relocating spawners as you go so cleared rooms stay quiet behind you (Apotheosis spawner editing lets you repurpose them).
  4. Descend for better loot. Take the stairs down as your gear allows — deeper floors hit harder but hold the richest caches.
  5. Retreat to resupply, then push the bottom. When food, torches or durability run low, head back up, restock, and return to drive toward the lowest floor.
  6. Run it again. Once you know a dungeon, keep it as a repeatable loot and XP run between tech milestones on The Ascent.
Pace yourself

You don't have to clear the whole dungeon in one trip. Push only as deep as your gear comfortably allows, retreat when stretched, and let the run grow with you.
