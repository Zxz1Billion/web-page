House Layouts

A gallery of 16 ground-floor plans to copy or remix — from a night-one cabin to a courtyard manor. Every shape is here: square cottages, L- and T-wings, a U-shaped courtyard, a cross-plan, an H-range and an atrium. Each is drawn top-down with labelled rooms, doors, windows and the cellar stair, so you can see the whole house before you place a block. Pick one, scale it to your spot, and build.

01 — 16 plans

The layouts

Sizes are in blocks; the front door is on the lower edge of each plan and the staircase runs down to a cold cellar. Mix and match — borrow a wing from one, a porch from another.

02 — Reading & building

Reading a plan

  • Walls are the thick lines; windows are the thin pale breaks in them; doors are the gaps with a quarter-circle showing the swing.
  • The staircase (the ruled box with an arrow) drops to a cold cellar — dig that out underground, wall it in stone, and keep it sealed with no skylight so food lasts.
  • Scale it to your site: the block sizes are a starting point — stretch a room a block or two to suit your ground and materials.
  • Vintage Story: set the kitchen fire on stone, a block clear of timber; a central hearth doubles as winter warmth; keep food in the cellar and tools/blocks in the ground-floor stores.
