House Floor Plans

A clean, top-down plan of a real two-bedroom house, level by level: a cellar cold store below, a ground floor where the entrance opens into the living room with the kitchen to the right, and a first floor with two bedrooms off a central landing. One staircase in the hall serves both the cellar and upstairs. Every room is labelled with its size; doors show their swing, windows sit in the walls, and the furniture marks how each room is used.

01 — Ground floor

Entrance, living room & kitchen

The front door opens straight into the living room — a hearth, seating and a table — with windows down the west wall. A door on the right leads to the kitchen (stove and counters along the back and side) and to the hall, which holds the staircase up and the steps down to the cellar. A back door opens off the hall.

02 — First floor

Two bedrooms off the landing

The stair lands on a central landing, with a door each side. Bedroom 1 sits over the living room — the larger room, bed and wardrobe, windows west and front. Bedroom 2 is over the kitchen, with windows to the back and east.

03 — Cellar

The cold store

Dug under the hall and reached by the same staircase, the cellar is a single stone-walled cold store. Line the walls with shelving for storage vessels and sealed crocks, and keep barrels for bulk. Cool, dark and fully enclosed keeps food longest — no skylight.

04 — From plan to blocks

Building it

  • Set out the ground floor first at 12×9, walls two-to-three blocks high. Mark the two interior partitions (living/kitchen and the hall) before you raise them so the doorways line up.
  • Dig the cellar down under the hall, 8×7, walled in stone, before you floor the ground level over it. Put the staircase in the hall so one run reaches both the cellar and upstairs.
  • Floor and repeat upstairs for the two bedrooms, mirroring the partitions below so the walls stack and carry the roof.
  • Doors swing into the room shown by the arc; hang plank doors and glaze the windows once the walls top out.
  • Keep the stove on stone and a block clear of timber. A cool, sealed cellar with one solid door keeps food best.
