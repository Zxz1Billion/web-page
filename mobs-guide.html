Bestiary

A survivalist's bestiary & ledger for To the Moon 2.0. The overworld is no longer a petting zoo — barrows hide axe-swinging guardians, snow-caves hold an ice-mantis the size of a house, and the zombie that just cornered you may be a mutant. This is the hub for the new hostiles, how to put them down, and the survival nets — graves, sleeping bags, a full cooler of fish — that keep you in the fight after you lose one.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

To the Moon 2.0 is a dangerous pack. The mods on this page do two opposite jobs: some add monsters that will end a careless run in seconds, and the rest are the safety equipment that lets you take those fights at all. Read this guide as a pair of ledgers — a bestiary of what hunts you, and an insurance policy for when it wins.

There are three kinds of mod covered here. Sort them straight before you wander out:

the threats

New hostiles

Mowzie's Mobs mini-bosses & ambient creatures, and Mutant Monsters — brutal upgrades of the vanilla mobs you stopped fearing long ago.

the nets

Survival insurance

Corail Tombstone keeps your gear on death, Aquaculture 2 keeps you fed, and Comforts lets you rest anywhere on the road.

the plan

Loadout & logistics

A combat loadout drawn from the other guides' gear, plus an expedition checklist so you never leave base under-equipped.

What this guide does not cover

  • Cataclysm bosses (the Ender Guardian, Ignis, the Leviathan, the Netherite Monstrosity and friends) live in the Cataclysm guide.
  • Twilight Forest progression bosses (Naga, Lich, Hydra, Ur-Ghast, and the rest of the locked-progression chain) live in the Twilight Forest guide.
  • Goblin Traders are only mentioned in passing here — they're wandering merchants you meet while exploring, covered with the rest of overworld exploration. (See the exploration material on the guide hub.)
The golden rule

Never engage a Mowzie's or Mutant boss for the first time in your only set of good gear without a Tombstone safety net active and a totem in hand. The point of §03 is that death is a setback, not a wipe — but only if you set it up before the fight, not after.

01 — The bestiary · bobmowzie

Mowzie's Mobs

Mowzie's Mobs is a creature mod built around a handful of hand-animated mini-bosses with real fight mechanics — telegraphs, phases, and signature gear rewards — plus a cast of ambient creatures that flavour the overworld. You don't grind these; you hunt them, usually after stumbling on their lair while exploring. Each boss is a self-contained encounter with a weapon or trinket worth the bruises.

Where they live

Mowzie's lairs and creatures are placed by world generation — barrows, snowy caves, savanna-style structures and deep stone. You find them by exploring, not by farming spawners. Treat sighting one as the start of an expedition (§07), not a quick detour.

The creatures & ambient life

cave

Grottol

A shy, crystalline cave mob that flees through stone. Kill it before it escapes and it drops diamonds — a rare but worthwhile cave windfall.

flying

Naga

A flying serpent that strafes and dives at travellers in the open. Aerial harassment — bring a bow and keep a wall at your back.

ambush

Foliaath

A carnivorous plant that lies hidden in jungle foliage until you step close, then lunges to bite. The classic "the bush just ate me" hazard.

lore

Lanterns & Ferrous lore

Decorative and lore items tie the bosses together — the mod has a quiet narrative threaded through its structures and drops for the curious.

Read the telegraph

Every Mowzie's boss broadcasts its big attack with an animation wind-up. These fights reward patience over DPS: bait the slam or the roar, sidestep, punish the recovery, repeat. Going in swinging blindly is how a Wroughtnaut deletes a full set of diamond.

02 — The bestiary · Fuzs

Mutant Monsters

Mutant Monsters takes the four vanilla horrors you long ago stopped fearing — the zombie, skeleton, creeper and enderman — and re-imagines each as a hulking, multi-attack mini-boss with far more health, devastating moves, and gear-grade drops. They appear naturally in the world (rarely) and can be deliberately created, so they're as much a resource as a threat.

The four mutants

Mutant variants — behaviour & the gear they enable
MutantWhat it doesReward / crafting payoff
Mutant ZombieA massive bruiser with ground-pound shockwaves and a roar that knocks you back. Plays dead and revives if you don't finish it.Drops Hulk Hammer material — a heavy, AoE-slamming weapon.
Mutant SkeletonFires barrages of fast arrows and slams with its bow in melee; mobile and relentless at range.Drops the parts for strong skeletal/mutant gear and ranged upgrades.
Mutant EndermanTeleports aggressively, throws blocks, and reaches with long-range tendril attacks. The most chaotic of the four.Drops the Endersoul Hand — a teleportation/utility tool built from its essence.
Mutant CreeperCharges and detonates in a much larger blast; can launch and spawn smaller creepers. Terrain-wrecking on a fuse.Explosive-themed drops; extreme reward-for-risk if you can out-range the blast.

Making your own mutants

You don't have to wait for a wild spawn. Using Chemical X on the corresponding vanilla mob transforms it into its mutant form — a way to farm the gear drops on your own terms, at your own risk.

Approximate, by design

Mutants carry vastly more effective health than their vanilla counterparts and hit far harder — exact figures are config-tunable and best read from the in-game entity, so treat any number you hear as approximate. Practically: assume a mutant survives several hits that would have flattened the normal mob, and that one of its hits can take a big chunk of an un-totemed health bar.

Open ground, please

The Mutant Creeper and Mutant Zombie both have AoE knock-and-slam moves and the Creeper a building-flattening blast. Fight all four in the open with room to kite — never in a corridor, and never standing on anything you'd hate to lose to an explosion.

03 — Staying alive · Corail_31

Corail Tombstone — death insurance & soul magic

This is the single most important quality-of-life mod for the bosses above. When you die, Corail Tombstone generates a Grave at the spot that holds your entire inventory and your experience. Your gear doesn't scatter, despawn, or burn in lava — it waits for you behind the grave's protection. For a pack that can one-shot you, this turns a death from a run-ending catastrophe into an inconvenient walk back.

The death net

  • Grave on death — all items and XP are stored in a grave block where you fell. Retrieve them by breaking it or using a Grave Key.
  • Grave Key — a craftable key that instantly recovers a grave's contents from a distance, so you don't have to fight back to the exact death tile through whatever killed you.
  • Teleport-to-grave scrolls — soul-magic scrolls that warp you (or pull the grave) so a death deep in a lair doesn't strand your gear.
  • Decorative graves — once recovered, graves can be kept as memorials; the mod has a whole cosmetic layer.

Knowledge of death & soul magic

Tombstone is more than a grave block — surviving (and dying) builds Knowledge of Death, a progression that unlocks soul-powered abilities fuelled by Soul energy:

progression

Knowledge of Death

An XP-like track earned through play that gates the mod's enchantments and magical perks. The more you live dangerously, the more it grants.

enchants

Tombstone enchantments

Unique enchantments — soul-bound gear, magic siphon, and more — that lean into the death/soul theme and reinforce the safety net.

companion

Familiar Receptacle

Capture and carry a familiar — a pet/companion bound to you via soul magic, summonable as backup in a fight.

fuel

Soul energy

The currency for scrolls and abilities, gathered through the soul mechanics. Budget it like any consumable before a big delve.

Set it up before you need it

The grave appears automatically, but the Grave Key and teleport scrolls are things you craft in advance. Carry a Grave Key on every expedition — recovering your loadout from across a lair, instead of corpse-running past the boss that killed you, is the difference between a bad day and a lost run.

Not a substitute for skill

Tombstone protects your items, not your progress on a fight — most bosses reset when you die. Use the net as a buffer to learn an encounter safely, not as a licence to facetank it.

04 — Staying alive · Shadow

Aquaculture 2 — the fishing overhaul

An empty hunger bar gets you killed as surely as any boss. Aquaculture 2 turns fishing from a vanilla afterthought into a reliable, scalable food and loot pipeline: dozens of new fish keyed to biome and water type, upgraded rods, and a real tackle system. It's the calmest way to keep an expedition fed and to pull genuine treasure out of the water.

The tackle

Aquaculture's gear & what it changes
ItemWhat it does
Material fishing rods (Iron & up)Sturdier, higher-durability rods beyond the vanilla wooden one — the Iron Rod is the baseline upgrade; better materials raise durability and capability.
HooksSlot into the rod to bias your catch — faster bites, better loot odds, or double-catches depending on the hook.
Bait & luresWorms and bait improve catch rate and quality. Stock up before a long trip so the fishing pays out.
Fillet KnifeProcesses whole fish into fillets (and turns some catches into usable cooking ingredients), stretching every catch further.

What you pull up

  • Biome & water-type fish — different species appear in different biomes and in fresh vs salt vs other water, so where you cast matters. Cooked fish are solid, abundant food.
  • Treasure & fishing loot — Aquaculture's loot tables yield more than fish: gear, materials, and rare hauls like Neptune's Bounty-tier finds for the patient angler.
  • Cross-mod cooking — fish flow straight into Farmer's Delight via Aquaculture Delight integration, so your catch becomes real cooked meals. (See the Farmer's Delight guide.)
The lazy food economy

Set up near water at base, slot a good hook, keep bait on hand, and a few minutes of fishing yields a stack of meals plus occasional loot. It's the lowest-effort way to never starve mid-delve — pack the surplus before an expedition (§07).

05 — Staying alive · TheIllusiveC4

Comforts — rest on the road

On a long delve you'll want to skip the night without dragging a bed everywhere or accidentally resetting your spawn to the middle of a hostile cave. Comforts solves both with two portable items: Sleeping Bags and Hammocks. Small mod, outsized impact on any expedition.

expedition

Sleeping Bag

Sleep through the night without setting your spawn point — your home bed stays your respawn. Lightweight and great for delves; they wear out with use, so pack a few rather than relying on one forever.

overworld

Hammock

String it between two solid blocks to skip the night out in the open. The same "rest anywhere" idea in a more permanent, reusable form for camps.

Why this matters for the bosses

Mowzie's and Mutant fights are easier in daylight with full hunger and no swarm of regular night mobs piling on. A Sleeping Bag lets you skip to morning right outside a lair, take the boss fresh, and keep your real spawn safely at base — no risk of respawning next to the thing that just killed you.

Same rules still apply

You can only sleep at night (or during a thunderstorm), and only when no hostile mobs are nearby. Clear the area first — a Sleeping Bag won't save you from trying to nap on a monster's doorstep.

06 — The hunt

Combat Loadout & Tactics

The pack gives you several whole arsenals to bring against the bestiary above. You won't use all of them at once — pick a primary damage source, layer on defence and consumables, and tailor the kit to the fight. Here's where the firepower comes from and how to point it.

Where your gear comes from

Arsenals across the pack (each has its own guide)
SourceBrings to a boss fightGuide
Tinkers' ConstructBuild-your-own melee weapons with modifiers (sharpness, looting/luck, fiery, knockback resist) tuned per encounter.Tinkers' guide
Iron's Spells 'n SpellbooksRanged magic — fire/lightning/ice damage and mobility spells to kite fliers and pressure ranged bosses.Spellbooks guide
ApotheosisAffix-enchanted gear, gems and the enchantment ceiling that pushes your damage and defence past vanilla limits.Apotheosis guide
MekanismThe MekaSuit & Meka-Tool — powered armour with damage absorption, flight and an electric melee for the late game.Mekanism guide

Consumables & safety items

  • Totem of Undying — the single most valuable thing in your offhand against any boss. It eats the one-shot you didn't see coming. Never fight without one.
  • Potions — Regeneration and Strength for the damage race; Fire Resistance for solar/fire bosses; Slow Falling and Speed for fliers and kiting.
  • Golden apples / enchanted apples — instant absorption + regen buffer to ride out a burst.
  • Milk bucket — clears the slows and debuffs Frostmaw's frost and others apply.
  • Tombstone Grave Key (§03) — your recovery plan, carried before the fight.

Tactics by threat type

heavy melee

vs Wroughtnaut / Mutant Zombie

Bait the slow wind-up, sidestep the slam, punish the recovery. Stack knockback-resistance gear so the shockwaves don't juggle you. Patience > DPS.

ranged

vs Barako / Mutant Skeleton

Use cover and break line of sight between volleys; close the gap or out-range them with magic. Fire Resistance for Barako's solar attacks.

flying

vs Naga / fliers

Bow, spells, or a thrown weapon. Keep a wall or overhang at your back so you only watch one approach lane; Slow Falling if you take the fight elevated.

explosive

vs Mutant Creeper

Out-range the blast entirely — ranged only, in open ground, never standing on anything you can't afford to lose. Blast Protection helps if it closes.

Offhand discipline

If you remember one thing from this section: Totem of Undying in the offhand, every single fight. Combined with the Tombstone net, it means almost nothing in this pack can permanently cost you — but only if the totem is equipped before the hit lands, not in your hotbar waiting to be swapped.

07 — The hunt · the ledger

A Survival Checklist for Expeditions

Most deaths in this pack are logistics failures, not skill failures — you ran out of food, slept in the wrong place, or left your totem in a chest. Run this checklist before you leave base to hunt a Mowzie's lair, chase a mutant, or push into a structure.

Pre-departure ledger

  1. Tombstone ready — Grave Key crafted and in your pack; a teleport scroll if you have one. Confirm your real spawn is set at base. (§03)
  2. Totem(s) of Undying — at least one in the offhand, spares in the hotbar. (§06)
  3. Food packed — a stack of cooked fish or Farmer's Delight meals from your Aquaculture pipeline. Never leave on a half-empty hunger bar. (§04)
  4. Rest gear — a few Sleeping Bags (or a Hammock) so you can skip the night at the lair without moving spawn. (§05)
  5. Primary weapon + backup — your tuned melee or spellbook, plus a ranged option for fliers and a second weapon in case durability runs out mid-delve. (§06)
  6. Potions & cures — Regen/Strength, Fire Resistance, and a milk bucket for debuffs. (§06)
  7. Build & light — blocks for walling off and pillaring, plus torches; the ability to seal a fighting arena turns chaos into a duel.
The expedition loop

Arrive → clear the area → Sleeping Bag to morning → scout the boss and read its telegraphs from safety → equip totem → engage with an escape route planned. If you die, Grave Key recovers your loadout while you regroup, then re-approach fresh. That loop beats every boss on this page eventually.

Cross-reference for the big bosses

This checklist also carries you into the pack's dedicated boss content. For the Cataclysm bosses and the Twilight Forest progression chain, the same loadout philosophy applies — but those mods gate their own gear and arenas, so read their guides before you commit a loadout to them.

The short version

  • Net: Grave Key
  • Cheat death: Totem offhand
  • Fuel: Cooked fish
  • Rest: Sleeping Bag
  • Reach: Bow / spell
  • Cure: Milk + potions
  • Read: the telegraph

★ — The path

Progression — survive, then hunt

There's an order to this pack, and it isn't "grab a sword and go". To the Moon 2.0 rewards the patient: build the safety nets first so a death costs you a walk instead of a run, then earn the gear, then spend it on the bestiary. Survive, kit out, hunt the easy bosses for unique tools, then turn those gains on the pack's dedicated boss content. This is the spine that threads every section above together.

  1. Lay the safety nets first. Before you pick a single fight, set up the insurance: get a Corail Tombstone grave net active with a Grave Key crafted and Soulbound protecting your best items, pack a Comforts sleeping bag so you can skip the night at any lair, and run an Aquaculture 2 line into Farmer's Delight meals so your hunger bar never decides a fight. (§03–§05)
  2. Kit out with the pack's combat gear. With the nets up, assemble a real loadout from the arsenals in §06 — a tuned melee weapon or spellbook, layered defence, potions, and a Totem of Undying glued to your offhand. Death is now a buffer, so you can learn fights instead of fearing them.
  3. Fight Mowzie's lair bosses for unique weapons. Hunt the Mowzie's Mobs mini-bosses for their signature rewards — the Axe of a Thousand Metals off the Ferrous Wroughtnaut, frost gear from Frostmaw, solar trinkets from Barako. These are self-contained, telegraph-readable fights and the best place to blood a fresh loadout. (§01)
  4. Take on the Mutant Monsters in the open. Carry those weapons against the Mutant Monsters out in open ground, where you can kite the AoE slams and out-range the Creeper's blast. Win and you walk away with the Hulk Hammer and the Endersoul Hand — gear-grade payoffs you can farm on your own terms with Chemical X. (§02)
  5. Push the pack's dedicated boss content. Now spend the gains. The unique weapons, the upgraded loadout, and the death net together are what get you through the gated boss chains — the Twilight Forest progression bosses and the Cataclysm behemoths. Read their guides before you commit a loadout; they gate their own arenas and gear.
  6. Loop and refine. Each boss feeds the next — Mowzie's drops harden your kit for the mutants, the mutants' gear hardens it for Twilight and Cataclysm. Re-run the expedition checklist (§07) every trip and let the net catch the mistakes you make learning each new fight.
Where this sits in the bigger climb

This page is the survival-and-early-boss leg of a longer journey. For how it slots into the whole pack — tech, magic, dimensions and the endgame — follow The Ascent. Survive here first; the nets and the unique weapons you earn on this page are what make the rest of the climb possible.
