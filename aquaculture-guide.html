00 — Orientation

Start Here

Vanilla fishing gives you cod, salmon, the occasional enchanted book, and a lot of waiting. Aquaculture 2 keeps the gentle rhythm but makes every cast worth something: dozens of fish sorted by biome and water type, better rods with slots for a Hook and Bait, an upgraded treasure and loot pool, and real food once you learn to fillet. It's a fishing overhaul that quietly becomes a progression system.

catch

More fish, by place

Tuna, Catfish, Arapaima, Gar, Perch, Piranha and many more — each bites in specific biomes and water types, not just "any water."

gear

Rods with slots

Material rods (iron, gold, diamond…) carry more durability and accept a Hook + Bait to shape what you reel in.

food

Worthwhile loot & food

Fillet the catch for cookable food, and fish up tackle, neptunium treasure, and the odd message in a bottle.

cross-ref

Plays with others

Pairs with the Mobs & Survival guide for biome context, and Farmer's Delight via the Aquaculture Delight add-on for cooking.

The loop, in one breath

Build a better rod → fit a hook and bait → fish the right water in the right biome → fillet your catch for food and feed it into your kitchen → cash in treasure for Neptunium gear. Everything below is just detail on those five beats.

Where this guide sits

  • For which biome is which and what lives around the water you're fishing, see the companion Mobs & Survival guide.
  • For turning your catch into proper meals, see the Farmer's Delight guide — the Aquaculture Delight add-on bridges your fish straight into that mod's cutting board and cooking pot.
  • Numbers here (weights, drop rates, durability) are approximate and frequently config/pack-tuned. The in-game Fish Bestiary and JEI are the ground truth.

01 — The rod ladder

Rods

Aquaculture's signature item is the Iron Fishing Rod and its higher-material siblings. Where the vanilla rod is a single tool, these are platforms: each tier carries more durability and exposes two slots — one for a Hook and one for Bait — that change how and what you catch.

The material rods, roughly weakest → strongest
RodFeelNotes
Wooden / vanillaBaselineThe starting point. Crafts into the Iron rod once you have iron.
Iron Fishing RodThe real starterFirst rod with Hook + Bait slots; the workhorse early-game.
Gold Fishing RodSidegradeLower durability but a different feel; often easy to craft from spare gold.
Diamond Fishing RodDurableHigh durability for long sessions; a solid mid-game rod.
Neptunium Fishing RodEndgameCrafted from fished-up Neptunium treasure (see §06); the best rod and water-themed.

Packs may add or rebalance further material tiers (e.g. other metal rods); check JEI for exactly which rods your install ships and their stats.

Crafting & upgrading

  1. Craft the Iron Fishing Rod. It follows the familiar rod shape but in its material — iron ingots and string — and immediately gives you the Hook + Bait slots.
  2. Open the rod's GUI / slots. Slot a Hook and some Bait before you cast; the rod remembers them between casts until the bait runs out.
  3. Move up materials as you get them — diamond for durability, then the Neptunium rod once treasure starts rolling in.
  4. Enchant it. The usual fishing enchants — Lure (faster bites) and Luck of the Sea (better outcomes) — still apply and stack with hooks/bait, plus Unbreaking/Mending for longevity.
Durability, not just catch rate

The point of climbing the rod ladder early isn't a dramatically rarer catch — it's durability and slots. A diamond rod with Unbreaking/Mending plus a good hook means you fish for an hour without re-crafting, which matters more than any single number.

02 — Tuning the cast

Hooks & Bait

The two rod slots are where Aquaculture gets tactical. Hooks change how you catch — what comes up and how the bobber behaves. Bait & Lures change how often and how well — raising catch rate and luck. Used together they let you aim a session at fish, or at treasure, or at sheer speed.

Hooks

Representative hooks (effects are approximate — read the tooltip)
HookBehaviour
Light HookBiases toward smaller / lighter catches; quicker, lower-stakes bites.
Heavy HookBiases toward larger, heavier fish — the trophy end of the weight range.
Double HookChance to reel in two items at once; doubles output on a good day.
Redstone HookEmits a redstone pulse on a catch — handy for automated/AFK fishing rigs.
Note (others)Aquaculture ships further hook types; their tooltips spell out the exact bias or bonus.

Bait & Lures

Bait is consumed over casts and pushes your odds toward fish (and away from junk). The two easiest sources are right under your feet:

  • Worms — till dirt/grass with a hoe and worms occasionally pop out. Free, renewable, the classic starter bait.
  • Minnows — tiny fish you catch (or find) that themselves serve as bait for bigger species.
  • Other lures — the mod includes additional bait/lure items; each lists its catch-rate / luck bonus on the tooltip.
How to attach them

Open the rod and place a Hook in the hook slot and Bait in the bait slot — or, in many versions, just combine the hook with the rod in a crafting grid and right-click bait onto the rod. Once fitted, the hook stays until removed and the bait depletes as you fish; refill it when the catch rate dips.

Match the tool to the target

A Heavy Hook + good bait is how you chase record-weight trophies; a Double Hook with cheap worms is how you farm volume for filleting; a Redstone Hook is for your auto-fisher. There's no single "best" loadout — pick for the session's goal.

03 — The bestiary

The Fish

This is the heart of the mod. Instead of cod-or-salmon, Aquaculture 2 stocks dozens of species that bite only in the right biome and the right water — fresh vs salt, warm vs cold, ocean vs swamp vs river. Catching a new one for the first time logs it in the Fish Bestiary, and each fish rolls a randomised weight/size so there are personal records to chase.

A sampler of species and where each bites (biome/water keyed — see in-game for the full list)
FishWhere it bites
TunaWarm / temperate oceans (saltwater)
Salmon variants (Atlantic, Pink, Blue, etc.)Cold rivers & oceans; specific variants per cold biome
CatfishSwamps & warm fresh water
PiranhaJungle / warm fresh water
ArapaimaJungle rivers & warm fresh water (a big one)

Tropical speciesWarm oceans & reefs
Biome + water type are the lock

Many fish are gated on both the biome's temperature/category and whether the water is salt or fresh. Fishing the same pond all day caps your variety fast — if you want the full bestiary you have to travel and fish different waters. Cross-reference the Mobs & Survival guide for which biome is which.

Weight, size & records

Every fish you land comes with a rolled weight (and an implied size), shown on its tooltip. The mod tracks your bests, so two anglers can both have "a Tuna" and still compete over whose was heavier. Hooks nudge this distribution — a Heavy Hook shifts you toward the top of a species' weight band, which is exactly how trophy hunters chase records.

The Fish Bestiary

The in-game bestiary is both a checklist and a hint book: it shows which species exist and, broadly, where they live. Treat "fill the bestiary" as the soft goal that pulls you across the map — each new biome's water is a new page or two.

04 — From catch to plate

Filleting & Food

A raw fish isn't always great food on its own — the value comes from filleting. The Fillet Knife turns most fish into Fish Fillets, which cook (furnace, smoker, or campfire) into solid, stackable food. One good fishing trip becomes a pantry.

The Fillet Knife

  1. Craft a Fillet Knife. Like a sword/knife in its material; better materials mean more durability per blade.
  2. Fillet your catch. Use the knife on fish to yield Fish Fillets (bigger fish give more) — far more food value than eating the fish whole.
  3. Cook the fillets in any heat source for cooked fillets: reliable, restorative food that stacks well for travel.
Why fillet at all?

Filleting concentrates your catch into compact, cookable food and frees you from carrying dozens of distinct fish items. It's the difference between "a backpack of random fish" and "a stack of cooked fillets" — and it's the on-ramp to the kitchen below.

Aquaculture Delight (cross-ref)

If your pack runs Farmer's Delight, the Aquaculture Delight add-on plugs your catch directly into that mod's kitchen: fish and fillets become ingredients on the cutting board and in the cooking pot, unlocking proper dishes (think fish stews, sushi-style plates, and more) instead of just "cooked fillet."

  • Use the cutting board as a knife-free way to process some fish into usable parts.
  • Feed fillets and fish into cooking pot recipes for higher-tier, longer-lasting meals.
  • See the dedicated Farmer's Delight guide for the kitchen mechanics; this is just the bridge that gets your fish into it.

05 — Not just fish

Treasure & Loot

Aquaculture rebuilds the loot pool around fishing. Alongside the fish you'll reel in treasure, tackle, the occasional message in a bottle, and — yes — junk. The headline is the Neptunium material line: fishing becomes a genuine source of gear, not just dinner.

What comes up besides fish
CategoryExamples & use
Treasure — NeptuniumNeptunium ore / Neptune's Bounty material used to craft the Neptunium tool & armour set (§06).
TackleHooks, bait, and rod components — fishing partly funds its own gear.
Message in a BottleFlavour / note item; a small reward and collectible.
Useful dropsEnchanted books, leather, name tags, and the usual vanilla-treasure flavour, reweighted.
JunkSticks, bones, bowls, lily pads — the chaff. Good bait/luck pushes the roll away from this.
Luck shapes the pool

The split between fish, treasure, and junk is a weighted roll. Luck of the Sea, bait/lures, and the right hook all bias it toward treasure and away from junk — so a treasure-hunting loadout is real and worth assembling when you're farming Neptunium.

Because the treasure pool feeds the Neptunium line, fishing is a viable way to gear up in its own right — especially handy early, or in packs where you'd rather sit by the water than spelunk for materials.

06 — Armed by the sea

Neptunium Gear

The payoff of all that fishing. Neptunium — gathered from fishing treasure (Neptune's Bounty / Neptunium material) — crafts into a full tool and armour set with water-themed perks. It's the reason an afternoon on the dock can actually arm you.

The Neptunium set (perks are approximate — confirm in-game)
PieceTheme / perk
Neptunium Pickaxe / Axe / Shovel / HoeHigh-tier tools on par with (or above) diamond, with water affinity.
Neptunium SwordStrong melee — bonus behaviour around water / aquatic mobs.
Neptunium Fishing RodThe best rod in the set; closes the loop — fishing makes the rod that makes the fishing better.
Neptunium Armour (full set)Water-themed defensive perks — aquatic mobility / underwater utility when worn as a set.
Why fishing can arm you

Because the material comes from the treasure pool, you assemble Neptunium by doing the thing the mod is about. Set bonuses lean into water — swimming, breathing, and fighting near the sea — which makes Neptunium especially strong for the very biomes you fish in. Run a treasure-luck loadout (§05) to farm the material faster.

Exact stats, durability, and which perks attach to which piece vary by version and pack balance. Hover the items and check JEI for the recipes and the set bonus your install actually grants.

07 — The angler's habits

Tips

Pulling it together: a handful of habits turn Aquaculture from "a few extra fish" into a reliable food and gear pipeline.

  • Match hook + bait to target
  • Travel for variety
  • Fillet for food
  • Cook in your FD kitchen
  • Luck for treasure
  1. Match hook + bait to the fish. Heavy Hook + strong bait for trophies, Double Hook + worms for volume, Redstone Hook for an auto-fisher. Set the loadout for the session's goal.
  2. Fish different biomes and waters. The bestiary is gated on biome and salt-vs-fresh — one pond caps your variety fast. Carry a rod when you travel and cast in every new water.
  3. Fillet everything you don't need raw. The Fillet Knife concentrates your catch into compact, cookable food; cooked fillets are some of the easiest stable food in the game.
  4. Feed the catch into your kitchen. With Aquaculture Delight, route fish and fillets into Farmer's Delight's cutting board and cooking pot for real meals — see that guide.
  5. Run luck when you want gear. Luck of the Sea + bait + the right hook bias the roll toward treasure; that's how you farm Neptunium and craft the set in §06.
  6. Keep the rod alive. Unbreaking + Mending on a diamond or Neptunium rod means you fish indefinitely without re-crafting.
A note on accuracy

Species lists, weights, drop weights, and Neptunium perks are configurable and differ between versions and packs. Treat every number here as a starting expectation, not a contract — your in-game Fish Bestiary, item tooltips, and JEI are the ground truth.
