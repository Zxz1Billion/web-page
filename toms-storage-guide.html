A working field guide to Tom's Simple Storage Mod — the lightweight, AE-lite storage network. No channels, no power, no cable spaghetti: link your chests and barrels with an Inventory Connector, slap a Storage Terminal on it, and access everything from one searchable, sortable window. 00 — Orientation

Start Here

Tom's Simple Storage Mod gives you a digital storage network with almost no overhead. You connect a cluster of vanilla (and modded) inventories — chests, barrels, drawers — to a single Inventory Connector, then place a Storage Terminal on or beside it. The terminal aggregates every linked inventory into one searchable, sortable grid. That's the whole idea: one window over many chests, no per-device wiring and no electricity.

cable-free

No power, no channels

Unlike AE2, nothing here draws energy or needs a controller. Build it and it just works.

searchable

One window, many chests

Search by name, mod, or tag; sort and auto-sort; pull and deposit stacks with shift-click.

cheap

Early-game friendly

Recipes are inexpensive — a terminal and a connector are reachable not long after first chests.

stepping-stone

The on-ramp to AE2

Learn network thinking here, then graduate to Applied Energistics 2 when you need autocrafting.

Where this sits in the pack

Think of Tom's as the bridge between "a wall of labelled chests" and a full AE2 system. If your pack has both, many players start here, then move the master inventory into AE2 once they want autocrafting and tidy item routing. See the AE2 guide and the modpack's Storage hub for the next step.

The shape of a build

  1. Place an Inventory Connector. This is the hidden heart of the network — every linked inventory feeds into it.
  2. Surround it with storage. Put chests, barrels, and drawers touching the connector (or reach further with cables and trim).
  3. Add a Storage Terminal. Place it on or adjacent to the connector; it now shows everything the connector can see.
  4. Search and sort. Type a name, mod, or tag; pick a sort mode; shift-click to move stacks.
  5. Go wireless (optional). Craft a Wireless Terminal to reach the same network from a distance.

02 — Wiring it up (without wires)

Building a Network

A network is one Inventory Connector plus whatever storage you attach to it. There's no controller, no power feed, and no channel budget — connectivity is purely physical adjacency, optionally extended by cables and trim.

The three ways to connect storage

  1. Directly adjacent. Any inventory touching a face of the Inventory Connector is automatically part of the pool. The simplest network is a connector with chests on every side.
  2. Via Trim. Place Trim blocks to extend the connector's effective surface, so a larger block of chests/barrels all register as connected without each one touching the connector itself.
  3. Via Cable. Run Inventory Cable from the connector and cap the far end with a Cable Connector against a distant inventory. This is how you reach storage in another room.
Minimal viable build

Place an Inventory Connector. Put a chest against it. Place a Storage Terminal on another face. Open the terminal — the chest's contents appear. Add more chests around the connector and they all merge into one searchable grid. That's a complete, working storage network.

How aggregation works

The connector doesn't move items — it presents the contents of every linked inventory as one combined view. When you withdraw from the terminal, the mod pulls from whichever chest actually holds that item; when you deposit, it slots the item into a chest that already has matching stacks or has free space. To you it looks like a single bottomless inventory; physically, your items are still sitting in ordinary chests and barrels you can open by hand.

Connected ≠ infinite

The connector tracks a finite number of inventories and slots (see §05). Big walls of chests are fine, but if items stop appearing after you add a lot of storage, you've likely hit the connector's capacity rather than a placement bug.

Letting automation in: the Inventory Proxy

If you want hoppers, pipes, or other mods to interact with the whole network, place an Inventory Proxy. It exposes the entire connected pool as a single inventory face, so an importer can dump items into the network or an exporter can draw from it — handy for feeding a mob farm's output into storage automatically.

03 — The window

The Terminal

The Storage Terminal is where you actually live. It's a scrollable grid of every item on the network with a search bar up top, sort controls, and shift-click transfers. Once you've used it, going back to opening chests by hand feels archaic.

Search bar & syntax

The search field filters the grid live as you type. Beyond plain text it understands a few prefixes:

Search syntax (approximate — your pack's build is ground truth)
TypeExampleMatches
Plain textcopperItems whose display name contains the text.
By mod@mekanismItems from a specific mod (match on mod name/id).
By tooltip#fireproofText found in an item's tooltip.
By tag$ingotsItems belonging to a tag / ore-dictionary group.
Prefixes vary by version

The exact prefix characters (@ for mod, # for tooltip, $ for tag) are the common convention, but they can differ between mod versions and packs. If a prefix doesn't filter as expected, check the in-game search tooltip or hover help — that's the ground truth for your install.

Sorting & auto-sort

A sort button cycles through modes — typically by name (A→Z / Z→A), by quantity (most / fewest), and by recency / slot order. With auto-sort enabled the grid re-sorts itself as counts change so the most-stocked items stay where you expect them; disable it if you prefer items to hold a stable position while you work.

Stack & transfer controls

  • Left-click a grid item to pick up a stack into your cursor; right-click to grab a half-stack.
  • Shift-click a grid item to pull a full stack straight into your inventory.
  • Shift-click an item in your own inventory to deposit it into the network.
  • Drag or use the quantity controls to pull custom amounts where supported.

The Crafting Terminal

The Crafting Terminal is the same window with a 3×3 crafting grid bolted on. The grid pulls ingredients directly from the network, so you can craft repeatedly without ferrying items into your own inventory — lay out a recipe, take the result, and it auto-refills the grid from storage. It is the single most quality-of-life upgrade in the mod; most players build this variant instead of the plain terminal.

JEI integration

With JEI installed, clicking a recipe's + (recipe-fill) button on a Crafting Terminal will populate the crafting grid from items in the network where they're available — turning the terminal into a near-one-click crafting station for known recipes.

04 — Access from anywhere

Wireless

Once your base sprawls, walking back to the terminal gets old. The mod's wireless tier lets you open the network from your inventory, within range of it.

Wireless access items
ItemWhat it does
Wireless TerminalA handheld terminal. Bind it to a network, then open the full searchable grid from anywhere in range.
Wireless Crafting TerminalThe handheld version with the built-in crafting grid — craft from your network on the move.

Linking & range

You bind a wireless terminal to a network — generally by using it on a Storage Terminal or the connector — which records the network's location. After that, opening the item connects you remotely as long as you're within the configured access range. Range is approximate and pack-configurable; some setups also tie effective reach to an upgrade/level so you can extend how far the link works. When in doubt, the item's tooltip shows what it's bound to.

One terminal to rule the base

A single Wireless Crafting Terminal in your hotbar effectively gives you your entire storage and a crafting bench everywhere you go. It's the convenience payoff that makes Tom's feel close to AE2's wireless access without any of the channel or power management.

Range is finite (and configurable)

Wireless access isn't infinite by default — step outside the range and the terminal won't open the network. Exact distances vary by version and pack config, so treat any number here as approximate and verify in-game.

05 — What it can and can't see

Filtering & Limits

The simplicity that makes Tom's pleasant also means it has hard edges. Knowing the connector's limits saves you a confusing afternoon of "where did my items go."

What the connector can see

The connector aggregates standard item inventories it's linked to — vanilla chests/barrels, and most modded storage that exposes an ordinary item-handler (including drawers). It reads and writes those slots directly. What it generally won't meaningfully surface are fluid/energy stores, machine inventories with restrictive input/output rules, or storage that exposes itself in a non-standard way — those may show partially or not at all.

Approximate connector capacity (verify against your pack's config)
LimitRoughlyWhy it matters
Linked inventories~a few dozenThe connector tracks a finite number of attached inventories; past it, extra chests stop registering.
Total slots~thousandsThere's an overall slot ceiling across the whole pool; large drawer walls eat into it.
Cable run lengthlimitedCables can only extend so far before the run stops carrying the connection.
These numbers are illustrative

The figures above are approximate and configurable per pack. Do not plan a megabase around them — open the connector's GUI in-game (it shows used/total inventories and slots) and check your pack's config. The in-game readout and JEI are the only ground truth.

Keeping it performant

  • Prefer fewer, bigger inventories. A handful of barrels or drawer banks is lighter than dozens of single chests holding the same items.
  • Use drawers for bulk. A drawer holds one item type in huge quantity using a single connection — far more efficient than spreading stacks across many chests.
  • Don't over-cable. Long, branching cable runs and very large pools add scanning overhead. Keep storage compact around the connector where you can.
  • One connector, one network. Splitting a base across several small connectors is usually cleaner than one sprawling pool straining its limits.

06 — Pick your storage

Comparison

Tom's, AE2, and plain drawers solve overlapping problems with very different complexity budgets. Here's how to choose.

When to graduate to AE2

Stay on Tom's while your needs are "find my items fast and craft from one window." Move to AE2 when you want autocrafting (request an item and have the system build it from sub-recipes), fine-grained item routing and storage prioritisation, or a network too large for a single connector's limits. Many players keep both: Tom's terminals on local sorting chests, AE2 as the master autocrafting brain.

Drawers are a complement, not a rival

Storage Drawers and Tom's pair beautifully: drawers give cheap, compact bulk storage for one item each, and the Inventory Connector reads them straight into the terminal. You get drawer efficiency with terminal convenience — no power, no channels.

07 — Field notes

Tips

Small habits that make a Tom's network feel effortless.

  • Bulk: drawers behind the connector
  • Access: one Crafting Terminal per base
  • Convenience: wireless in the hotbar
  • Performance: fewer, bigger inventories
  • Reach: trim & cable for distant chests

Build smart

  • Put barrels and drawers behind the connector for cheap bulk. Each one is a single high-capacity slot the terminal reads instantly — the most efficient way to store thousands of one item without spending channels or power.
  • Run one main terminal per base. A single Crafting Terminal (or its wireless twin) is usually all you need; resist scattering many terminals across one network — it just adds clutter, not capability.
  • Lean on wireless for convenience. A Wireless Crafting Terminal turns your whole storage into a hotbar item. Bind it once and craft from anywhere in range.
  • Use trim to neatly enclose a chest wall. It lets a tidy block of storage all register without each chest touching the connector face.
  • Bridge to other mods with the Inventory Proxy when you want hoppers or pipes auto-depositing farm output into the network.
  • Check the connector GUI before expanding. It shows used vs. total inventories and slots — your early warning before you hit a limit.
The honest summary

Tom's Simple Storage does one thing extremely well: it makes a pile of chests behave like a single searchable inventory, with zero infrastructure. Build it early, enjoy the convenience, and when you start wishing items would craft themselves, that's your cue to read the AE2 guide.
