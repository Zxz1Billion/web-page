The Cartographer's
Journal

To the Moon 2.0 is an exploration-heavy pack: the overworld, the ocean floor, the caves and the other dimensions are seeded with hand-crafted structures, sprawling procedural dungeons and rare boss lairs. This journal maps what is out there — what each structure is, roughly how dangerous it runs, and what reward it pays — so you can plan a route rather than stumble into a pirate ship at half hearts.

Worldgen & structures YUNG's · When Dungeons Arise · Dungeon Crawl MC 1.19.2 · Forge ⚔ Some structures are lethal early

00 — Orientation

Start Here

A surveyor's first job is not to dig — it is to know the terrain. This pack stacks several structure mods on top of vanilla worldgen, so almost every chunk you cross can hide something worth the detour, and a few things worth turning around for.

The mods covered here fall into a few families. Knowing which family a structure belongs to tells you what to expect before you even step inside:

overhaul

YUNG's family

Replaces vanilla's tired dungeons, mineshafts, strongholds and temples with hand-built, loot-rich, more dangerous versions. You will meet these constantly.

landmark

When Dungeons Arise

Big, lavish set-pieces — keeps, towers, treehouses, undead pirate ships — scattered across biomes and oceans. Rare but spectacular and well-defended.

procedural

Dungeon Crawl

One enormous multi-level dungeon woven beneath the surface, themed by the biome above it. A grind-and-clear delve, not a quick raid.

flavour

Taverns · Traders · Lairs

Dungeons and Taverns fleshes out the world; Goblin Traders give you somewhere to spend emeralds; Mowzie's bosses hide in rare lairs.

Two tools you should install first

These live in the Quality-of-Life guide and are covered in full there — but no exploration run should leave home without them, so they earn a mention here:

  • A map mod — JourneyMap (with Map Atlases for the survival-friendly cartography side). A minimap and a waypoint list turn "I found a keep somewhere east" into a coordinate you can return to with a stack of repair materials.
  • Nature's Compass — point it at a biome and it tells you the nearest one. Many structures are biome-locked (desert temples in deserts, pirate ships in oceans, jungle treehouses in jungles), so biome-hunting is structure-hunting.
How to read the danger ratings

Throughout this journal a structure's threat is described qualitatively as mild / moderate / severe / deadly. These are relative to your gear, not absolute — a Better Dungeon is trivial in netherite-plus-affixes and a death-trap in leather. When in doubt, scout the entrance, light it, and retreat to gear up.

Cross-references

Two big structure families have their own dedicated journals and are not re-covered here: the abyssal/temple bosses of Cataclysm, and the entire dimension of Twilight Forest with its progression of bossed structures. When this guide points you at a lair, it sends combat questions to those guides and to the Mobs & Adventure guide.

01 — The overhaul

YUNG's Overhauled Structures

By YUNGNICKYOUNG, this is a whole family of mods that quietly rip out Minecraft's most forgettable structures and rebuild them as hand-crafted, multi-room, loot-rich, and noticeably more hostile places. They all sit on top of a shared library, YUNG's API, which handles the heavy lifting of placing big templated structures into the world — if the API is missing, none of them generate.

The design philosophy is consistent across the family: vanilla layouts you recognise, scaled up into proper dungeons. A cobweb-choked corridor instead of a single mossy box; a fortress you can actually get lost in; temples with real puzzles and real traps. Loot is richer than vanilla but described qualitatively below — treat any specific item as approximate and seed/config dependent.

How to find & clear them

Because most of these replace a vanilla structure, you find them exactly as you always have — Eyes of Ender to the stronghold, dig under a desert pyramid, follow rails to a mineshaft. The difference is what happens after you walk in:

  1. Light the threshold first. These are bigger and darker than vanilla; carry a stack of torches and seal the entrance behind you so nothing follows you out.
  2. Expect more spawners. Better Dungeons and Mineshafts place several mob spawners per structure. Break or block-light them as you go rather than fighting an endless tide.
  3. Watch for traps in the temples. Better Desert & Jungle Temples lean on pressure plates, TNT, falling sand and arrow dispensers. Mine in from above or break the plates from the side.
  4. The loot scales with the structure. The hidden lower levels (especially in desert temples) hold the best chests — clear the obvious floor, then look for the way down.
The Nether fortress is the spike

A YUNG's Better Nether Fortress is genuinely large and genuinely lethal — blazes, wither skeletons and piglin-adjacent threats across multiple levels, over lava. Bring fire resistance, gold trim, a bow, and blocks to bridge. It is still your source of blaze rods and wither skeleton skulls, so you will be coming here regardless.

02 — The set-pieces

When Dungeons Arise & Seven Seas

By Aureljz, When Dungeons Arise is the pack's showcase mod — a collection of large, lavish, hand-built landmarks that generate sparsely across the world. Where YUNG's overhauls the familiar, this drops something you have never seen: a mountain keep clinging to a peak, a thatch-roofed jungle treehouse complex, an illager-held tower, a giant mushroom house. They are gorgeous, they are loot-dense, and they are guarded — most come pre-populated with mobs and a fair few with their own mini-boss-tier defenders.

The companion mod When Dungeons Arise: Seven Seas adds the naval half of the catalogue: undead pirate ships, merchant vessels and other floating structures that generate out on the open ocean. You will not find these by walking — you find them by sailing, or by spotting masts on the horizon from a high coast.

Tactics

  • Approach from above or from range. Keeps and towers funnel you into kill-zones. A bow, a few blocks and the high ground beat charging the front gate.
  • Clear in layers. These are big enough that mobs respawn behind you in the dark. Light each cleared wing before pushing on.
  • Bring a boat for Seven Seas. Pirate ships sit in deep water; you will be fighting on a deck with water on all sides. Watch your footing and don't drop your loot overboard.
  • The reward is gear and blocks. Expect enchanted gear, emeralds, unique decorative blocks and the occasional rare drop. Worth a dedicated voyage once you are past iron.
Worth the trim

These structures are the single best scenic payoff in the pack and a strong source of mid-game gear. If you run a map mod, waypoint every keep and ship you spot — they are rare enough that you will want to come back rather than risk clearing one underprepared.

03 — The deep delve

Dungeon Crawl

By xiroc_, Dungeon Crawl generates a single, large procedural dungeon beneath the surface in most places you stand. Unlike a YUNG's set-piece, this is not one room — it is a multi-level labyrinth of corridors, chambers and staircases that descends floor by floor, growing more dangerous (and more rewarding) the deeper you push. Each dungeon is themed by the surface biome above it, so a desert crawl looks and feels different from a forest one.

Finding the entrance

The dungeon hides underground, but it announces itself with a distinctive entrance staircase — a vertical spiral or stepped shaft that connects the surface (or a near-surface cave) down to the first floor. You find a Dungeon Crawl one of three ways:

  1. Spot the entrance shaft. Watch for an unnatural square stairwell while caving or strip-mining; it leads straight into the top floor.
  2. Break in from the side. If you tunnel into a suspiciously clean stone corridor at depth, you have likely clipped a dungeon wing — follow it to find the stairs.
  3. Dig down and look for structure. They are common enough that a deep shaft in a fresh area often intersects one. The themed brickwork is unmistakable against natural stone.

What to expect inside

layout

Descending floors

Each level is a maze of rooms linked by staircases down. The lower you go, the tougher the mobs and the better the chests.

enemies

Spawners & ambushes

Rooms are seeded with spawners and dark corners. Clear methodically; a single room can flood you if you sprint past a spawner.

loot

Tiered chests

Chests improve with depth. The deepest floors hold the run's best rewards — and usually a final guarded chamber.

theme

Biome flavour

Brick palette, decor and sometimes the mob mix shift with the surface biome above the dungeon.

Pace yourself, mark your way

It is genuinely easy to get lost. Carry plenty of torches, place a consistent trail (e.g. torches always on your left going in), and don't descend a floor you can't afford to fight back up. These reward a slow, complete clear far more than a smash-and-grab.

04 — The trimmings

Dungeons and Taverns

By Nova_Wostra, Dungeons and Taverns is the connective tissue that makes the world feel lived in. Rather than one signature mega-dungeon, it adds a spread of smaller structures and village additions — most notably taverns that generate as part of villages, plus standalone structures such as reworked strongholds, illager-themed buildings and other settlement set-pieces depending on the version and config.

village

Taverns

A social hub building that generates within villages — decorative, occasionally with a chest or two and a bit of atmosphere. Worth a peek when you pass through.

structure

Stronghold additions

The mod expands or adds stronghold-style structures, giving you more fortified places to raid beyond the vanilla/YUNG's set.

structure

Illager & outpost builds

Hostile humanoid set-pieces that flesh out the overworld between the bigger landmarks. Light defence, light loot.

Treat Dungeons and Taverns as opportunistic exploration. You will rarely set out specifically to find a tavern, but they reward the habit of actually walking into every village and checking every unfamiliar building. The loot is modest compared to a When Dungeons Arise keep, but the structures are low-risk and frequently right on your path.

Good filler between big runs

When you are travelling between two major targets, the taverns, outposts and minor structures from this mod (and YUNG's Extras) are the snacks along the way — quick to clear, no real risk, and they keep a long overland trek from feeling empty.

05 — Folk of the deep

Goblin Traders

By MrCrayfish, Goblin Traders sprinkles friendly goblin merchants into the underground. Unlike a villager, a goblin spawns out in the caves — you stumble across one in a torch-lit pocket while mining — and offers a rotating set of unusual trades for emeralds and assorted items. They are a genuinely handy mid-game shopping source for things that are otherwise a pain to gather.

There are two flavours. The ordinary Goblin Trader is peaceful and will deal with you; the rarer, hostile Vein Goblin spawns deeper down, will not trade, and would rather rob you — kill it for drops, or avoid it. Both are part of the same mod's underground ecosystem.

Trading etiquette

Goblin trades are quirky by design — prices and stock shift, and some deals are clearly better than others. Don't hit a peaceful Goblin Trader; if provoked it can turn on you or flee. Keep a small emerald reserve while exploring so you can buy on the spot when a good trade comes up, because the same goblin may not be there when you return.

Mind the Vein Goblins

A Vein Goblin is the hostile cousin — it spawns in the deep dark of the caves and is an enemy, not a shop. Engage it on your terms or back off; it is not worth dying to on a casual mining run.

06 — Where the bosses sleep

Boss Lairs to Find

Some of the pack's bosses don't roam — they wait in rare, fixed lairs you have to seek out. Mowzie's Mobs (by bobmowzie) is the headline here: alongside its roaming creatures it generates a handful of dedicated arenas and lairs that house its bosses. This section is about finding the lairs — the fights themselves (movesets, phases, drops) belong in the Mobs & Adventure guide, which covers Mowzie's combat in full.

Mowzie's Mobs — boss lairs & where to hunt them
BossLair / structureHunt in
Ferrous WroughtnautA sealed underground barrow / chamberUnderground; look for its distinctive stone tomb
FrostmawAn icy cave lair with a frozen entranceSnowy mountains & frozen biomes
Umvuthi (the Sun bird) / UmvuthanaAn open-air shrine / arena with its tribeSavanna-type biomes
Don't wake one by accident

Several Mowzie's bosses are severe-to-deadly fights with mechanics you can't brute-force. If you find a barrow or a frozen lair while underprepared, mark it and leave. Come back with the strategy from the Mobs & Adventure guide, proper potions and a bow.

The other lair-holders (covered elsewhere)

Two more structure-and-boss families have their own dedicated journals. Find their lairs the same way — scout, mark, return prepared — but read the fights there:

  • Cataclysm — its bosses sit in their own ruined temples and abyssal structures (the sunken cities, the ancient remnants). The Cataclysm guide covers how to reach and beat each one.
  • Twilight Forest — an entire dimension built as a structure-gated boss ladder (Naga courtyard, Lich tower, and onward). The Twilight Forest guide is the map for that progression.
Lairs are landmarks too

Even if you're not ready to fight, a discovered lair is a useful waypoint: it fixes a biome, often sits near other structures, and gives you a clear "graduation" goal to gear toward. Treat the boss as the boss-fight you schedule, not the ambush you blunder into.

07 — Provisioning

Loot & Gearing — What to Bring

Exploration in this pack is a gear race: the structures pay out in equipment, but the dangerous ones demand equipment to survive. The combat and crafting systems below each have their own guide — this is just the field summary of what to carry on the road.

Where your combat gear comes from

weapons & tools

Tinkers' Construct

Build and repair modular tools/weapons from parts and traits. Your everyday road kit — a sturdy, modifiable weapon and a pick that won't break mid-dungeon.

magic

Iron's Spells 'n Spellbooks

Ranged and utility magic — fireballs for clearing rooms, healing and mobility for the deep delves. Pack a focus and some mana support.

affixes

Apotheosis

The affix/gem/socket system that makes structure loot worth grabbing. Salvage junk affix gear; socket gems into your keepers before a big run.

The road kit

A sensible loadout before you set out to clear anything rated severe or worse:

ArmourBest affixed/enchanted set you have, repairedThe deadly structures punish chip damage

What you'll find (qualitatively)

  • Gear & enchanted books — the bread and butter; YUNG's, When Dungeons Arise and Dungeon Crawl all drop equipment worth feeding into Apotheosis.
  • Materials & emeralds — ores, ingots, building blocks and trade currency, heaviest in the deeper Dungeon Crawl floors and the bigger landmarks.
  • Unique & decorative blocks — When Dungeons Arise structures in particular are worth visiting just for their palettes.
  • Boss drops — the lair bosses (Mowzie's, and the cross-referenced Cataclysm/Twilight Forest bosses) gate the strongest rewards behind a real fight.
Loot specifics are approximate

Exact chest contents, drop rates and trade pools depend on the modpack's config, the mod versions and your world seed. Everything above is described qualitatively on purpose — the in-game chests and the mod wikis are the only ground truth.

08 — The route

The Exploration Checklist

A surveyor works in passes, not in panic. Here is a rough progression that keeps your reach matched to your gear — explore upward in danger as your equipment improves.

  1. Set up your tools. Install and learn the map mod and Nature's Compass (QoL guide). Make a habit of waypointing everything you spot.
  2. Early — the mild tier. Better Witch Huts, YUNG's Extras, Dungeons and Taverns builds, surface Better Dungeons. Low risk, learn the loot tables, build an emerald reserve from Goblin Traders.
  3. Mid — the moderate tier. Better Mineshafts, deeper Better Dungeons, Better Jungle Temples, the upper floors of a Dungeon Crawl. Start feeding loot into Apotheosis.
  4. Gear up. Lock in a Tinkers' weapon, an affixed armour set, and a magic option from Iron's Spells before pushing the severe tier.
  5. Severe — the landmarks. When Dungeons Arise keeps & towers, Better Desert Temples, Better Ocean Monuments, Seven Seas pirate ships, the deep Dungeon Crawl floors.
  6. Deadly — the fortress & the lairs. YUNG's Better Nether Fortress, Mowzie's boss lairs, and (via their own guides) the Cataclysm and Twilight Forest bosses. Bring everything.

Route advice

scout first

Mark, then commit

Discovering a structure and clearing it are two trips. Waypoint it, gauge the threat at the door, and come back at the right gear tier.

biome-chain

Hunt by biome

Use Nature's Compass to chain targets: a desert run nets temples and mineshafts; an ocean voyage nets monuments and Seven Seas ships.

light & seal

Own the dark

Every severe structure is lost the moment mobs respawn behind you. Light as you go, seal entrances, clear in wings.

extract

Have an exit plan

Carry an ender chest or shulker, a water bucket and spare food. The goal is to leave with the loot, not to die on the deepest floor.

One last word from the margin

This pack rewards curiosity over efficiency. The map fills in the more you walk it — every keep on a ridge, every staircase in the dark and every goblin in a torch-lit pocket is a line in the journal. Go slow, mark everything, and gear into the danger rather than against it. Safe travels, surveyor.
