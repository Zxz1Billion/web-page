The Pattern Book

A catalogue of things to build, drawn in 3D so you can see the shape before you place a block. Nineteen structures across four jobs: somewhere to sleep, somewhere to work, power and water, and the works that tie a base together. Each card gives the footprint, the key materials, and what it's best for. Pick one and copy the massing; dress the detail with The Architect.

01 — Somewhere to sleep

Shelters & homes

Start with a roof and four walls, then grow. A lean-to survives night one; a cabin or hip-roof house carries you for seasons; a homestead, longhouse or watchtower is where a settled base goes.

Lean-to shelter

Day oneTemporary
Footprint6×4
Key materialsLogs, sticks, dry grass
Best forA roof over your head on night one

A back wall, two posts, one sloped roof. Buys you a dry night while you plan the real base.

Log cabin

Starter homeGable roof
Footprint7×6
Key materialsLogs, planks, thatch
Best forYour first permanent base

Solid log walls and a pitched gable roof. Smooth the roof slope with stairs and slabs in-game.

Hip-roof house

CottageHip roof
Footprint7×7
Key materialsCob/plaster, timber, shingles
Best forA tidy weatherproof cottage

A four-sided hip roof sheds rain on every side and overhangs the walls.

Two-storey homestead

Family baseTwo floors
Footprint7×6
Key materialsPlaster, timber frame, shingles
Best forA long-term home with room to grow

Two full floors under one gable. Stack windows to light both levels; chimney serves a ground-floor hearth.

Longhouse

CommunalLong span
Footprint11×5
Key materialsLogs, thatch
Best forLots of indoor space, one roofline

A long footprint under one ridge. Cheap to roof per square, easy to extend along its length.

Watchtower

LookoutDefence
Footprint3×3 × tall
Key materialsStone, timber
Best forSeeing trouble before it arrives

A tapered stone shaft with a crenellated top. Height is the point: light it as a landmark you can run home to.

02 — Somewhere to work

Workshops & production

The buildings that turn raw materials into gear: an open smithy for metal, a charcoal pit for fuel, a kiln for pottery, a bloomery for iron, a greenhouse for winter crops.

Open smithy

ForgeOpen shed
Footprint7×6
Key materialsPosts, shingles, stone
Best forWorking metal without a stuffy room

Four posts, a shed roof, a stone forge and an anvil. Open sides vent the heat and smoke.

Charcoal pit

FuelSmoulder
Footprint5×5 mound
Key materialsFirewood, soil
Best forTurning stacked wood into charcoal

A cone of firewood buried under soil so it smoulders instead of burning. Charcoal feeds every furnace past the forge.

Pottery kiln

FiringPottery
Footprint4×4 round
Key materialsClay bricks
Best forHard-firing pots, crucibles and molds

A round clay chamber with a domed top and a stoke hole at the base. Fire your formed clay here before it holds water or metal.

Bloomery stack

IronSmelt
Footprint2×2 stack
Key materialsFire clay, bellows
Best forSmelting iron ore into a bloom

A tall, narrow clay chimney. Layer ore and charcoal, work the bellows, and tap a spongy iron bloom from the base.

Greenhouse

FarmingGlass
Footprint7×5
Key materialsStone sill, glass panes
Best forGrowing crops through a hard winter

Glass walls and roof over a low stone sill. Traps warmth so tender crops keep going when the ground outside freezes.

03 — Power & water

Power & water

Automate the grind and secure your water. A windmill catches the wind, a waterwheel takes a stream, a covered well keeps the rain out of your drinking supply.

Windmill

PowerSails
Footprint4×4 × tall
Key materialsStone, cloth sails, gears
Best forFree rotation to grind, pulverise and hammer

A tapered tower with four sail blades. Put it high and in the open; wind strength climbs with altitude.

Waterwheel mill

PowerFlowing water
Footprint5×4 + channel
Key materialsTimber, gears
Best forSteady power day and night from a stream

A vertical wheel in flowing water drives the mill beside it. Unlike wind, the current never drops.

Covered well

WaterUtility
Footprint3×3
Key materialsStone, timber, rope
Best forClean water at the doorstep

A stone ring, two posts and a little gable to keep rain and leaves out. Dig down to the water table first.

04 — Tying it together

Land & infrastructure

The works around the buildings: a barn and paddock for animals, a granary that keeps grain dry, a bridge across the river, a gate in the wall, a jetty out to deep water.

Barn & paddock

LivestockStorage
Footprint7×6 + pen
Key materialsLogs, thatch, fencing
Best forHousing animals and feed

A big door for hauling, a steep roof for hay, and a fenced paddock alongside for grazing animals.

Granary on stilts

StorageDry
Footprint5×5 raised
Key materialsLogs, thatch
Best forKeeping grain dry and away from vermin

Raised on posts so damp and animals can't reach the grain. A ladder is the only way up.

Stone-pier bridge

CrossingStone
Footprint12 span
Key materialsStone piers, timber deck
Best forCrossing a river or ravine dry-shod

Two stone piers carry a timber deck with railings. Sink the piers to the riverbed so the span sits clear of the water.

Palisade gate

DefenceWall
Footprint10 wall + gate
Key materialsLogs
Best forWalling a base against drifters and wolves

A pointed-top log wall broken by a gatehouse. Keeps night creatures out of your compound.

Jetty & dock

WaterAccess
Footprint7 reach
Key materialsPosts, planks
Best forReaching deep water or mooring a boat

Planks on posts walking out over the shallows. Stand at the end to fish deeper water or tie up a boat.
