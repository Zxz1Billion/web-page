The Pattern Book

A catalogue of things to build, drawn in 3D so you can see the shape before you place a block. 62 structures across ten themes: shelters, homes, grand stone, workshops, furnaces, farming, power, defence, crossings and mining. Each card gives the footprint, the key materials and what it's best for. Pick one, copy the massing, then dress the detail with The Architect.

01 — Night one

Quick shelters

Get a roof over your head fast. None of these are pretty, but each one survives a night while you scout the spot for something permanent.

Lean-to shelter

Day oneTemporary
Footprint6×4
Key materialsLogs, sticks, dry grass
Best forA roof over your head on night one

A back wall, two posts, one sloped roof. Buys a dry night while you plan the real base.

Earth dugout

CheapWarm
Footprint6×4
Key materialsDug soil, a door, a chimney
Best forA warm hole when wood is scarce

Cut into a bank and frame a door. Soil insulates, so it stays warmer than a timber hut in deep cold.

A-frame

SimpleSheds rain
Footprint4×6
Key materialsLogs, thatch
Best forA steep roof that needs no walls

The roof is the building. Steep sides shed rain and snow; the gable ends hold a door and a vent.

Hide tent

NomadPack up
Footprint4×4 round
Key materialsPoles, hides
Best forA movable camp while exploring

A cone of hides on poles. Light, quick, and you can take it with you when you move on.

Roundhouse

CozyThatch cone
Footprint5×5 round
Key materialsCob/plaster, thatch
Best forA snug single room

Round cob walls under a conical thatch roof. No corners to draught, cheap to raise.

Windbreak camp

First minutesBedroll
Footprint5×3
Key materialsA wall, a fire, a bedroll
Best forStopping for the night on the move

A low wall to block the wind, a fire ring and a bedroll. The absolute minimum that counts as 'camp'.

Stilt house

Three storeys on a small footprint. Stack the windows and run a chimney up the gable end.

05 — Fire & ore

Furnaces & industry

The hot end of the tech tree: charcoal pits, kilns, the bloomery, a blast furnace, a cementation furnace and a clay quarry.

Charcoal pit

FuelSmoulder
Footprint5×5 mound
Key materialsFirewood, soil
Best forTurning stacked wood into charcoal

A cone of firewood buried under soil so it smoulders, not burns. Charcoal feeds every furnace past the forge.

Pottery kiln

FiringPottery
Footprint4×4 round
Key materialsClay bricks
Best forHard-firing pots, crucibles and molds

A round clay chamber with a domed top and a stoke hole. Fire your formed clay here before it holds water or metal.

Bloomery stack

IronSmelt
Footprint2×2 stack
Key materialsFire clay, bellows
Best forSmelting iron ore into a bloom

A tall, narrow clay chimney. Layer ore and charcoal, work the bellows, tap a spongy bloom from the base.

Blast furnace

IronScaffold
Footprint3×3 stack + frame
Key materialsStone, timber, bellows
Best forSmelting iron in bigger batches

A tall stone stack charged from a timber platform. A tap arch at the base lets the metal out.

Cementation furnace

SteelChimney
Footprint5×4 + flue
Key materialsStone, fire clay
Best forCarburising iron into blister steel

A sealed furnace house under a tall flue. It runs for days, soaking iron bars in charcoal to make steel.

Clay quarry

ClayExcavation
Footprint8×6 pit
Key materialsDug clay, a ladder
Best forDigging clay, gravel and stone in bulk

A stepped pit worked down in benches with a ladder and a barrow. Clay is the base of pottery and cob.
