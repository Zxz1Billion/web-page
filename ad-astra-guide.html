Ad Astra
Per Aspera

The headline act of To the Moon 2.0 is, of course, leaving it. Ad Astra bolts a whole space program onto Minecraft — a metal-and-plate tech chain, oxygen and temperature survival, fuel refining, and Tier 1–4 rockets that carry you from the launch pad to the Moon, Mars, Venus, Mercury, and the frozen world of Glacio. This manual walks the full arc: from your first Coal Generator to a sealed, oxygenated base on another planet.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Ad Astra is a destination mod. Everything you build on the ground exists to get you off it — and then to keep you alive once you arrive somewhere that wants you dead. Two systems run the whole show: a tech chain (ore → plate → machine → rocket) and a survival layer (oxygen + temperature).

Three rules describe almost every hazard you'll meet out there. Internalise them before you spend a single ingot:

breathe

Oxygen

Off-world atmospheres are unbreathable. Without an Oxygen Tank on your back or an Oxygen Distributor sealing your base, your air bar drains and you suffocate.

temperature

Heat & Cold

Venus and Mercury cook you; Glacio freezes you. A bare-skinned visit deals continuous damage. The right Space Suit tier shrugs it off.

gravity

Gravity

Low-gravity worlds (the Moon especially) send you floating on every jump. Useful for traversal, dangerous near a cliff or a launch.

The places you can go

Ad Astra ships a compact solar system plus orbital space stations. Each body is gated behind a higher rocket tier and demands more of your survival kit:

  • Earth — home. Plus an orbital Earth space station you build and dock at.
  • Moon — the first hop (Tier 1). Low gravity, no air, source of Desh and Cheese.
  • Mars — a thin, hostile atmosphere; home of Ostrum.
  • Venus — searing heat and acid rain; a Calorite heat-tier world.
  • Mercury — brutal sun-side heat, a barren resource run.
  • Glacio — the frozen, distant end-tier world; the toughest cold survival.
The shortest path to space

Build a Coal Generator for power, a Compressor to make plates, a NASA Workbench to assemble the rocket, and an Oxygen Loader + tank to breathe. That quartet gets you a Tier 1 rocket and a one-way ticket to the Moon — bring a return supply of fuel.

01 — On the ground

Early Machines & Power

Ad Astra runs on its own energy system and its own set of metals. The early game is a short, satisfying ladder: generate power, press metal into plates, and refine oil into rocket fuel. Build these and you've built a space program in miniature.

Steel & the metal tiers

Before any of this you need Steel. Ad Astra's Steel is crafted by compressing/smelting iron (an Iron Plate route through the Compressor and smelting), and it's the backbone of every early machine and the Tier 1 rocket. Above Steel sit the planet-tier metals, each mined on a world that the previous rocket tier can reach:

Etrium is the energy-grade metal — it shows up in cables, the Energizer, and Etrionic blocks (see §06). Each metal is pressed into a plate in the Compressor before it goes into anything structural.

Plates first, always

Almost nothing structural takes raw ingots. The pattern is ore → ingot → plate (Compressor) → part. When a recipe stalls, the missing reagent is usually a plate you forgot to press. Keep a stack of every plate tier on hand.

02 — Liftoff

Building a Rocket

Rockets are assembled in the NASA Workbench from four physical parts plus a fuel charge. Each rocket comes in Tier 1 through Tier 4, and the tier determines how far you can travel — and which plate metal the parts demand.

The anatomy of a rocket

top

Nose Cone

The aerodynamic tip. Crafted from the tier's plate metal.

core

Body / Fuel Tank

The hull and fuel reservoir. The bulk of the plates go here.

stability

Fins

The fins at the base that keep the craft upright.

thrust

Engine

The motor at the very bottom. Higher tiers carry you farther.

From parts to launch

  1. Press the plates. Run your tier's ingots through the Compressor to make the plate stacks each part needs.
  2. Craft the parts. Build the Nose Cone, Body, Fins, and Engine for that tier.
  3. Assemble in the NASA Workbench. Slot the four parts into their positions; the workbench shows the completed rocket and outputs it as a placeable item.
  4. Place the rocket on flat, open ground — it acts as its own launch pad.
  5. Refine fuel in the Fuel Refinery (oil → fuel) and load it into the rocket with a fuel container until the tank is full.
  6. Board and launch. Right-click to enter, hold the launch key to count down, then pick a destination from the map screen.

Launch site layout

One-way until you pack a return

Your rocket is consumed on landing — you arrive on foot. To get home you must bring the means to build and fuel another rocket, or pre-stage fuel and parts. Stranding yourself fuel-less on Mars is the classic Ad Astra death.

03 — Staying alive

Life Support

The moment your rocket touches down on an airless world, the clock starts. Two systems keep you breathing: carried oxygen (tanks + suit) for moving around, and sealed atmosphere (the Oxygen Distributor) for living in a base.

Carried oxygen — tanks & suits

An Oxygen Tank stores breathable air; the Oxygen Loader fills it from water + power before you leave. Worn with a Space Suit, the tank feeds your air bar. The suit tiers also handle temperature and, at the top, flight:

Space Suit tiers
SuitOxygenTemperatureExtra
Space SuitHolds tank airBasic protectionThe standard kit for the Moon & Mars.
Netherite Space SuitLarger capacityStrongerTougher, more durable mid-tier armour.
Jet SuitLargest capacityBestPowered flight — the endgame suit for hostile worlds.
Wear all four pieces

Oxygen and temperature protection only apply when the full suit set is equipped. A missing boot means a draining air bar — Ad Astra checks the whole armour set, not just the chestplate.

Sealed atmosphere — the Oxygen Distributor

For a permanent off-world home you don't want to babysit a tank. The Oxygen Distributor floods a fully enclosed room with breathable air, creating a pocket of atmosphere you can walk around in helmet-off. The rules:

  • The room must be completely sealed — no open doorways or gaps, or the oxygen leaks and the seal fails.
  • The distributor needs power and an oxygen supply (piped O₂ or its own loading) to maintain the bubble.
  • Bigger sealed volumes need more oxygen throughput; the distributor has a coverage limit per unit.

Pair it with a Cryo Freezer for water/ice handling where liquid water is scarce, so your Oxygen Loader and distributor never run dry.

Seal discipline

Use airlocks (a two-door buffer) for entrances so opening a door doesn't dump your whole atmosphere. One unsealed block and the distributor stops counting the room as airtight — and your colonists start suffocating.

The Worlds

Each body has its own gravity, atmosphere, temperature hazard, signature resource, and natives. Plan your kit around the column that will hurt you most — for the Moon that's oxygen, for Venus it's heat, for Glacio it's cold.

Orbit & space stations

Beyond the surfaces, you can launch into orbit and assemble a Space Station from its component blocks — a buildable platform that floats in the void. Solar power is strongest off-world (no atmosphere, no night-cycle dimming on some bodies), so orbit makes a fine power and staging hub.

Read the hazard before you pack

The hostile column dictates your suit. The Moon & Mars are oxygen trips (a basic Space Suit suffices). Venus and Mercury are heat trips — bring the highest temperature-rated suit and watch for acid rain on Venus. Glacio is a cold trip and the stiffest survival test in the mod.

05 — Off-world haul

Planet Resources & Ores

Every world you unlock supplies the metal that builds the next rocket. The loop is self-propelling: land, mine the local ore, press it into plates, build the higher-tier rocket, fly farther. Here's what each world is worth carrying home.

The ore → plate → rocket chain

Stripped to its bones, the entire mod is one repeating pipeline. Memorise it once and every tier reads the same:

  1. Mine the planet's ore (Desh, Ostrum, Calorite…).
  2. Smelt it to an ingot.
  3. Compress the ingot into a Plate.
  4. Craft the rocket parts (nose, body, fins, engine) from those plates.
  5. Assemble & fuel the next-tier rocket — and go one world farther.
Bring the press with you

Setting up a small off-world base with a Compressor and a power source means you can mine, press, and even assemble the next rocket on site — no hauling raw ore back through a return trip you might not be able to afford.

06 — Power grid

Energy & Cabling

Ad Astra has its own energy machines and cables to wire them together. Power flows from generators, through cables, into machines and the Energizer for buffering. In a kitchen-sink pack like To the Moon 2.0 it also coexists with the other big energy systems.

Power sources & carriers
BlockRoleNotes
Coal GeneratorGenerationThe reliable bootstrap; burns coal/fuel for energy.
Solar PanelGenerationStronger off-world — vacuum & clear skies boost output.
Cables (Etrionic)TransmissionCarry energy between generators, machines & the Energizer.
EnergizerStorage / bufferBanks energy so demand spikes don't stall your machines.
Off-world solar wins

Plant a field of Solar Panels on the Moon or in orbit and let them feed an Energizer. With no clouds and a hard vacuum, off-world solar far outpaces the same array back on Earth — ideal for a remote base that has to run an Oxygen Distributor around the clock.

Cross-mod energy

Because the pack bundles other tech mods, Ad Astra's energy generally interoperates with the broader Forge Energy economy, and converters/bridges may let you feed Ad Astra machines from a Mekanism or generic-FE grid. Treat the exact compatibility as pack-specific — check JEI for converter recipes rather than assuming.

07 — Getting around

Vehicles

Walking a low-gravity world in a bulky suit is slow. Ad Astra gives you two ways to cover ground fast: the drivable Rover and, at the top of the suit tree, personal flight.

drive

Rover

A planet-surface buggy you place, fuel, and drive. Seats you (and a passenger), handles rough terrain, and turns a long Desh-mining trek into a quick loop.

fly

Jet Suit Flight

The Jet Suit grants powered flight — the cleanest way to cross Venus's acid plains or Glacio's ice fields without touching the hazardous ground.

Fuelling the Rover

The Rover runs on the same fuel economy as your rockets — refine oil in the Fuel Refinery and top up the vehicle's tank. Keep a fuel can in your inventory for refills in the field, and remember the Rover is recoverable: pick it back up when you're done rather than abandoning it on the surface.

Suit up before you board

A vehicle doesn't supply oxygen or temperature protection on its own — you still need your full Space Suit and a charged Oxygen Tank while driving an airless world. The Rover moves you faster; it doesn't keep you breathing.

Survival Checklist

Setting up an off-world base

  1. Seal a shelter. Wall off a small airtight room the moment you land — somewhere to refill and breathe.
  2. Power it. Drop a Solar Panel field (off-world solar is strong) into an Energizer.
  3. Pressurise. Run the Oxygen Distributor; confirm the room seals and the air bubble forms.
  4. Mine & press. Gather the local metal and Compress it into plates for the next-tier rocket.
  5. Stage your return. Build and fuel the homeward (or onward) rocket before you run low on supplies.
Fuel is the lifeline

The single most common way to die in Ad Astra isn't a mob — it's stranding. Always know how you're getting home before you leave the launch pad. If in doubt, bring a full extra fuel load and the parts for a spare rocket.

09 — Field notes

Gotchas & Tips

The small things that trip up first-time astronauts. None of these are obvious from the recipe book — they're learned the hard way, so learn them here instead.

  • Oxygen needs the whole set. Three pieces of suit and one vanilla boot equals a draining air bar. Equip all four.
  • Distributor rooms must be airtight. A single open block, an ungated doorway, or a gap behind a torch breaks the seal. Build airlocks.
  • The rocket is consumed on launch/landing. Plan your return trip's parts and fuel before you go anywhere.
  • Refuel the rocket after assembly. A freshly built rocket is empty — load fuel into its tank or the launch does nothing.
  • Heat worlds need a heat suit. A basic Space Suit will cook on Venus and Mercury; bring the temperature-rated tier.
  • Acid rain on Venus damages you in the open — stay sheltered or fly over it in a Jet Suit.
  • Low gravity is a hazard near launch. A mistimed jump on the Moon can drift you somewhere you didn't intend; mind ledges.
  • Press, don't smelt-and-pray. Structural recipes want plates from the Compressor, not raw ingots — check JEI when a craft greys out.
JEI is your co-pilot

Every value in this guide that says "approximate" can be confirmed precisely in-game. Use JEI to trace any recipe back to its raw ore, and read the live machine GUIs for energy and fuel rates — they're the final word over any wiki or guide.

★ — The path

Progression — to the Moon and beyond

Ad Astra is a ladder where every rung is a world: each planet hands you the metal that builds the rocket for the next one. This is the full arc threaded through the sections above — what to build, what to mine, and why — from your first lump of Steel to a sealed colony on frozen Glacio. For where Ad Astra sits in the wider modpack run, see the pack roadmap, The Ascent.

  1. Bootstrap on the ground (early). Smelt iron and craft Steel, then stand up the starter quartet from §01: a Coal Generator for power, a Compressor to press ingots into plates, a Fuel Refinery to turn oil into rocket fuel, and a NASA Workbench to assemble rockets. Steel is the backbone of every early machine and the Tier 1 hull.
  2. Kit out to breathe (early). Build an Oxygen Loader and fill a couple of Oxygen Tanks (needs water + power), and craft a full Space Suit set. Off-world atmospheres are unbreathable and the suit only protects when all four pieces are worn — see §03.
  3. First hop to the Moon (early→mid). Press Steel plates, build the four rocket parts plus a fuel charge into a Tier 1 rocket, place it on a clear pad, fuel it, and launch. Land on the Moon, mine Desh (and grab some Cheese), and bring the means to fuel a return — the rocket is consumed on landing.
  4. Climb the metal ladder to Mars (mid). Compress Desh into plates for a Tier 2 rocket and fly to Mars, source of Ostrum and Ice Shards. Bring a Compressor and a power source so you can mine and press on site rather than betting on a return haul.
  5. Push to the heat worlds (mid→late). Press Ostrum into Tier 3 parts and head for Venus and Mercury to mine Calorite. These are heat trips: bring the highest temperature-rated suit, watch for acid rain on Venus, and consider a Jet Suit to fly over the hazardous ground.
  6. Reach the end of the system (late→endgame). Compress Calorite into Tier 4 plates for the top rocket and make the cold run to Glacio, the stiffest survival test in the mod. By now you're running a Jet Suit with the largest tank and best temperature protection.
  7. Go permanent (endgame). Stop touring and settle in: seal rooms with an Oxygen Distributor (use airlocks), feed it from an off-world Solar Panel field into an Energizer over Etrionic cabling, and assemble an orbital Space Station as a power and staging hub. With self-sufficient bases you stop ferrying everything from Earth.
Pack tip

Treat the rocket tiers as a checklist, not a grind: each world you unlock supplies the plate metal for the next jump, so never leave a body until you've banked enough of its ore to build the rocket that takes you onward. In To the Moon 2.0, Ad Astra is the headline payoff at the top of the pack's arc — pair this ladder with The Ascent to see how your wider tech and resource base feeds each launch. And remember the golden rule from §08: know how you're getting home before you leave the pad.
