The Twilight
Atlas

A traveller's account of the Twilight Forest — a whole world hung in perpetual dusk, reached by drowning a diamond in a flowered pool. It is no sandbox to roam at will: it is a locked road. Each great beast you fell breaks a shimmering ward and opens the path to the next. This atlas walks that road from the Naga's coils to the Snow Queen's frozen throne, and catalogues every dungeon, biome, and treasure between.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

The Twilight Forest is a self-contained dimension — its own world, reached through a portal you build in the overworld. It is eternally twilight: never quite night, never quite day, and crowded with strange wood, hollow hills, and dungeons full of teeth.

The single most important thing to understand before you step through: this dimension is gated. The mod ships a Progression system that magically shields most bosses and many areas until you have defeated the boss before them. You will literally bounce off invisible barriers or take damage trying to enter a dungeon you haven't earned. So the whole adventure is a ladder, climbed one rung at a time.

where

A separate world

The Forest is its own dimension, like the Nether. You travel there and back through a portal; what you build at home stays at home.

when

Perpetual dusk

It is always dim. Hostile mobs spawn freely on the surface, so light your camps and keep moving with intent.

how

Earned, not explored

Progression Advancements unlock each new boss and region. Beat the prerequisite, the ward drops, the next door opens.

The shape of the journey

  • Build & light the portal — a flowered water pool plus a thrown diamond (§01).
  • Climb the boss ladder — Naga → Lich → mid-bosses → Knight Phantoms → Ur-Ghast → Alpha Yeti → Snow Queen (§02).
  • Loot the materials & magic — each boss and dungeon hands you the gear that makes the next fight survivable (§07).
  • Gear up between fights at home — this pack hands you Mekanism, Tinkers and Blood Magic; use them (§09).
Your in-game compass

Keep an eye on the Advancements screen. The Twilight Forest tab spells out, in order, which boss unlocks what — it is the canonical, always-correct version of the ladder this atlas describes. When in doubt, trust the game.

01 — The threshold

The Portal & First Steps

Getting in is a small ritual. You build a shallow natural-looking pool, ring it with growing things, and offer a diamond to the water. The Forest answers with lightning.

Build & light the portal

  1. Dig a 2×2 pool and fill it with water — a simple flush pool, source blocks all the way.
  2. Ring it with nature. Surround the water's edge with flowers, grass, saplings, mushrooms or similar growing blocks. The portal wants to be born in something green.
  3. Throw a diamond in. Drop (Q) a Diamond directly into the water — don't place it, toss it. A bolt of lightning strikes, the water churns, and the pool becomes a Twilight Forest portal.
  4. Step in to be carried to the dimension. The same portal at the far end carries you home — and you can always make a fresh return pool out there if you wander far.
Returning home

You exit the Forest the same way you entered: stand in a portal pool. If you're lost on the far side, just build a new 2×2 flowered pool and diamond it — every portal links back to the overworld near where you left.

First dangers

The early Forest is not subtle about wanting you dead. Among the welcoming committee:

cave

Kobolds

Small, fast, swarming gremlins that pour out of Hollow Hills. Individually weak, dangerous in a pack.

cap

Redcaps

Pickaxe-swinging miners that mine toward you and lay TNT-like traps. Don't let them dig under your feet.

druid

Skeleton Druids

Robed skeletons that fling potions and summon swarms. Close the distance fast or break line of sight.

Finding your bearings

The landscape is studded with landmarks even before the bosses. You'll quickly meet:

  • Hollow Hills — grassy domes hollow inside, packed with ores, treasure, and the aforementioned Kobolds. Small, medium and large variants, each richer than the last.
  • Hedge Mazes — sprawling labyrinths of dense hedge that hurts to push through; they hide chests at the heart.
  • Mazestone — the patterned stone that marks dungeon structures. Seeing it is a sign you've found something built, not grown.
  • Hollow Trees — enormous trunks you can climb or hollow out; some make ready-made bases.
Don't dive in underdressed

Arrive with at least iron gear, food, and torches. The Forest punishes the careless, and your very first goal — the Naga — wants you in solid armour before you tangle with it.

02 — The locked road

The Progression Ladder

Here is the spine of the whole mod. Each boss drops a ward elsewhere in the world, recorded as a Progression Advancement. You cannot meaningfully skip ahead — the later dungeons stay shielded (and will hurt or repel you) until the prerequisite is dead. Read this table top-to-bottom as your route.

The mid-bosses sit side by side

The Minoshroom (Labyrinth) and the Hydra (Fire Swamp) both open after the Lich and both feed toward the Goblin Knight Stronghold. Tackle them in whichever order you like, but expect to want gear from both before facing the Knight Phantoms.

How the ward actually works

Picture each region behind glass. The glass is real: walk into a still-shielded dungeon and you'll be shoved back or singed. The instant the prerequisite boss dies, the matching Progression Advancement fires and that glass shatters — quietly, world-wide. Then, and only then, can you fight what's inside.

The Highlands and the unfinished Final Castle on the Plateau are the far end of the line. They contain placeholder and forgotten content — the road runs out before it truly ends, which is part of the Forest's lore: a story still being written.

03 — The first two rungs

The Naga & the Twilight Lich

The Naga

Your first wall. The Naga is a giant serpent that circles its Naga Courtyard — a ring of mossy Naga Stone pillars. It charges, body-slams, and is too fast to simply out-run.

  • Tactic: use the pillars. The Naga can smash through some terrain, so duck behind the courtyard's stone to break its charges, then strike as it passes. Bring a bow and plenty of food.
  • Drop: Naga Scales, which craft into Naga Scale Armor — a light, durable early set that's a clear upgrade over iron and your first proper Forest gear.
  • Unlocks: the Naga's death opens the way to the Lich Tower.

The Twilight Lich

The Twilight Lich waits atop the Lich Tower, a tall dark spire. It is a three-phase fight and the first real test of your reflexes:

The Lich's three phases
PhaseWhat it doesHow to answer
Shield & boltsSurrounded by a shield, it hurls magic bolts at you.Deflect its own shots back by hitting them — each reflection chips its shield away.
Summon minionsWith its shield gone it summons swarms of minions and teleports.Cut down the adds, keep chasing, don't get cornered on the tower.
MeleeOut of tricks, it closes for a straight melee duel.Trade blows and finish it. This is where it's most vulnerable.

The Lich is generous with rewards — it drops a set of Scepters, the Forest's first taste of magic weaponry:

summon

Zombie Scepter

Summons short-lived zombie minions to fight for you. Great for soaking aggro.

drain

Lifedrain Scepter

Saps life from foes and returns it to you — a portable, charge-based vampiric beam.

guard

Shield Scepter

Grants you temporary protective shields that absorb incoming hits.

The Lich is the keystone

Beating the Lich unlocks both the Minotaur Labyrinth and the Fire Swamp / Hydra. It's the branch point of the whole tree — gear up well here, because the road forks after it.

04 — The fork

The Labyrinth & the Hydra

Two mid-bosses open after the Lich. Both feed toward the Goblin Knight Stronghold; do them in either order.

The Minotaur Labyrinth & Minoshroom

An underground maze of Mazestone sprawling on multiple levels, full of Minotaurs and dead-ends. At its heart, on a lower floor, lurks the Minoshroom — a fungal Minotaur mini-boss.

  • Maze Maps are the key to sanity here: crafted cartography that reveals the labyrinth's layout level by level so you don't wander forever.
  • The Minoshroom drops the Diamond Minotaur Axe — a powerful weapon that hits harder while you're charging.
  • Clearing the labyrinth and the Minoshroom is a step on the road toward the Stronghold.

The Fire Swamp & the Hydra

The Fire Swamp is a hostile, smouldering biome of fire jets and choking spires — you'll want fire resistance just to cross it. Deep within, in its lair, coils the Hydra: a massive, multi-headed serpent boss.

  • Tactic: the Hydra's heads attack independently — biting, breathing fire, and slamming. Stay mobile, punish the heads when they recoil, and keep healing topped up; this is a long, attritional fight.
  • Drops: Fiery Blood and Fiery Tears, used to craft the Fiery material set — armour and tools that set foes alight. The Hydra also yields Hydra Chops, a generous food source.
Bring fire protection

Both of these are fire-themed in part — especially the Fire Swamp. Fire Resistance potions, Fire Protection enchantments, or the Naga/Fiery gear go a long way toward surviving the crossing, never mind the boss.

05 — Goblins & ghasts

Goblin Knight Stronghold & the Dark Tower

Goblin Knight Stronghold & the Knight Phantoms

A fortified warren built of Cracked Stone Bricks and crawling with goblins and armoured knights. Its boss is the Knight Phantoms — a procession of spectral armoured knights you fight in sequence, each appearing as the last falls.

  • Tactic: face them one wave at a time, watch for their lance charges, and use pillars and corners. Patience beats panic here.
  • Drops & spoils: Knightmetal for the Knightly armour and tool set, plus the prized Phantom Helm and Phantom Plate — striking, sturdy armour pieces.
  • Unlocks: clearing the Knight Phantoms opens the road to the Dark Tower.

The Dark Tower & the Ur-Ghast

The Dark Tower is a towering, trap-laden fortress in the Dark Forest, built of Towerwood and riddled with shifting blocks, ghastlings, and devices. You'll need a Tower Key (found within) to open its vaults.

  • The tower swarms with Carminite-charged blocks that change and reform around you — towerwood that vanishes and reappears, ghastlings that harry you in clouds.
  • At the summit broods the Ur-Ghast: a colossal, weeping ghast that rains fireballs and calls lesser ghastlings to its side. Reflect or dodge its barrage, clear the ghastlings, and close in during its vulnerable windows.
  • Its death yields Carminite and tower spoils, and — crucially — drops the ward on the snowy north, opening the path to the Yeti.
Carminite is the tower's signature

The reactive blocks and the Ur-Ghast's loot revolve around Carminite, the substance that makes the Dark Tower shift and breathe. It threads into several of the mod's stranger magic items — keep what you gather.

06 — The frozen north

The Glacier: Alpha Yeti & the Snow Queen

Past the Ur-Ghast the Forest turns to ice. Two cold bosses crown the journey — and the Snow Queen is, for now, the last truly finished fight in the mod.

The Alpha Yeti

Deep in the snowy hills, the Yeti Cave holds the Alpha Yeti — a huge, furious yeti that charges and hurls you into the walls.

  • Tactic: let it charge into the cave walls to stun itself, then punish. Don't stand between it and a wall unless you want to be the thing it slams.
  • Drops: Alpha Yeti Fur, which crafts Yeti Armor — warm, strong gear with handy cold-world perks. You'll also gather Ice Bombs, throwable freezing explosives.
  • Unlocks: the Alpha Yeti's fall opens the Glacier and the Aurora Palace.

The Snow Queen

Atop the Aurora Palace — a luminous structure of shifting Aurora Blocks — reigns the Snow Queen, the final completed boss of the Forest.

  • Tactic: she summons drifting ice clouds and shards and dashes about the throne room. Dodge the falling ice, close in during her grounded moments, and keep your footing on the slick floor.
  • Drops: powerful bows — the Triple Bow (fires three arrows at once) and the Seeker Bow (homing arrows) — among the best ranged weapons the mod offers.
The road thins here

The Snow Queen is the last "complete" boss. Beyond her lie the Highlands, the Thornlands, and the unfinished Final Castle — content that is forgotten or still being built. Reaching her is, in practice, finishing the Twilight Forest.

Magic & utility items

The Forest's charms and gadgets are half its character. A working traveller's kit:

Charm of Keeping

Protects part of your inventory from being dropped on death. A vital safety net before every boss.

safety

Charm of Life

Revives you with health when you'd otherwise die, consuming itself. Carry spares into hard fights.

mining

Ore Magnet

Pulls nearby ores toward you through stone — a treasure-hunter's wand for Hollow Hills.

Crumble / Carminite Builder

Crumble Horn weakens blocks; the Carminite Builder & Reactor manipulate reactive towerwood.

Moonworm Queen

Fires glowing moonworms onto surfaces for light — your renewable torch in the dark.

tame

Transformation Powder

Transforms certain mobs into others — turning hostile creatures into friendlier (or different) ones.

Trophies & pedestals

Every boss drops a Trophy. These aren't mere ornaments: placing boss trophies on the Trophy Pedestals in the Final Castle is part of the endgame gating. Mounted on a wall, they're also the bragging rights for a journey survived.

Scepters & Twilight tools

Beyond armour, keep the Lich's scepters and the boss bows (Triple Bow, Seeker Bow) in rotation — they cover summoning, draining, shielding and ranged work that plain swords can't. The Forest rewards a varied loadout.

Landmarks worth a detour

  • The Quest Ram — a friendly creature you feed wool of each colour. Complete the rainbow and it rewards you handsomely. A charming side-goal you can chip away at the whole journey.
  • Hollow Hills — grassy domes hollow within, ranging small to large, stuffed with ores and treasure (and Kobolds).
  • Hedge Mazes — dense hedge labyrinths hiding chests at the centre.
  • Hollow Trees — colossal hollow trunks, climbable and habitable.
  • Mushgloom — the glowing mushroom mobs of the Mushroom Forest, lighting the fungal canopy.
The Quest Ram is worth it

Hoard sheep and a loom early. Working through the Quest Ram's wool colours over the course of your run is the most pleasant "achievement" the Forest offers, and the payoff is generous.

09 — Hard-won advice

Strategy & Tips

The Forest is designed to be climbed slowly. The single biggest mistake new travellers make is rushing — trying to sprint to the next boss in the gear from the last one.

Gear up before each rung

  • Use each boss's drops before the next fight. Naga Scale before the Lich, Fiery/Knightmetal before the Dark Tower, Yeti gear before the Snow Queen. The loot is balanced to make the next step survivable, not the current one.
  • Carry a Charm of Keeping and a Charm of Life into every boss arena. They are your insurance policy — losing your kit on the floor of a shielded dungeon you then can't re-enter is a special kind of misery.
  • Bring fire resistance for the Fire Swamp and Dark Tower stretch; warmth and footing for the Glacier.

Don't rush — and tech up at home

Because this is a modpack, you don't have to fight fair. To the Moon 2.0 hands you other toolboxes — go use them between bosses:

tech

Mekanism

Power, ore-multiplication and the MekaSuit. See the Osmium Codex — its armour and tools dwarf early Forest gear.

smith

Tinkers' Construct

Build custom weapons tuned to each boss — reach, damage, and modifiers the vanilla toolkit can't match.

arcane

Blood Magic

Sigils, living armour and altars give survivability and utility worth stacking before the harder fights.

Why the gates matter

The progression system isn't an obstacle to route around — it's the structure of the experience. Returning to the overworld to mine, smelt and craft between bosses is not "cheating ahead": the Forest stays locked no matter how strong you get, so you climb the ladder rung by rung with better gear, not by skipping rungs. Pace yourself, and the journey from the Naga to the Snow Queen is one of the most satisfying arcs in modded Minecraft.

Respect the wards

Trying to brute-force into a still-shielded dungeon wastes potions and tempers. If an area repels or burns you, you haven't beaten its prerequisite yet — check the Advancements screen, find the boss you're missing, and go down the ladder first.

★ — The path

Progression — the locked boss ladder, in order

The atlas above explains each rung in depth; this is the route in one place — a strict checklist you climb top to bottom. Every step's ward stays sealed until the boss before it is dead, so work through it in order, and tech up in the overworld between fights.

  1. Build & light the portal. A 2×2 flowered water pool, then toss a diamond in. Gate: entry to the Forest itself. Then: arrive in iron+ gear, food and torches.
  2. Naga. Circle-strafe its courtyard pillars. Unlocks: the Lich Tower. Reward: Naga Scale Armor — your first proper Forest set.
  3. Twilight Lich. Reflect its bolts, clear minions, finish in melee. Unlocks: both the Minotaur Labyrinth and the Fire Swamp. Reward: the Zombie / Lifedrain / Shield scepters.
  4. Minoshroom & Hydra (either order). The two mid-bosses both open after the Lich and both feed toward the Stronghold. Reward: the Diamond Minotaur Axe, plus Fiery Blood & Tears for the Fiery set. Want gear from both before moving on.
  5. Knight Phantoms. Fight the spectral knights one wave at a time in the Goblin Knight Stronghold. Unlocks: the Dark Tower. Reward: Knightmetal (Knightly set), Phantom Helm & Plate.
  6. Ur-Ghast. Climb the trap-laden Dark Tower (Tower Key needed), then reflect its fireball barrage at the summit. Unlocks: the snowy north / Yeti Cave. Reward: Carminite and tower spoils.
  7. Alpha Yeti. Bait its charges into the cave walls to stun it. Unlocks: the Glacier & Aurora Palace. Reward: Yeti Armor and Ice Bombs.
  8. Snow Queen → Highlands / Final Castle. Dodge her ice clouds in the Aurora Palace; she is the last finished boss. Reward: the Triple Bow and Seeker Bow. Beyond: the Highlands and the unfinished Final Castle — the road's far, forgotten end.
You can't skip ahead

The wards are real — a still-shielded dungeon will repel or burn you until its prerequisite is dead. So don't sprint the ladder; pause between rungs and tech up at home, where this pack hands you Tinkers, Mekanism and Apotheosis. For that overworld arc see The Ascent, and if you crave bosses on the same scale outside the Forest, Cataclysm is the natural next climb.
