The First Day

You wake with empty hands in a cold, gorgeous wilderness. There is no crafting table, no punching trees into planks, and the night is genuinely dangerous. This is the calm, exact walkthrough from spawn to your first sunrise — knap a knife, make fire, eat something that won't kill you, and wall yourself in before dark. Do these eight things and Day Two is yours.

Survival start Standard world Commoner class 🌙 Night is lethal — plan for it

00 — Orientation

What This Game Is

Vintage Story looks like a voxel survival game and shares the grid, but it plays like a patient simulation of the Stone Age clawing its way toward the Iron Age. If you arrive expecting Minecraft, three differences will catch you out on the first day.

slower

No instant tools

You don't punch a tree into planks. You knap stone into a blade, lash it to a stick, and only then can you cut and chop. The first tool is a minigame, not a recipe.

hungrier

Nutrition, not just hunger

You have a saturation bar and separate nutrition for fruit, vegetable, protein, grain and dairy. Eating one food group forever leaves you frail — variety is health (see The Larder).

stranger

Temporal stability

Reality itself has integrity. Deep underground and during temporal storms, stability falls and monsters called drifters pour in. The surface is mostly safe — at first.

The golden rule of Day One

Do not dig a deep mine and do not wander far. Your only jobs today are a knife, an axe, a fire, some food, and four walls before sunset. Everything else is tomorrow.

01 — Who you are

You, the Commoner

At character creation you pick a class. This guide assumes the Commoner — the default and the best first character. In the game's own words, Commoners "are generalists from a multitude of backgrounds. While they have no defining skills or strengths to speak of, they also lack the weaknesses of those who specialize."

That neutrality is exactly why it's recommended: every other class trades a bonus for a real penalty, and a beginner doesn't yet know which trade they want.

Commoners can do everything

A Commoner can still craft, hunt, smith and tailor — they simply get no head-start. Nothing in this guide is class-locked except the bow (Hunter) and sling (Malefactor); a Commoner throws spears and stabs instead, which is plenty for Day One.

03 — The first hour

Knap Your First Tools

Everything starts with knapping: shaping a loose stone into a tool head by chipping away voxels, then lashing that head to a stick. Your two priorities are a knife (harvests grass, reeds, berries and butchers animals) and an axe (fells trees for firewood).

Gather the raw materials

  • Loose stones & flint. Walk the ground and right-click the small stones lying about. Flint (often near gravel and water) is best — it knaps the sharpest blades and is the only stone that makes a usable knife/axe edge. Grab a handful.
  • Sticks. Pick up the loose sticks on the forest floor, or knock leaves with a tool later. You need one stick per tool as the handle.

How knapping works

  1. Place a knapping surface. With loose stone selected on your hotbar, hold Shift and click the top of a solid block. The stone lays out as a flat voxel grid.
  2. Chip to the pattern. Left-click the voxels you need to remove. The Survival Handbook shows the target outline for each tool; chip everything outside the shape until the blade remains.
  3. Collect the head. When the shape is complete it pops off as a tool head (e.g. a Knife Blade or Axe Head).
  4. Assemble at the grid. Press E and combine the head + a stick in the crafting grid to finish the tool.

For the exact target patterns of every knappable tool — knife, axe, hoe, shovel, spear and arrowheads — see The Four Crafts, which shows each one as an interactive grid you can step through.

Knap the knife first, then the axe

A flint knife unlocks grass (for the firestarter and torches), cattails and reeds (for cordage), berries and butchering. A flint axe then turns trees into firewood. With those two you can make fire and food — the rest of Day One.

04 — Warmth & light

Fire & Light

Fire cooks your food, lights your shelter and keeps you warm as nights get cold. You need three things: dry grass, firewood, and a firestarter.

  1. Cut dry grass with your knife from tall-grass tiles. A few handfuls will do.
  2. Make firewood. Chop a tree with your axe — the trunk topples realistically; break the felled logs to get firewood.
  3. Craft a Firestarter (a bow-drill) in the crafting grid from sticks + dry grass. It has a low success rate — hold right-click on the fuel and keep trying until it catches.
  4. Build the fire pit. Place 4 firewood on the ground to form a fire pit, then ignite it with the firestarter (hold right-click).
Torches are nearly free

A torch is just a stick + dry grass (or a cattail). Make a few — they're cheap portable light, and light level matters: drifters spawn in the dark, so a lit base is a safe base.

The fire pit is also your first cooker: put raw meat or foraged food in its slots to roast it. Later it becomes the base for a cooking pot and crucible — but tonight, roasted meat is dinner.

05 — Don't starve, don't poison yourself

Food on Day One

You won't farm today. Day-One calories come from foraging and a little hunting. Aim for a mix so your nutrition bars don't run dry.

forage

Berries

Right-click a berry bush to harvest without destroying it (left-click breaks it — then replant). Quick fruit nutrition.

forage

Cattail & roots

Cut cattails at the water's edge with your knife for cattail roots (roast them) and tops for cordage. Reliable early veg.

forage

Mushrooms

Plentiful, but some are poisonous. Check the Handbook (H) before eating an unfamiliar one — cook the safe ones.

hunt

Hares & chickens

Easy prey. Stab with a knife or throw a knapped spear (hold & release right-click), then harvest with the knife for raw meat to roast.

Cook it, and eat the rainbow

Raw meat can make you ill; roast it on the fire pit. And don't binge a single food group — your five nutrition bars (fruit/veg/protein/grain/dairy) each add a little max-health, so a varied diet literally makes you tougher. Full system in The Larder.

06 — Before the sun drops

A First-Night Shelter

You don't need a house tonight — you need walls, a roof, a door and light. The fastest shelter is a small dirt or cobblestone box dug into a hillside, with the fire pit inside for light and warmth. Below is a minimal, legal 5×5 plan; step through the layers.

  1. Floor & walls. Clear a 5×5 footprint and ring it with dirt or cobblestone two blocks high, leaving a one-block doorway.
  2. Fire inside. Place your lit fire pit in the centre — it lights the interior so nothing spawns in with you, and warms you as the night cools.
  3. Roof it. Cap the top so nothing drops in. A solid roof also keeps rain off your fire.
  4. Close the door. A quick wooden door (planks) or just a stacked block in the gap. If you have grass cordage, you can make a bedroll to sleep through safe nights and set your respawn.
Dig in, don't build up

On Day One it's faster to carve into a hill than to stack a tower. Three dug walls + one built wall + a roof is a complete shelter in a couple of minutes, and the surrounding stone is free.

07 — Holding until dawn

Surviving the Night

The danger after dark is drifters — shambling temporal creatures that spawn in low light — and surface predators like wolves. Your shelter plus a fire handles drifters; wolves you simply don't go out to meet.

The three night rules

  • Stay lit. Drifters spawn in darkness. A fire pit and a couple of torches keep your interior safe.
  • Stay walled. If you hear the dry rasp of a drifter, do not open the door. Wait it out or fight from a doorway you control.
  • Stay shallow. Don't tunnel deep tonight. Stability is high on the surface; it drops as you go down, and that's a Day-Three problem, not a Day-One one.
You have a grace period

Full temporal storms — when drifters spawn everywhere, even in daylight — don't begin immediately; the first ones are days away. Use that grace to get established. The whole system is explained in The Rust & The Gear.

Survive to morning and you've cleared the hardest part of the game's opening. Day Two is about pottery and clay (a bowl, a cooking pot, a crucible) and scouting copper — which is where The Long Road picks up.
