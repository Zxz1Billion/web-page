Rest on the Road

A small mod that punches far above its weight: Comforts adds sleeping bags and hammocks so you can skip the night anywhere — without ever moving your home spawn. The quiet hero of every long expedition.

Sleeping bags Hammocks MC 1.19.2 · Forge

00 — Orientation

Start Here

In vanilla, sleeping means hauling a bed and resetting your spawn to wherever you woke up. Comforts breaks that link: rest in the field, skip to morning, and keep your real respawn point safely back at base.

That one change makes caving, dungeon-diving, and off-world trips dramatically safer. It pairs naturally with the pack's expedition content — see the Wilds Bestiary and Cartographer's Journal.

01 — The essential

Sleeping Bags

A Sleeping Bag is a flat, placeable bedroll. Right-click it to sleep and skip the night (or wait out a thunderstorm) without setting your spawn point — your bed or respawn anchor at home stays exactly where it is.

  • Place it anywhere flat, sleep, then pick it back up — they're meant to be packed and reused (exact pick-up/consume behavior is config-dependent; approx by default they return to you).
  • Because they don't change spawn, you can sleep in a cave at bedrock and still respawn in your base if you die.
  • They reset the night and the phantom insomnia timer just like a bed.
Pack one always

A single wool sleeping bag in your backpack turns any expedition into a series of safe day-trips. It's the cheapest quality-of-life upgrade in the pack.

02 — The camp option

Hammocks

A Hammock strings between two solid anchor points (posts, walls, or trees). Climb in to sleep through to day — a lighter, more permanent "forward camp" fixture than a bedroll.

  • Hang it by placing the hammock between two supports the right distance apart (it needs the gap to string up).
  • Like sleeping bags, it skips the night without claiming your spawn.
  • Great for a semi-permanent mining outpost or a base on the Moon where you don't want a full bedroom.

03 — The point

Why It Matters

The whole value is the decoupling of "skip night" from "set spawn." In a heavily-modded pack full of dangerous detours, that means:

Safe respawns

Die in a Cataclysm arena or a Twilight boss fight and you wake up at base, not next to the thing that killed you.

No phantoms

Resetting the insomnia timer on the road keeps phantoms off your back during long builds and expeditions.

Off-world rest

Skip the long, hostile nights on a half-built Ad Astra outpost without committing a spawn there.

04 — Making them

Crafting & Colors

Sleeping bags are crafted from wool and come in every dye color, just like beds — so you can keep a tidy color-coded set or just grab the cheapest wool you have. Hammocks use a similar wool-plus-string recipe (check JEI for the exact grid in your pack).

Both are inexpensive and renewable from a small sheep pen, so there's no reason not to carry one.

05 — On servers

Multiplayer Notes

On a server, sleeping interacts with the usual "percentage of players must sleep to skip night" rule. Sleeping bags participate in that vote like a bed would. Specifics — whether one player can skip the night, decay timers, and pick-up behavior — are config-driven verify in-pack, so check with your host if the behavior surprises you.

06 — Field notes

Tips

  • Carry a sleeping bag on every trip; hang a hammock at any base you'll revisit.
  • Remember the headline rule: they do not change your respawn — that's the entire feature, not a bug.
  • Combine with a Tombstone grave and good food (Farmer's Delight) for a complete expedition survival kit.

★ — The path

Progression — fitting Comforts into your kit

Comforts is tiny — two items and one rule — so there's no tech tree to climb. Think of it instead as a habit you build up over a playthrough: from your very first wool to your deepest off-world expedition, the sleeping bag quietly becomes part of how you travel.

  1. Turn one: craft a wool Sleeping Bag. The moment you have wool, make a bag. It's the cheapest quality-of-life upgrade you'll touch all game.
  2. Always carry one for caving and early nights. Sleep at bedrock to skip the night without moving your spawn — wake up where you are, respawn where you started.
  3. Hang a Hammock at a forward mining base. Once you've got an outpost you keep coming back to, string a hammock as a permanent "skip night" fixture so you don't burn a bag each visit.
  4. Pack a bag for every big expedition. Twilight Forest trips, Cataclysm dungeons, and Ad Astra missions all turn into safe day-trips when you can rest in the field without committing a spawn there.
  5. On servers, learn the percentage-sleep interaction. Sleeping bags vote toward the "share of players asleep" night-skip rule just like beds — know your server's threshold before you rely on one to skip the night.
The whole point

No matter how deep you sleep, Comforts never changes your respawn — that's the entire feature. For where to take it, see the Mobs & Survival hub, the Exploration hub, and The Ascent.
