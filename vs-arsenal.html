The Arsenal

Every tool, weapon and piece of armor sits on a tier ladder, and each age opens the next rung. Two gates decide your pace: your pickaxe tier sets which ore you can mine, and your anvil tier sets which metal you can smith. This page lists what becomes craftable in each age, then explains the armor system in full.

00 — The rungs

The Tier Ladder

Tool materials run worst to best in a fixed order. Higher tiers mine faster, last longer and hit harder, and each one needs the previous age's gear to make.

The two gates

You can't mine iron ore without a bronze (tier-3) pickaxe, and you can't smith steel or meteoric iron without an iron anvil. A bronze anvil works everything up to and including iron. Plan each age around reaching the next gate.

01 — Staying alive

Armor Explained

Armor fills three slots — head, body and legs — separate from your warmth clothing. A hit picks a slot by chance, so a gap anywhere leaks damage.

20% hit

Head

Least-often struck, but unprotected head wounds hurt. Cheapest slot to cover first.

50% hit

Body

The most-hit slot by far. Prioritise the best body piece you can carry.

30% hit

Legs

Second most-hit. Don't leave it bare against predators or drifters.

The eight armor types

Four need no metal (early game), four are forged metal (copper, bronze, iron or steel). Within a type, the material sets durability; the type sets protection and how much it slows you.

Armor types, light → heavy (protection and movement penalty both rise)

How damage is cut

Armor removes a flat amount of damage and resists by tier. A high-tier blow (bear, storm-grade drifter) punches through flimsy armor; match your set to the threat. A full steel plate suit shrugs off almost everything — but the weight wrecks your mobility and ranged aim, so many players run brigandine or chain for balance, or gambeson while exploring.

Making metal armor

Metal armor is forged (plates and mail on the anvil) or cast (lamellar, poured from molds), then assembled — plate stacks plates onto chainmail onto a leather jerkin, so it's the most material-hungry. Upgrade the body slot first; it takes half your hits.

02 — Tier 1 · by hand

Stone Age

Everything here is knapped from stone or flint, or built by hand. You can't mine ore yet — the whole goal is to gather surface copper nuggets and reach the next age.

Tools

Knapped

Knife, Axe, Shovel, Hoe. No pickaxe — copper is the first one.

Weapons

First arms

Spear (throw or stab), flint arrows + Bow (Hunter), Sling (Malefactor), scavenged Club.

Armor

No metal

Improvised, Lamellar (wood), Leather, Gambeson — all craftable without smithing.

Stations & gear unlocked

Firepit, knapping, clay forming + the pit kiln (fired pottery), the clay cooking pot, baskets, torches and a firestarter. Build a forge and gather nuggets to cross into the Copper Age.

03 — Tier 2 · first metal

Copper Age

Smelt surface copper in a crucible, cast it in clay molds, and the full toolset opens up — most importantly the first pickaxe, which lets you mine ore at last.

Tools

The whole kit

Pickaxe, Axe, Knife, Shovel, Hoe, Hammer, Saw, Chisel, Scythe, Prospecting pick, Cleaver.

Weapons

Cast & smithed

Falx (sword), Spear, plus your bow/sling. The Falx first appears here.

Armor

Metal begins

Copper Chain, Scale, Brigandine and Plate — and cast Lamellar.

Stations & gear unlocked

Crucible + molds (casting), the cast copper anvil, bellows and quern. The hammer + anvil unlock smithing; the saw makes planks; the chisel carves blocks and gears. Now mine toward tin or bismuth/zinc to alloy bronze.

04 — Tier 3 · the gateway

Bronze Age

Alloy copper into tin, bismuth or black bronze. The same toolset returns, tougher and faster — and this age holds the two keys to iron.

Tools

Bronze set

The full kit again in bronze. The bronze pickaxe is required to mine iron ore.

Weapons

Harder hits

Bronze Falx and Spear: more damage, more durability than copper.

Armor

Bronze sets

Chain, Scale, Brigandine, Plate in any of the three bronzes.

Stations & gear unlocked

The bronze anvil works every metal up to and including iron — it's what refines bloomery blooms into wrought iron. Black bronze is the best of the three. With a bronze pick and bronze anvil, iron is open.

05 — Tier 4+ · the shortcut

Meteoric Iron

A side-grade a notch above plain iron, salvaged from fallen meteorites near the surface — no smelting needed, just smithing.

Tools

Iron-plus

The full set in meteoric iron: faster and tougher than wrought iron.

Weapons

Sharp edge

Meteoric Falx — a notch above iron. (No meteoric spear: spears stop at bronze.)

Armor

Premium

Meteoric armor where moulds/plates allow — durable mid-game protection.

The catch

Meteoric iron can't be worked on a bronze anvil — you need an iron anvil first. So it usually arrives after your first real iron, as a quick quality bump rather than a way to skip the Iron Age.

06 — Tier 4 · the workhorse

Iron Age

Mine iron ore with a bronze pick, smelt blooms in a bloomery, refine them to wrought iron on a bronze anvil, then smith the lot. Durable, abundant, the backbone of a settled base.

Tools

Iron set

The full kit in iron — long-lasting daily drivers. Cast/forge an iron anvil.

Weapons

Reliable steel-lite

Iron Falx (no iron spear — spears stop at bronze). The Tuning spear is a Clockmaker tool for disrupting locusts, not a rift-sealer; vanilla has no rift-sealing tool.

Armor

Iron sets

Iron Chain, Scale, Brigandine, Plate — strong, heavy, dependable.

Stations & gear unlocked

Bloomery and the iron anvil — the gate to both steel and meteoric iron. (A blast furnace for bulk iron is a popular mod addition, not vanilla.)

07 — Tier 5 · endgame

Steel Age

Carburise wrought iron in a cementation furnace over several days, then smith steel on an iron anvil. The best gear in the game, and a multi-day project to reach.

Tools

The best

The full kit in steel: top speed and durability. The last tools you'll make.

Weapons

Finest blades

Steel Falx (spears stop at bronze); rarer swords like the Blackguard shortsword and Forlorn Hope estoc.

Armor

Steel plate

A full steel plate suit takes almost no damage from most attacks — at a heavy mobility cost.

Stations & gear unlocked

The cementation furnace (turns iron bars into blister steel). After steel, progression is about quantity and decoration, not new tiers. See Fire & Metal for the full process.

★ — The numbers

Damage & Armor Values

Exact figures are config- and version-dependent, so hover an item in-game for live numbers. These are the wiki's published values and the rules that actually decide a fight.

Armor protection

Each piece has a protection tier, a % protection (applied after the flat cut) and a flat damage reduction. Non-metal types have fixed values; metal types climb with the material (copper → steel), which also sets durability.

How the tiers interact

Compare the attack tier to your armor tier. For each tier the attack is at or below yours, armor adds a small extra reduction; for each tier it is above yours, a much larger share gets through. A steel plate set (high tier) shrugs off low-tier hits almost entirely, while a copper set leaks against a storm-grade drifter. Flat reduction is subtracted first, then the percentage.

Weapon behaviour

A weapon's attack tier (its armor penetration) matters as much as raw damage. Damage and durability rise with the metal; exact points are in the Handbook.

Spears top out at bronze

You can't make an iron, meteoric or steel spear, and spears are attack-tier 0 — they never punch through armored monsters. Carry a falx for tiered foes (drifters, locusts); keep spears for hunting and throwing.
