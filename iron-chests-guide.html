Boxes

Two small mods with one honest promise: more space. Iron Chests upgrades the humble vanilla chest into a ladder of bigger, sturdier, sometimes see-through containers; Iron Ender Chests bolts a colour-coded frequency dial onto the ender chest so you can run as many separate teleporting stashes as you like. No power, no multiblocks, no chemistry — just the simplest "give me more slots" upgrade in the pack.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Iron Chests does exactly one thing and does it well: it takes the vanilla chest and gives you upgraded versions with far more slots, climbing from Iron all the way to Crystal and Obsidian. Iron Ender Chests does the same favour for the ender chest, adding a selectable frequency so different chests can share — or deliberately not share — their contents. Together they are the no-fuss answer to "I have stuff everywhere and nowhere to put it."

storage

Iron Chests

A tier ladder of upgraded chests — Iron, Gold, Diamond, Copper, Crystal, Obsidian (plus Dirt & Wood novelty tiers) — each with more slots than the last.

ender

Iron Ender Chests

Ender chests with a colour-coded frequency dial, so each frequency is its own shared, cross-base teleporting inventory.

no power

Zero infrastructure

No cables, no energy, no chemistry. Craft, place, open. The cheapest quality-of-life win in any tech pack.

see-through

Glass tiers

Diamond and Crystal chests are transparent — you can read their contents at a glance without opening them.

Where this fits

These chests are early-game storage that scales surprisingly far. They belong in the broader Storage & Logistics hub alongside drawers and AE2/QIO — start here, then graduate to those systems when you need search, auto-sorting, or a unified network. See §06 for the cross-references.

The whole idea in three steps

  1. Build a vanilla chest. Everything in Iron Chests starts from an ordinary chest, surrounded or framed by the tier's metal/material.
  2. Pick a tier. Iron for "a bit more," Diamond/Crystal for "everything," Obsidian for "and keep it through an explosion."
  3. Upgrade in place when you outgrow it. A Chest Upgrade item bumps a placed chest to the next tier without dumping its contents (§02).

01 — The ladder

The Chest Tiers

The progression is the classic Iron Chests ladder: Iron → Gold → Diamond → Copper → Crystal → Obsidian, with Dirt and Wood tucked in as cheap/novelty starters. Each step up the ladder hands you more slots. A vanilla single chest is 27 slots and a double is 54, so even the first Iron tier is already a meaningful jump.

Slot counts drift between Iron Chests versions and packs sometimes re-tune them. The values above are the rough brackets you'll see — treat them as "much bigger than vanilla, climbing each tier." The chest GUI itself is the ground truth; hover the recipe in JEI to confirm the exact tier you're crafting.

About Copper's placement

Copper is the odd one out: rather than slotting near the top, it's a low/early tier (below Iron) added to fit Minecraft's copper economy. Think of it as a cheap way to get somewhat more than a vanilla chest before you've smelted much iron — not as a stepping-stone to Crystal.

The two glass tiers

Diamond and Crystal chests are rendered transparent, so you can see the items stacked inside without opening the lid. That makes them ideal for display walls and "at-a-glance" storage rooms where you want to scan a whole wall and spot what you're low on. Crystal is the purest version of this — effectively a glass box with maximum capacity.

02 — No re-sorting

Upgrading In Place

The feature that makes Iron Chests painless: you never have to empty a chest to grow it. Craft a Chest Upgrade item, right-click it on a placed chest, and the chest jumps to the next tier with all its contents intact. No hauling, no temporary holding chest, no lost stacks.

The upgrade items

Each upgrade is a specific "from → to" item — you apply the one that matches your current chest and your target tier. The common ones:

Chest Upgrade items (apply to a placed chest)
UpgradeTakes youWhy
Iron → Gold UpgradeIron Chest → Gold ChestAdd rows once an Iron chest fills.
Gold → Diamond UpgradeGold Chest → Diamond ChestJump to the big, see-through bracket.
Diamond → Crystal UpgradeDiamond Chest → Crystal ChestTrade the frame for a fully transparent box.
Diamond → Obsidian UpgradeDiamond Chest → Obsidian ChestTrade visibility for blast resistance — turn a vault into a safe.
(Copper / Wood) → Iron UpgradeLower tier → Iron ChestBring a cheap starter chest up to the first real tier.
How to apply one

Hold the matching Chest Upgrade and right-click the placed chest. The block swaps tier in place; your items stay exactly where they were. The upgrade is consumed. Because the path branches at Diamond (→ Crystal or → Obsidian), decide there whether you want visibility or protection — you can't have both in one chest.

Why upgrade instead of re-craft

  • No downtime. A full chest stays full through the upgrade — handy for storage you can't easily empty.
  • Cheaper than you'd guess. An upgrade item only supplies the difference in materials, not a whole new chest.
  • Incremental. Grow a chest one tier at a time, exactly when it fills, instead of over-building from the start.

03 — The top of the ladder

Crystal & Obsidian

The two endgame tiers share the same huge capacity bracket but pull in opposite directions. One is built to be seen; the other is built to survive. Picking between them is really a question of what the chest is for.

visibility

Crystal Chest

Fully transparent and enormous. Every stack inside is visible from outside — the perfect display/showcase chest, and the easiest way to read a bulk store at a glance.

protection

Obsidian Chest

Same top-bracket capacity, but blast-resistant. Creeper, TNT, or a misbehaving reactor won't pop it and scatter your loot. The vault tier.

Choosing: safety vs visibility

When to reach for which
You want…PickBecause
To scan contents without openingCrystalSee-through walls make a tidy, legible storage room.
A display / museum wallCrystalThe whole point of a glass box is showing what's inside.
Storage near explosives or a reactorObsidianBlast resistance means a blowout doesn't cost you the chest.
A "do not lose this" vaultObsidianRare loot, backups, and irreplaceables ride out accidents.
You branch at Diamond

Both Crystal and Obsidian are reached by upgrading a Diamond chest (§02). Since the upgrade is one-way and one-or-the-other, think ahead: a wall of Crystal for the show floor, a pocket of Obsidian behind a blast door for the things you truly can't replace. Obsidian's protection only matters where things actually blow up — don't pay for it on a chest in a quiet attic.

04 — Teleporting storage

Iron Ender Chests

A vanilla ender chest gives every player one personal, shared-by-location-but-not-by-channel inventory. Iron Ender Chests blows that open with a frequency: a colour-coded three-dial code on the front of the chest. Any two ender chests set to the same frequency open the same inventory, anywhere in the world — and chests on different frequencies stay completely separate.

How frequencies work

The face of the chest carries a small dial of coloured pips you set with a click. That colour combination is the channel. Place a chest with the same colour code in your mine, your base, and the Nether, and they're all windows onto one shared box. Change one pip and you're suddenly looking at a different, empty stash.

  • Same frequency → same shared inventory
  • Different frequency → fully separate storage
  • Cross-dimension by default
  • No power needed

Personal vs shared channels

Ways to slice up your frequencies
SetupEffectGood for
One frequency, many chestsEvery chest is the same boxA unified drop-off you reach from anywhere.
Several frequenciesEach colour code is its own separate boxSorting by category — ores on one, food on another.
Personal (owner-locked) channelOnly you can access that frequencyKeeping a private stash on a shared/SMP server.
Shared (public) channelAnyone with the code can access itA team supply box every base feeds into.
Cross-base teleport storage

This is the killer use. Put one ender chest at every outpost on a single frequency, and material dropped in at the mine is instantly available at the main base — no carrying, no carts, no logistics network. Add a second frequency for "outbound" supplies (food, torches, tools) and you've got a two-way pipeline between every base you own.

Mind who knows the code

A public frequency is exactly as secure as the colour combination — if someone else sets a chest to the same code, they share your box. On servers, use a personal/owner-locked channel for anything you don't want pooled, and keep "interesting" colour codes off the obvious defaults.

05 — Living with them

Trapped & Variants

Beyond the plain tiers, Iron Chests carries a few variants and plays nicely with automation. The chests are still chests at heart — hoppers, comparators, and sorting mods treat them like inventories, just much bigger ones.

Trapped variants & hoppers

  • Trapped variants emit a redstone signal when opened, mirroring the vanilla trapped chest — useful for alarms, auto-lighting a storage room, or triggering a door when someone reaches for the vault.
  • Hopper interaction works as you'd expect: a hopper feeds into or pulls out of any tier. Because the chests are single-block (not double), they line up cleanly under a row of hoppers for input automation.
  • Comparators read fullness, scaled across the larger slot count — handy for "stop the machine when this Diamond chest is full" logic.
They're a buffer, not a sorter

A big chest is a great destination but it won't organise itself. Pair the chests with a sorting layer and you get the best of both: enormous quiet capacity behind a smart front end.

06 — Field notes

Tips

A handful of habits that get the most out of two very simple mods — and a clear-eyed note on when to move past them.

display

Crystal for bulk display

Build storage rooms out of Crystal (or Diamond) chests so you can read the whole wall at a glance and instantly see what's running low — no GUI hunting.

safety

Obsidian near hazards

Anywhere things explode — TNT mining stores, creeper-exposed builds, near reactors or a Mekanism fission setup — put your irreplaceables in blast-resistant Obsidian.

portable

Ender chests for expeditions

One ender chest at base and one in your pack (or at the dig site) on a shared frequency = a portable, shared stash. Mine fills up? Dump it home without walking back.

scale

Know when to graduate

When you need search, auto-crafting, or a single unified inventory, it's time for drawers + a network. Chests stay great as buffers and display even then.

When to graduate to drawers / AE2

Iron Chests scales beautifully up to "a tidy room of big boxes," but it has no search and no auto-sort. The moment you're spending real time hunting for items, move bulk staples into Storage Drawers and your long-tail into an AE2 (or QIO) network with a search terminal. Cross-reference the Storage & Logistics hub for that next step — and keep your Crystal chests as the human-readable display layer in front of it.

Quick reminders

Upgrade chests in place rather than re-crafting (§02). Branch carefully at Diamond — Crystal or Obsidian, not both (§03). Keep ender-chest frequencies written down somewhere, and use a personal channel on servers (§04). And whenever a number here disagrees with your game, the chest GUI and JEI are right.
