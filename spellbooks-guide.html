00 — Orientation

Start Here

Iron's Spells 'n Spellbooks bolts a full action-RPG magic system onto Minecraft. You learn spells, hold them on a casting focus, and spend Mana to hurl fireballs, raise the dead, mend wounds, or teleport across a battlefield. There is no vanilla XP cost — your power comes from gear and specialisation.

Three currencies and one verb describe almost everything in the mod. Get these straight and every tooltip in your inventory reads cleanly:

resource

Mana

The fuel for every spell. A pool that regenerates over time; both its maximum size and refill speed come from your gear and stats, not from levelling up.

focus

Casting Item

You cannot cast bare-handed. A Spell Book, a single-use Scroll, or a casting weapon/staff channels the spell.

stat

Spell Power

The per-school multiplier on a spell's effect. Stack the right Spell Power and a common spell out-damages a careless legendary.

The shape of the system

  • Nine schools of magic, each with its own flavour, signature status effects, and dedicated scaling stat and gear.
  • Spell rarity runs Common → Uncommon → Rare → Epic → Legendary, and most spells have multiple levels that raise mana cost and effect together.
  • Mage mobs — Pyromancers, Cryomancers, Archevokers, Necromancers and friends — drop scrolls, gear and crafting reagents.
  • The Scroll Forge lets you craft scrolls from spell-specific ingredients once you find the recipe materials.
Your first hour

Pick up any spell scroll from a loot chest, hold it, and cast (default a quick-cast / use key). Then craft or find a Spell Book so you can imbue that scroll into a permanent, reusable slot. Wear even one piece of mage armour for a starter mana pool — without it your pool is tiny.

Dependencies

Iron's Spells 'n Spellbooks runs on GeckoLib (animations & models), Curios (the ring/amulet slots that hold your mana-boosting trinkets), and Playeranimator (casting gestures). All three ship with the pack.

01 — The fuel

Mana & Casting

Mana is your single spell resource. Every cast subtracts a cost from your pool; the pool refills passively over time. You can never "run out forever" — but in a hard fight you will run dry, and a dry mage is a target. Managing the pool is the core skill of the class.

What sets your mana

Where max mana and regen come from
SourceAffectsNotes
Mage armour sets+Max Mana, +Mana RegenThe single biggest source. A full set dwarfs unarmoured casting.
Curios trinkets+Max Mana, +Regen, +Spell PowerRings & amulets in Curios slots — often school-specific.
Spell Power stat×Spell effectMultiplies damage/heal/duration; comes from gear, not the mana pool itself.
Cooldown ReductionRecast speedLowers the per-spell cooldown so you spend a full pool faster.
Spell ResistanceIncoming magic takenThe defensive mirror of Spell Power; mage armour grants some.
Numbers are configurable

Exact mana pools, regen rates and per-spell costs vary by config and pack tuning, so this guide describes them qualitatively. Trust the live tooltip on each spell (it shows mana cost and cooldown) and your HUD mana bar over any number written here.

The three ways to cast

reusable

Spell Book

Holds multiple spells in slots. Open it to imbue scrolls, then swap the selected spell on the fly while held. The mage's main hand for the whole game.

single-use

Scroll

One spell, one cast, then consumed. Great for emergencies, utility, or trying a spell before committing it to a book.

hybrid

Weapon Casting

Certain staffs and magic swords cast a bound spell directly — the foundation of spell-sword builds.

Cooldowns

Every spell has its own cooldown after casting, shown on the hotbar/HUD. Cooldown is independent of mana: a cheap spell can still gate you on time, and a pricey nuke might recharge instantly but bankrupt your pool. Cooldown Reduction gear lets you weave more casts into the same window — the second pillar of throughput after raw mana.

Two knobs, not one

Damage per cast scales with Spell Power; casts per fight scale with Max Mana + Regen + Cooldown Reduction. A good build pushes both — a glass cannon with no mana is a one-shot wonder.

02 — The disciplines

The Schools of Magic

Spells belong to schools, each with a thematic identity, a signature status effect, its own Spell Power stat, and synergy gear that amplifies it. Specialising means picking a school and stacking its power, its cooldown reduction, and the mana to fuel it.

Why schools matter

Spell Power is tracked per school. A piece of Pyromancer gear pumps Fire Spell Power but does nothing for your Ice spells. This is the whole reason to specialise rather than dabble — focused gear multiplies one school hard.

Generic vs. school power

Some trinkets grant generic Spell Power that helps every school, while armour sets usually grant school-specific power plus cooldown reduction. Mix one generic-power amulet with a school-locked armour set for the best of both.

03 — Power & scarcity

Spell Tiers & Rarity

Two independent axes describe a spell: its rarity (how hard it is to find) and its level (how strong this particular copy is). Higher levels cost more mana and hit harder; rarer spells tend to be more powerful or more situational.

Rarity tiers (drop chance & ceiling rise together)
RarityFoundTypical role
CommonoftenBread-and-butter staples; cheap, spammable.
UncommonregularStronger staples and early utility.
RarescarceNotable power spikes; build-defining picks.
EpicrareHeavy hitters and signature school spells.
Legendaryvery rareThe capstone nukes, summons and ultimates.

Spell level

Most spells roll at a level from 1 up to a per-spell maximum. Each level:

  • Raises the spell's effect — more damage, longer duration, more summons, bigger heal.
  • Raises the mana cost, so a higher level is not free — it asks more of your pool.
  • Can be increased by acquiring a higher-level copy (or upgrading via the mod's progression items) and imbuing it into your book.
Level requirements

High-level spells often demand a minimum Spell Power or gear threshold to cast effectively — pouring a Level 5 nuke through a robe-less novice wastes most of its potential. Grow your gear and your spell levels together.

Rarity ≠ best

A maxed common spell on a fully-kitted mage frequently out-performs a low-level legendary. Optimise the spell you have gear for, not the rarest line in the tooltip.

04 — Gathering the lore

Acquiring Spells

You build a spellbook from scraps found across the world. There are four main pipelines, and a serious mage uses all of them:

loot

Loot Chests

Dungeons, structures and the mod's own buildings hide scrolls and mage gear. The most reliable early supply.

mobs

Mage Mobs

The mod adds hostile casters that drop scrolls, gear and reagents — fight fire with fire.

craft

Scroll Forge

Craft specific scrolls from spell-specific ingredients once you've gathered the recipe materials.

imbue

Imbuing

Move a scroll's spell into a permanent Spell Book slot, turning a one-shot into a reusable spell.

The mage mobs

Iron's Spells adds themed enemy casters — each tied to a school, each dropping its school's loot. Hunt the one whose magic you want to learn:

Hostile casters & what they teach you
MobSchoolDrops / theme
PyromancerFireFire scrolls, Pyromancer gear
CryomancerIceIce scrolls, Cryomancer gear
ArchevokerEvocation / mixedPowerful evocation scrolls, robes
NecromancerEnder / Blood / summonsSummoning & death-themed loot
ApothecaryNature / Poison / HolyPotion-flavoured scrolls & reagents

The Scroll Forge & imbuing

  1. Gather the spell-specific ingredients a scroll recipe calls for (often reagents dropped by the matching school's mobs).
  2. Use the Scroll Forge to craft the scroll — a single-use copy of that spell at a given level.
  3. Open your Spell Book and imbue the scroll into a free slot, consuming the scroll but making the spell permanent.
  4. Repeat to fill the book; higher-tier books hold more slots, letting you carry a full school's kit at once.
Check JEI

Because exact recipes and ingredient lists are pack- and version-specific, look up each scroll in JEI in-game. It shows the Scroll Forge recipe and the reagents you still need to farm.

06 — The robes & rings

Mage Gear & Armour

Gear is where a mage's real power lives. Armour sets grant +Max Mana, +Mana Regen, +Spell Power (often school-specific), and −Cooldown. Weapons channel spells; Curios trinkets stack mana and power on top.

Read the tooltip set bonus

Many sets give their best numbers only when worn complete. Hover the pieces — a full Pyromancer set's combined Fire Spell Power and cooldown reduction far exceeds the sum of mismatched pieces.

Weapons & casting items

  • Staffs — magic weapons that boost casting and can channel bound spells; the classic mage main-hand alternative to a book.
  • Spell Book (offhand) — carry your reusable spell loadout in the offhand while wielding a staff or sword.
  • Ritual / Concentrated weapons — specialised magic weapons that lean into spell-power scaling for spell-sword hybrids.

Curios trinkets

ring

Mana Rings

Slot rings that raise Max Mana or Mana Regen — the easiest way to top up your pool without changing armour.

amulet

Power Amulets

Necklaces granting Spell Power (generic or school-specific) and cooldown reduction.

slots

Curios Slots

Curios adds dedicated ring/amulet/belt slots so trinkets don't compete with armour or hands.

Trinkets are free stats

Because Curios slots sit outside your armour and hands, every ring and amulet is pure additional mana or power. Fill every slot you have — there's no opportunity cost.

07 — The path to archmage

Progression & Build Paths

Iron's Spells rewards focus. Rather than learning one spell of every school, pick a lane and stack everything that amplifies it: that school's Spell Power, cooldown reduction, and enough mana to keep casting.

The general arc

  1. Scavenge scrolls. Cast from single-use scrolls and any starter armour piece for a basic mana pool.
  2. Get a Spell Book. Imbue your favourite scrolls into permanent slots so you stop burning consumables.
  3. Pick a school. Choose the school whose spells you keep reaching for, then farm its mage mobs for gear.
  4. Assemble a set. Complete that school's armour for the set bonus — a huge jump in power and cooldown.
  5. Fill Curios. Add mana rings and a power amulet; now you can sustain a whole fight.
  6. Push to endgame. Chase Archmage / Netherite-tier sets and Legendary spells for the capstone build.

Build archetypes

specialist

Pure Caster

One school, stacked Spell Power + cooldown + mana. Maximum damage, minimum melee. Wants the most mana of any build.

hybrid

Spell-Sword

A casting staff/sword in main hand, Spell Book in offhand. Fewer spells, but real armour and melee fallback when mana's dry.

support

Holy Sustainer

Heal, Cleanse, buffs & Wisps to keep a team (or yourself) alive through long bosses.

summoner

Necromancer

Raise minions to tank and swarm while you cast from the back line — a numbers-game build.

PvE in To the Moon 2.0

  • Cataclysm bosses — big, hard-hitting fights reward ranged nukes (Fire/Lightning) plus a reliable escape (Teleport/Ascension) and self-heal.
  • Twilight Forest — varied progression bosses; control schools (Ice root/slow) and summons shine for managing adds.
  • Keep at least one mobility and one defensive/heal spell slotted no matter your school — a glass cannon dies to one mistake.
Don't out-level your gear

A Legendary spell at high level with no matching Spell Power underperforms a tuned common. Grow spell level and school gear together, and check that you meet a spell's power threshold before relying on it in a boss fight.

08 — Field wisdom

Tips & Synergies

The difference between a robe-wearer and an archmage is economy and timing. A few habits that compound:

Mana economy & combat habits
HabitWhy it works
Open with cheap spellsSave your big nuke's mana for when it lands clean; don't dump the pool turn one.
Watch the mana bar, not the cooldownA ready spell you can't afford does nothing. Pace casts to your regen.
Stack cooldown reductionMore casts per fight from the same pool — multiplies your effective DPS.
Carry one mobility + one healSurvival utility keeps you alive long enough for damage to matter.
Fill every Curios slotFree, stacking mana & power outside your armour and hands.

Combining schools

Specialising doesn't mean ignoring everything else. The strongest builds keep a splash of off-school utility:

  • Any damage school + a single Holy Heal or Ender Teleport scroll covers your two biggest survival gaps.
  • Ice control before a Fire or Lightning nuke — freeze, then detonate into a stationary target.
  • Summons (Necromancy/Wisp) plus a back-line caster turns every fight into a 2-against-1.
  • Generic Spell Power trinkets help the splash spells too, since they aren't school-locked.

Pairing with the pack

  • Iron's Spells slots in alongside the pack's combat content — use mage gear and trinkets as an alternative or complement to tech armour.
  • Curios trinkets from other mods share the same slots — budget your rings/amulets across everything competing for them.
  • Magic mobility (Teleport, Ascension, Angel Wings) covers traversal in big structure dungeons without needing tech jetpacks.
When in doubt, read the tooltip

Every spell shows its mana cost, cooldown, school and level live. JEI shows where scrolls come from. The mod wiki and your in-game GUIs are the final word — this codex is a map, not the territory.
