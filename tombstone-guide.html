A working field manual for Corail Tombstone — the death-management mod that turns dying from a catastrophe into a recoverable inconvenience. When you fall, a decorative Grave rises holding every item and every level of XP you were carrying. On top of that safety net sits a whole soul-magic progression: Knowledge of Death, special enchantments, familiars, altars, and rites. This guide runs from your first death to a fully kitted soul-shrine.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Corail Tombstone exists to make death stop meaning total loss. The moment you die, a Grave spawns at the spot you fell, swallows your whole inventory and your experience, and waits for you to come back and collect it. That single mechanic changes how you play an unfamiliar modpack — and around it the mod wraps a deep, optional layer of soul magic you can grow into.

core

The Grave

On death your items + XP are stored in a Grave at the death spot. Recover them by right-clicking it.

progression

Knowledge of Death

A soul/XP-like resource you gain over time and by surviving; the currency of every perk and rite.

magic

Enchantments & Magic

Soulbound, Magic Siphon, Shadow Step and more, plus poppets, scrolls, and the Ankh of Pray.

ally

Familiars

Capture a pet into a Familiar Receptacle so it follows you, aids in fights, and can revive you.

Why it matters in this pack

This pack is full of things that can flatten you — see the Mobs & Survival guide for the Cataclysm, Twilight Forest, and Mutant bosses. Tombstone is your essential insurance against them: even a wipe to a boss leaves your loadout sitting safely in a Grave instead of scattered on the floor or despawned in lava. Learn the Grave first, then grow the soul magic that makes the next attempt survivable.

The shape of the climb

  1. Trust the Grave. Die once on purpose somewhere safe and watch the Grave form. Recover it. Now you know your gear is backed up.
  2. Bank Knowledge. You accrue Knowledge of Death just by surviving and adventuring; it's the fuel for everything below.
  3. Get Soulbound early. Put the Soulbound enchant on your best gear so it stays on you through death — no grave-run needed for those items.
  4. Build a kit. A Scroll of teleport-to-grave, a Voodoo Poppet, and a Familiar turn dying into a non-event.
  5. Raise a shrine. An Altar of the Pure Souls and the rites let you spend Knowledge on lasting buffs before expeditions.

Numbers and exact effects below are approximate — Tombstone is highly configurable and its enchant values can shift between versions. When in doubt, the in-game Book of Knowledge and the official wiki are ground truth.

01 — The safety net

The Grave

When you die, Tombstone intercepts the drop. Instead of scattering items on the ground (where they burn, despawn, or get stolen), it places a decorative Grave block at your death position and stuffs your entire inventory and all your experience inside it. The Grave is yours; it sits there patiently until you return.

What goes in, and where it lands

  • Everything you carried — main inventory, hotbar, armour, off-hand, and your XP — is stored in the Grave.
  • Relocation if obstructed. If you die in lava, in the void, mid-air, inside a wall, or anywhere a grave can't be placed, the mod relocates the Grave to the nearest safe spot (and, in some configs, can pull you and your loot out of harm's way).
  • The grave is marked. A subtle beam or particle effect and a death message help you find it; the death point is also logged so you can navigate back.
Finding your way back

Tombstone gives you location aids so the grave-run isn't a guessing game: the death message records coordinates, a Grave can show a guiding beam, and items like the Scroll of Knowledge / Grave Scroll or a death-compass style item point you home. The cleanest option is a Scroll of teleport-to-grave (see §04) that simply warps you to the spot.

Recovering it

Right-click the Grave to recover its contents — your items and XP flow straight back into you (and ideally back into the right slots). Depending on pack configuration, opening a grave may require a Grave Key, which ties access to ownership and prevents others from looting your stuff.

Don't assume it's forever

Whether graves decay over time is a config option, and some packs enable it. If your pack has decay on, a grave left too long can vanish along with its contents. When you die deep in a dangerous biome, treat recovery as urgent — or carry a teleport-to-grave scroll so distance is never the problem.

02 — The currency of souls

Knowledge of Death

Knowledge of Death is Tombstone's second XP track — a soul-flavoured progression resource separate from vanilla levels. You earn it slowly just by surviving and adventuring, and you spend it on perks, on applying the mod's special enchantments, and on powering its soul magic.

How you earn it

  • Over time & by surviving — staying alive and playing the game accrues Knowledge in the background.
  • Through actions the mod rewards — exploring, certain kills, praying at altars, and reaching milestones can grant Knowledge.
  • Your total is tracked on a Knowledge bar/screen (often surfaced via the in-game book and a dedicated key/UI).

How you spend it

Knowledge is the gate on everything advanced in the mod. You burn it to:

  • Unlock and rank up perks — passive bonuses to survival, magic, and recovery.
  • Apply Tombstone enchantments via the Enchanter / soul magic (§03).
  • Power rites and prayers at the altar (§05) for temporary buffs.
Knowledge is patient money

Because it accrues passively, the longer you play the more options open up — you don't have to grind it directly. Spend early Knowledge on the perks and the Soulbound enchant that protect your gear, and let the rest bank for rites you'll want before a boss fight.

03 — Soul-bound steel

Tombstone Enchantments

Tombstone adds its own family of special enchantments themed around death, souls, and recovery. They aren't rolled on a vanilla enchanting table — they're applied through the mod's Enchanter's altar / enchantment books, paid for with Knowledge. The exact magnitudes below are approximate and configurable.

Lead with Soulbound

Of the whole set, Soulbound is the one to prioritise. Put it on your favourite weapon and your armour as soon as you can afford the Knowledge: those pieces now survive death automatically, so even a failed grave-run can't cost you your core kit. Everything else is gravy.

Applying these usually means crafting the enchantment via the soul-magic workflow (an Enchanter's altar or enchanted books) and spending Knowledge. Treat the values as guidance — check the in-game book for the exact tiers and costs in your version.

The Familiar is more than a pet

A Familiar Receptacle binds a companion to you that fights alongside you and — crucially — can help revive you or soften death in a pinch. Capture a sturdy mob early; it's one of the best returns on soul magic for the bosses in this pack.

05 — The shrine

Altars & Rites

The heart of Tombstone's active magic is the Altar of the Pure Souls. Build it, pray at it, and spend Knowledge to perform rites — rituals that grant temporary buffs, utility effects, or world interactions. Exact rite names and effects are approximate and version-dependent.

Praying for buffs

At the altar you can pray to receive blessings — temporary boons that help before a dangerous outing. Prayers and rites draw on your Knowledge and the altar's soul charge, and the Ankh of Pray (§04) lets you carry that praying capability into the field.

Rites (representative)

The kinds of rites you'll perform (effects approximate)

Recovery ritesRestore, retrieve, or safeguard items / souls and ease the cost of dying.
Utility ritesWorld and movement helpers — light, transport, environmental effects.
Soul / familiar ritesBind, empower, or interact with familiars and soul magic.

Building a soul shrine

A proper shrine combines the altar with the soul furniture — Grave Plates, a Cenotaph, and decorative graves — into a dedicated room you return to before each big run. Keep it near your base, charged and stocked, so praying and rite-casting is a quick pre-expedition ritual rather than a detour.

Pray before you push

Make a habit of swinging by the altar before diving into a boss arena. A protection blessing plus a topped-up Knowledge bar costs you a minute and can be the difference between a clean kill and a grave-run.

06 — On a server

Multiplayer & Safety

Tombstone is built with servers in mind. Its grave protection makes shared worlds far less punishing — your death drops aren't free loot for whoever wanders by.

Grave access & anti-grief

  • Owner-only access. By default a Grave can only be opened or broken by its owner, so other players can't loot your death pile.
  • Grave Keys tie access to a token you hold, reinforcing ownership and giving admins a clean permission model.
  • Anti-grief friendly. Because items go into a protected block instead of scattering, grave-camping and drop-stealing largely stop being a thing.
Multiplayer-relevant controls
SettingEffect
Grave protectionRestricts open/break to the owner (and optionally trusted players / admins).
Grave Key requirementRequires a key item to access, hardening ownership.
Keep-inventory interplayIf the server runs keepInventory, Tombstone's grave can be redundant — configure which one wins.
Decay timersWhether graves expire; set carefully on servers so absent players don't lose gear.
Watch the keep-inventory overlap

If your world has vanilla keepInventory turned on, you won't drop anything to a grave at all — the two systems overlap. On most Tombstone packs keepInventory is off precisely so the grave system can do its job (recovery with a small cost), which is the intended experience. Check your pack's config before assuming one or the other.

Making expeditions safe

For group runs into dangerous dimensions, the combination of owner-locked graves, Soulbound gear, and a shared base shrine means a party wipe is recoverable: each player's loadout waits in their own protected grave, and Soulbound pieces never even leave their hands. Coordinate teleport-to-grave scrolls so everyone can regroup fast.

07 — Habits that pay off

Tips

Tombstone rewards a few simple routines. Build these into how you play and dying stops being scary.

  • Soulbound your best gear early
  • Always carry a teleport-to-grave scroll
  • Keep a Voodoo Poppet equipped
  • Bind a Familiar for fights
  • Pray before boss runs

The checklist

  1. Get Soulbound on your kit. Weapon and armour first — those pieces now survive death automatically, so a lost grave can't strip you.
  2. Keep a Scroll of teleport-to-grave on you. Recovery becomes one click from anywhere; carry a spare in storage.
  3. Use a Familiar. Capture a tough companion in a Familiar Receptacle — it fights with you and can soften or reverse a death.
  4. Bank Knowledge and pray. Visit the altar before expeditions for a protection blessing and to spend Knowledge on the perks that matter.
  5. Treat graves as a net, not a plan. They're insurance against the pack's bosses — not an excuse to skip armour and consumables.
Tombstone vs. the pack's bosses

This is your safety net against the heavy hitters in the Mobs & Survival guide — the Cataclysm bosses (Ignis, the Netherite Monstrosity, the Ender Guardian), the Twilight Forest progression bosses, and the Mutant mobs. Go in with Soulbound gear, a teleport scroll, a Familiar at your side, and a fresh altar blessing, and a wipe becomes a quick regroup instead of a run-ending disaster.

The one failure mode

The only way Tombstone really lets you down is grave decay plus a long absence: if your pack enables decay and you forget where you died, you can lose everything that wasn't Soulbound. Mitigate it with Soulbound on essentials and a teleport-to-grave scroll so distance and time are never the problem.

★ — The path

Progression — from grave insurance to soul magic

Tombstone isn't a one-trick mod you bolt on and forget — it's a ladder. You start by trusting the Grave to save your loadout, then turn passively-earned Knowledge of Death into enchantments, soul-magic blocks, and altar rites. Walk it in order and each rung makes the next dangerous expedition more survivable.

  1. Confirm the safety net & learn recovery. Die once somewhere safe, watch the Grave form, and recover it. Learn how the grave-run works in your pack — including whether opening it needs a Grave Key key requirement is config-dependent — so you trust the system before you rely on it in a fight.
  2. Bank Knowledge of Death. It accrues over time just by surviving and adventuring; you don't grind it directly. Let it accumulate so you can afford the enchants, blocks, and rites below.
  3. Soulbound your best gear. Spend early Knowledge to put Soulbound on your favourite weapon and armour so those pieces survive death entirely — no grave-run needed for your core kit. enchant tiers & costs vary by version
  4. Build the soul-magic blocks. Raise an Altar of the Pure Souls as your shrine's heart, and bind a sturdy companion with a Familiar Receptacle so it fights beside you and can soften a death.
  5. Run rites & prayers for buffs. At the altar, spend Knowledge on protection/blessing rites and prayers before each big outing. exact rite names & effects approximate
  6. Carry a teleport-to-grave scroll into boss fights. Keep a Scroll of teleport-to-grave on you (and a spare in storage) so any wipe is a one-click return rather than a long, dangerous walk back.
This ladder is your safety net

Walked end to end, this progression is what makes the pack's heavy hitters survivable: the Cataclysm bosses, the Twilight Forest progression, and the Mutant mobs. Soulbound gear, an altar blessing, a Familiar at your side, and a teleport scroll in hand turn a boss wipe into a quick regroup. Pair it with the Mobs hub for what you're fighting and The Ascent for where this sits in your overall climb.
