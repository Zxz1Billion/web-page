A working guide to Supplementaries — the big, charming grab-bag of blocks that are both decorative and functional. It stores honey in jars, drains your barrels through faucets, points the way home with sign posts, rotates blocks on a turntable, fires fireworks from a cannon, and lights a crypt with a hundred little sconces. This guide groups the standouts so you can find the one block that finishes your build.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Supplementaries isn't one machine or one system — it's dozens of small, charming blocks that each solve a little problem or finish a little scene. The trick is that almost none of them are purely cosmetic: the jar that looks lovely on a shelf also stores honey, the sign post that dresses a fence also points to your base, the brazier that lights a hall also cooks food. This guide groups the standouts so you can reach the right one fast.

light

Lighting & Atmosphere

Sconces, candle holders, wall lanterns, hanging lanterns, the brazier and bellows — ambience that actually emits light.

store

Storage & Display

Item shelves, jars, faucets, the locked safe, the sack, pedestals, statues, the globe.

info

Signage & Info

Sign posts, hanging signs, notice boards, blackboards, checkerboards, flags & banner patterns.

redstone

Mechanisms

Crank, turntable, pulley, spring launcher, the cannon, relayer, speaker block, clock block.

It pairs beautifully with other decor mods

Supplementaries is one of the headline blocks-and-decoration mods in many "kitchen-sink" packs. If your install also has Decorative Blocks, Chipped, Macaw's sets or similar, treat Supplementaries as the functional layer over their texture layer. See the dedicated Build Ideas section, and cross-reference your pack's Building & Decoration hub for the rest of the palette.

How to think about it

  1. It's a Moonlight Lib mod. Supplementaries is built on Moonlight Lib (also by MehVahdJukaar), which it needs as a dependency. Moonlight handles the shared plumbing — block registration, the dynamic models, soft-incompatibility shims — so things like sign posts and hanging signs can reuse any wood type in your pack.
  2. Almost everything is craftable early. Most blocks use mundane materials — planks, iron, glass, terracotta, string — so you can start decorating from day one rather than at endgame.
  3. Almost everything is configurable. Any block you dislike can be toggled off; many have tweakable behaviour. The in-game config (and JEI) is the ground truth for what your pack actually enabled.
  4. Look for the dual purpose. When you meet a new block, ask "what does this do as well as look like?" — that's the Supplementaries design signature.
A note on accuracy

Supplementaries is large and frequently updated, and nearly every block can be reconfigured. The mechanics here are the established, well-known ones; exact stack sizes, fluid amounts and trade prices are approximate. When in doubt, your in-game JEI and config screen win.

01 — Light & mood

Lighting & Atmosphere

Vanilla gives you torches, lanterns and not a lot else. Supplementaries fills the gap with a whole family of light sources sized for taverns, crypts, manors and cottages — most of which can be wall-mounted, hung, or dyed.

Light sources & ambient fixtures
BlockWhat it isWhy you'd use it
SconceA small wall flame (also Soul, End & coloured variants)The torch's elegant cousin — flush wall lighting for halls and stairwells.
Sconce LeverA sconce that doubles as a redstone leverHidden switches behind a bookshelf or fireplace.
Candle HolderA standing/wall candlestick, dyeableSoft tabletop light; rows along a banquet table.
Wall LanternMount any lantern flush against a wall/postFixes vanilla's biggest lantern annoyance — no more floating lanterns.
Hanging variantsLanterns & pots hung beneath blocks/chainsSuspended light over walkways, wells and porches.
BrazierA standing fire bowl; can cook food & emit signal smokeCourtyard centrepiece and a campfire-style cooker.
BellowsRedstone-driven air pump that feeds firePumps a brazier/fire hotter, or spreads/extends flame for redstone-lit scenes.
Wall Lantern is the quiet MVP

The Wall Lantern works with lanterns from any mod in your pack (thanks to Moonlight Lib tagging). Sneak-place a lantern against a wall or fence post and it snaps flush — instantly making every tavern, mineshaft and street look hand-built instead of vanilla.

Setting a mood

  • Crypt / dungeon: Soul Sconces (blue flame) in alternating wall slots, a low Brazier at the altar, and a single hanging lantern at the entrance.
  • Tavern / inn: Candle Holders down the tables, Wall Lanterns by each door, and a warm Brazier near the hearth.
  • Manor hall: Sconce Levers hidden among ordinary Sconces so the room "knows" which one opens the secret passage.
Dye & wax

Many candle/holder pieces accept dye for colour. If you've waxed something and want to redecorate, the Soap item (see §05) strips wax and resets banners — useful when a build evolves.

02 — Keep it & show it

Storage & Display

This is where Supplementaries earns its "functional decoration" reputation hardest. These blocks store real items and fluids while looking like part of a lived-in workshop, kitchen or vault.

Jars & Faucets — the brewery duo

The Jar is the flagship. It stores and displays its contents: drop in items (cookies, slimeballs, spider eyes) and they appear inside; or fill it with a fluid — water, honey, milk, potions, and more. A Faucet placed on the side of a barrel, keg or tank will drain fluid downward into a jar (or any container) below it, optionally toggled by redstone.

JarItems or a fluid (water, honey, milk, potions…)Displays its contents; stackable look; fillable from a Faucet above.
FaucetMoves fluid downward, one container to the nextPlace on a barrel/keg face; redstone-toggleable; the heart of any drip line.
Item ShelfUp to a couple of display items on a slim ledgeWall-mounted display for trophies, tools or potions.
SafeLocked personal storageBind it to a Key; only the owner (or key-holders) can open it.
SackPortable bag / small storage blockTuck-away storage that fits a rustic aesthetic.
PedestalRaises a single item/block on a plinth, slowly spinningMuseum-style display; pairs with a Statue or trophy.
StatueA poseable decorative figureGuardians at a gate; centrepiece in a hall.
GlobeA spinning world globe (sepia & coloured variants)Cartographer's-desk decor; a rare loot/trade find in some setups.
A working honey/potion line

Set a barrel or keg of fluid above a row of jars, drop a Faucet on each barrel face, and let it drip into the jars below. Wire the faucets to a lever or comparator and you have a brewery that looks like a brewery — fluid visibly leaving the barrel and pooling into displayed jars.

The Safe — locked personal storage

The Safe is genuine secure storage for multiplayer. Place it, and it binds to you as owner. Craft a Key and bind the key to the safe: anyone holding a matching key can open it; everyone else is locked out, and the safe resists being broken/stolen. It's the cleanest answer to "how do I keep my diamonds safe on a shared server" without a separate protection mod.

Keep a spare key

Treat safe keys like real keys: bind a backup and store it somewhere else (a second safe, a friend's ender chest). Losing the only key to a bound safe is exactly as inconvenient as it sounds, and server admin help may be the only recovery.

Showing things off

For pure display, the Pedestal (rotating plinth), Item Shelf (wall ledge) and Statue cover trophy rooms, shop windows and shrine builds. The Globe is the cartographer's centrepiece — and in some configurations it's a special craft or trade rather than a plain recipe, so check JEI for how your pack hands it out.

03 — Words on the wall

Signage & Info

Bases get big and confusing. Supplementaries adds a whole signage suite so a player (or a guest) can actually navigate, read notices, and leave messages — far past vanilla's single sign.

Signage & information blocks
BlockShowsUse
Sign PostOne or two directional arrow-signs on a fenceCrossroads markers; "Spawn →", "Mine ←". Stacks multiple on one post.
Hanging SignA sign suspended beneath a block/chainShop fronts and tavern signs (predates/complements vanilla's own).
Notice BoardA framed board that displays a written book or itemPost quests, rules, or a recipe page that players can read in-world.
BlackboardA chalk drawing surface (glowing variant too)Doodle maps, mark progress, draw signage by hand.
CheckerboardA flat patterned boardGame tables, floor accents, draughts/chess scenery.
FlagA tall hanging banner that uses banner patternsHeraldry on castle walls; faction colours on a server.

Sign Posts — the navigation workhorse

A Sign Post attaches arrow-shaped signs to a fence. Each face can be rotated freely, you can stack two per post (upper and lower), and you can point them at any angle — so a single crossroads post can read "Town →", "Nether ↖", "Home ↓" at once. They're the backbone of a base directory and a server road network.

Right-click to aim

After placing a sign post, interact with the upper or lower sign to rotate that one independently. Sneak-interacting and using a stick/empty hand cycles the angle, so you can aim each arrow at its real-world destination rather than just N/E/S/W.

Notice Boards & Blackboards

A Notice Board holds a single item or a written book and shows it framed on the wall — perfect for posting server rules, a quest log, or a "today's menu" page that anyone can right-click to read. A Blackboard is a freehand chalk surface: draw rough maps, tally counts, or sketch directions; the glowing variant reads in the dark.

Flags & banner patterns

The Flag hangs taller than a vanilla banner and accepts the full banner pattern system, so your heraldry, mojang-charge or faction crest drapes properly down a castle wall. Combine with Ash & Soap (§05) to clean and re-pattern banners when your sigil changes.

04 — Moving parts

Mechanisms & Redstone

Supplementaries has a genuine kinetic streak: blocks that rotate other blocks, launch entities, lift things on ropes, fire projectiles, and tick out signals. These are the toys that make a contraption feel alive.

Crank + Turntable — rotation without redstone

The Turntable rotates whatever directional block sits on top of it by one step each time it gets a pulse. Drive it with redstone, or pop a Crank beside it and right-click to advance it by hand. It's the easiest way to re-aim a piston, point a dispenser, or slowly spin a display piece without rebuilding.

The Cannon

The Cannon is exactly as fun as it sounds: load it, set the pitch/aim and charge, and it launches fireworks or projectiles. Use it for celebratory firework salutes over a city, a redstone-triggered fireworks finale, or — with the right ammo — genuine area defense.

Mind the blast & the config

Cannon ammunition and the Spring Launcher can damage entities and, depending on config, terrain. On servers, the cannon's destructive options are exactly the sort of thing pack authors gate behind config flags — confirm what's allowed before you build a battery of them near spawn.

Quality-of-life redstone

The Clock Block gives you a compact, self-contained timed pulse (handy for day/night automation), the Relayer tidies up signal routing, and the Speaker Block turns a redstone edge into a chat announcement — perfect for "intruder!" alarms or a tavern that greets guests.

05 — Green & growing

Nature & Garden

A second cluster of blocks turns Supplementaries into a gardener's and homesteader's kit: planters that actually grow things, hanging greenery, edible decor, and a couple of genuinely useful cleaning items.

Garden, food & cleaning
Item / blockWhat it does
Planter / Flower BoxA raised bed you can plant crops, flowers or saplings in — decorative and farmable.
Hanging PotA suspended flowerpot for a single plant beneath a block.
Bamboo SpikesA damaging trap block (can be tipped/poisoned); deters mobs and players.
PancakesStackable edible decor food — build a tall, syrupy pile and eat from it.

Planters & hanging greenery

The Planter (and flower-box style variants) is a raised bed: place it, plant a crop, flower or sapling, and it grows like farmland but reads as furniture — perfect for windowsills, balconies and tidy indoor herb gardens. Hanging Pots suspend a single plant beneath a block for trailing vines over a doorway or porch.

Soap & Ash — the cleaning kit

Soap is more useful than it looks: it removes wax from waxed copper/blocks, strips patterns from banners so you can re-pattern them, and clears applied colour — the "undo" button for decoration. Ash turns up as a byproduct and feeds into crafting (including soap chains, depending on recipes).

Bamboo Spikes for cheap defense

Bamboo Spikes damage entities that walk over them, and can be tipped with a potion for poison/weakness. Line a moat or a mob-farm drop with them for a low-tech, no-redstone deterrent — just remember they hurt you too.

06 — The wandering merchant

Daily Goods & Trades

Supplementaries adds a trader and a slice of seasonal content that ties its odds-and-ends together. The headline is the Red Merchant and the Daily Goods trade list.

The Red Merchant & seasonal content
FeatureWhat it isNotes
Red MerchantA special wandering trader variantSells Daily Goods — rotating, sometimes hard-to-craft Supplementaries items.
Daily GoodsA rotating trade list of decorative/rare itemsHow you get items the mod intends to be "found" rather than freely crafted.
Seasonal / holiday contentThemed items tied to real-world datesOptional & config-gated; appears around the relevant season if enabled.

The Red Merchant is the way the mod distributes some of its rarer or "discovered" items — globes, special variants, and seasonal goods — instead of putting everything in a crafting recipe. Trading with it is worth doing if you want the full set without grinding mats.

Seasonal content is opt-in flavour

Some holiday/seasonal items only appear around their real-world date and are easy to disable in the config if you'd rather keep the world date-agnostic. If you can't find a "seasonal" item someone mentioned, the calendar — or the config toggle — is usually the reason.

Check JEI for "how do I get this?"

If a Supplementaries item has no obvious recipe in JEI, it's likely a trade-only or loot-only item by design. Look for it on the Red Merchant's Daily Goods list before assuming it's disabled.

07 — Tuning the cabinet

Configuration

Supplementaries is heavily configurable — which is part of why it ships in so many packs. Nearly every block can be toggled on or off, and many have behaviour knobs. If a block does (or doesn't) appear in your world, the config is the place to look.

What you can typically tune
KnobEffect
Per-block enable/disableTurn off any block you don't want registered or craftable.
Worldgen togglesControl which features (e.g. salt-like or structure additions) generate.
Trades & merchantEnable/disable the Red Merchant and adjust Daily Goods behaviour.
Seasonal contentToggle holiday items so they (don't) appear by date.
Mechanic tweaksCannon damage, spike behaviour, jar capacities and similar per-block options.
Client/visual optionsSome purely cosmetic toggles (particles, tooltips, rendering).

Where to look

  • In-game config screen — if your pack ships a config UI (Mod Menu / Forge config), Supplementaries exposes its toggles there with descriptions.
  • Config files — under your instance's config/ folder, the Supplementaries (and Moonlight) configs are editable text; split into common/server/client where relevant.
  • JEI / EMI — the fastest "is it enabled and how do I make it?" check. If a block has a recipe in JEI, it's enabled and craftable; if not, it may be trade/loot-only or toggled off.
On a server, server-side wins

Block enables and gameplay knobs are decided by the server's config, not your client's. If a tooltip promises a block your admin disabled, it simply won't be there. Coordinate config changes with whoever runs the world before assuming something's broken.

Trust in-game over any guide

Because every value here is configurable and the mod updates often, treat this guide as orientation. Your in-game config screen, tooltips and JEI are the ground truth for what's enabled and how it behaves in your pack.

The mod is huge; don't try to use everything. Pick the one Supplementaries block that solves the problem in front of you — the floating lantern, the un-navigable base, the boring storage room — and let the rest of the cabinet reveal itself as you build.

★ — The path

Progression — what to reach for, and when

Supplementaries is a grab-bag, not a tech tree — there's no boss to beat or tier to unlock. So the useful question isn't "what's next?" but "which blocks matter at which stage of a base?" Lean on it earliest for the functional wins, then layer in storage, mechanisms, and finally a full decoration pass once the bones are in place.

  1. Early functional wins. Day one, reach for the blocks that solve real problems: a Safe for locked storage (essential on shared servers), Sconces and Wall Lanterns so your build actually lights up without floating torches, and Sign Posts or a Notice Board to label and navigate a growing base.
  2. Storage & display. Once you're settled, graduate to the "functional decoration" core: Item Shelves for trophies and tools, and Jars fed by Faucets to both store and show off items and fluids — honey, potions, milk — as part of the room.
  3. Mechanisms. With the essentials handled, add moving parts: a Crank + Turntable to re-aim and spin blocks, a Pulley for hidden doors and dumbwaiters, and a Cannon for firework salutes (or genuine ballistics).
  4. The decoration pass. Finally, dress the finished base: banners and Flags for heraldry, Planters and hanging greenery on the windowsills, and a Globe on the cartographer's desk to tie the whole scene together.

None of this is gated — you can craft a globe on day one — but building in this order means each block earns its place rather than cluttering an unfinished shell. For the bigger picture, see the Building hub, pair it with Decorative Blocks for the surrounding palette, and slot it into your wider modpack journey via The Ascent.

Your pack decides the menu

Supplementaries is heavily configurable, so any of these blocks may be toggled off (or behave differently) in your install. Before you plan a stage around a block, check JEI for what's actually enabled and craftable — it's the ground truth for the path your pack lays out.
