00 — Orientation

Start Here

This is a structure mod: it adds taverns (often in or near villages) plus a handful of standalone buildings that flesh out the world with more character and loot. The exact roster shifts between the mod's versions, so treat specifics as a guide and confirm in-game.

It complements the bigger exploration mods covered in the Cartographer's Journal — more reasons to wander.

01 — The namesake

Taverns

The signature addition: tavern buildings that generate in or beside villages. A tavern gives a village a social waypoint — atmosphere, food, seating, and a little loot — and makes a natural rest stop on a long overland trip.

  • Look for them when you arrive at a village; they stand out from the standard houses.
  • They're a good spot to grab food and a quick chest before moving on.

02 — Beyond the bar

New Structures

Alongside taverns, the mod adds assorted standalone structures to world-gen — the kind of illager outposts, camps, and small dungeons that give the map more to stumble onto. The precise list depends on your installed version verify in-game, but expect a mix of:

Outposts & camps

Hostile-held buildings guarding loot — clear them like a mini-dungeon.

Themed dungeons

Small set-piece structures with their own layout, mobs and chests.

Worldly flavor

Decorative/landmark builds that make exploration feel richer.

03 — Version-dependent

Stronghold Overhaul

Some versions of Dungeons and Taverns are known for revamping the End stronghold into a grander, more dungeon-like complex around the End portal. If your pack's version includes it, expect the stronghold to be a bigger, more dangerous, more rewarding affair than vanilla's. This is version-specific approx — check the in-game structure before assuming.

04 — The payoff

Loot & Danger

These structures tend to hold mid-tier loot — resources, enchanted books, themed blocks — guarded by the usual hostiles (and sometimes illagers). Nothing here is free; the combat-oriented structures expect you to fight room by room. Loot tables are config-driven, so contents vary by pack.

05 — Where to look

Finding Them

  • Taverns: check every village you visit.
  • Standalone structures: they generate naturally as you explore; spawn frequency is config-tunable.
  • Use a map mod to mark and revisit them — see the Operator's Handbook.

06 — Field notes

Tips

  • Visit taverns for flavor and a quick resupply; clear the combat structures with mid-tier gear and a grave net.
  • Bring the pack's combat kit (Tinkers / Iron's Spells / Apotheosis) for the tougher buildings.
  • Check JEI / the in-game structure list to see exactly which structures this pack's version adds.

★ — The path

Progression — working it into your travels

Dungeons and Taverns is structure flavor more than a tech ladder — there's no recipe tree to climb, just buildings to find as you wander. Fold its stops into the journeys you're already making, and let your gear decide how deep you push.

  1. Resupply at taverns. When you pass a village on an overland trip, duck into its tavern for food, seating, and a quick chest before moving on. Bring nothing special; carry a grave net as a habit.
  2. Clear the easy standalone structures. As you explore, take the outposts, camps, and small dungeons that you stumble onto — mid-tier gear handles them room by room. Always pack a grave net before opening a fight.
  3. Gear up between stops. Bring the pack's combat kit (Tinkers / Iron's Spells / Apotheosis) so the tougher themed buildings stay worthwhile rather than lethal.
  4. Tackle the overhauled stronghold when End-ready. If your version revamps the End stronghold version-dependent, save it for when you're geared for the End — it's a bigger, more dangerous, more rewarding affair than vanilla's. Gear plus a grave net before you descend.
  5. Confirm the roster. Check JEI / the in-game structure list to see exactly which structures this version adds verify in-game, so you know what's actually out there to find.
There's no fixed order here — pin this onto the journeys mapped in the Exploration hub, and let The Ascent set the overall pace. Gear and a grave net for every note above.
