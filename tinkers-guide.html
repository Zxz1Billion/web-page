A working field manual for Tinkers' Construct 3 — the mod where you never touch a crafting grid to make a tool. You forge parts, assemble them at a station, then layer on modifiers forever. From your first flint hand axe to a molten Smeltery that triples your ore and casts Manyullyn into a blade that out-mines diamond — this is how the whole machine fits together, plus a representative interactive Smeltery schematic.

Tinkers' Construct 3.x + Tinkers' addons MC 1.19.2 · Forge 🔥 The Smeltery runs on lava

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Tinkers' Construct is not a "craft a pickaxe" mod. It is a tool foundry. Every tool is built from interchangeable parts, each part is a chosen material with its own stats and traits, and every finished tool is an upgradeable platform you sink modifiers into for the rest of the world.

Three ideas carry the entire mod. Internalise them now and every later screen reads cleanly:

build

Tools = Parts

A pickaxe is a Pickaxe Head + Tool Handle + Tool Binding. You make each part separately, then assemble — never in a 3×3 grid.

choose

Material = Identity

The same part shape in Flint, Bone, Iron or Manyullyn behaves completely differently. Material picks are the gameplay.

grow

Modifiers = Forever

Tools gain XP, level up, and accept Sharpness, Haste, Fortune, Unbreaking and dozens more. You upgrade one tool for the whole save.

Why you never "lose" a tool

  • Tinkers tools don't break. At 0 durability they go inert until repaired — they are never destroyed, and they keep every modifier you invested.
  • They earn XP. Using a tool levels it, granting extra modifier slots over time (the leveling system / addons like Tinkers' Leveling expand this).
  • They are repairable. Patch them at a Tinker Station with more of the head material, or pour molten metal over them in the Smeltery.
Your first hour

Punch a tree, craft a Pattern and a Part Builder + Tinker Station, and make a wooden Pickaxe and a flint/stone Hand Axe. No Smeltery required yet. Read the in-game book "Materials and You" — it is the canonical tutorial and updates itself as you progress.

01 — The wood tier

First Tools — No Smithing Needed

You can play a full early game before melting a single ingot. The starter loop is three blocks and a stack of patterns. Everything here works with materials you can punch, mine or kill for: wood, stone, flint, bone and a little copper.

The starter stations

templates

Pattern

A blank template crafted from wood. Used in the Part Builder to shape a part. Cheap and reusable.

parts

Part Builder

Crafts non-metal parts from a Pattern + a material (planks, stone, flint, bone). Your no-Smeltery part factory.

assembly

Tinker Station

Where parts become tools, where you repair, and where you apply modifiers. The heart of the workflow.

book

Materials & You

The given starter book. "Puny Smelting" then "Mighty Smelting" unlock as you build the Smeltery.

The starter tools

Build a tool by laying its parts on the Tinker Station. A few you'll want immediately:

Early tools and the parts they need
ToolPartsDoes
PickaxePickaxe Head + Handle + BindingStandard mining; your day-one tool.
Hand AxeSmall Axe Head + HandleChops wood and fights — a flint head is a great start.
MattockPick Head + Small Axe Head + HandleHoe + axe + shovel in one; the classic farming/utility multitool.
Dagger / SwordBlade + Handle (+ Crossguard)Melee. Fast and modifiable.

Part shapes vs material

A part has a shape (what slot it fills — head, handle, binding) and a material (what it's made of). The Part Builder handles non-metal materials directly; metal parts are cast in the Smeltery (see §03–04). The same Pickaxe Head shape in Flint, Bone or Iron is three very different heads.

Material tiers gate mining

A tool can only mine blocks at or below its head's harvest tier. Stone/flint mine iron-level; you need an iron-tier (or higher) head to mine diamonds and obsidian. Pick your head material first, then dress the rest for stats.

02 — The substance

Materials & Traits

Every material a part can be made from carries two things: a set of stats (durability, mining speed, attack damage, harvest tier, and for handles a durability/speed multiplier) and one or more traits — passive quirks that change how the tool plays. Materials are loosely grouped into tiers 1–4, roughly mirroring vanilla tool progression.

Numbers are descriptive here

Exact durability/speed/attack values shift between Tinkers' versions and are config-tunable. Treat the table below as relative guidance — JEI and the in-game material screens are authoritative for your pack.

Traits worth knowing

Traits are free, permanent, and stack with modifiers. A few canonical ones:

  • Stonebound — mines faster as durability drops (Stone). The inverse, Dense/Sturdy-style traits, reward keeping it full.
  • Jagged — deals more damage the more worn it is (Flint). Pairs with Stonebound on a "use-it-down" build.
  • Ecological — slowly self-repairs over time (Wood). Great on backup tools.
  • Crumbling — bonus speed on instant-mine / soft blocks.
  • Sharp / Insatiable — raw or ramping attack — the reason Manyullyn blades hit so hard.
  • Dwarven — copper-line bonus the deeper (lower Y) you are.
Mix materials for synergy

Because each part picks its own material, you can stack traits: a fast Cobalt head, a Stonebound-leaning handle for late-durability speed, and a binding chosen purely for its trait. The art of Tinkers is in the combination, not any single "best" material.

03 — The core multiblock

The Smeltery

The Smeltery is the beating heart of mid-game Tinkers. It is a hollow multiblock furnace built from Seared Bricks that melts metals (and more) into molten fluid, then lets you cast that fluid into parts, ingots and blocks. Crucially, melting ore yields more metal than smelting it — most ores melt into roughly 2× (or more) an ingot's worth of molten metal, and the Smeltery alloys metals you can't get any other way.

The required pieces

shell

Seared Bricks

The wall and floor blocks. Smelt grout (clay + sand + gravel) to seared brick. The hollow interior is the melting volume.

brain

Smeltery Controller

One special wall block with a face — your access point and the block that "forms" the structure.

fuel

Seared / Fuel Tank

Holds the fuel. Lava is the standard — one bucket melts a lot. Tanks also raise the liquid capacity.

output

Seared Drain + Faucet

The Drain is a wall port; a Faucet on its outer face pours molten fluid onto a Casting Table or Basin below.

How melting & alloying work

  1. Heat the bottom. A Seared/Fuel Tank of lava sits in the wall; the Smeltery heats from the fuel and melts whatever you drop inside.
  2. Ore → more metal. Raw ore and many ores melt into a surplus of molten metal — your ore-doubling. Ingots/blocks melt to exactly their worth.
  3. Alloy automatically. When two compatible molten metals share the tank in the right ratio, they combine into an alloy on their own.
  4. Pour it out. A Faucet on a Drain empties molten fluid onto a Casting Table (parts/ingots) or Casting Basin (blocks/large items).

A representative Smeltery build

Below is a small, legal Smeltery: a hollow seared-brick shell with a floor, a Controller, a Tank (fuel), a Drain with a Faucet, and a hollow interior. Step through the Y-layers. The Smeltery scales up — taller and wider gives more melting slots and capacity, with a foundry-style interior that grows with height.

It must be airtight and seared

Every wall, floor and corner must be a valid seared block (bricks, tank, drain, controller, etc.), the interior must be hollow, and the controller forms it. A single gap or a non-seared block and the structure won't activate.

04 — Shaping the molten

Casting & Casts

Molten metal is useless until you pour it into a mold. Tinkers gives you two surfaces and a clever bootstrap for the molds themselves — the Cast system.

The two casting surfaces

small

Casting Table

Sits under a Faucet. Holds a Cast (or item) and casts parts, ingots, and the Casts themselves. Your part-printer.

large

Casting Basin

For blocks and large items — metal blocks, seared blocks, gems, Plate parts for armor. Pour and let it cool.

Making a Cast (the bootstrap)

  1. Place the item you want a mold of on a Casting Table — say an iron Pickaxe Head you made in the Part Builder, or a vanilla ingot.
  2. Pour molten Gold (or Copper) over it from the Faucet. The metal hardens around the item into a reusable Gold Cast.
  3. Reuse the Cast forever. Place the Cast on the table and pour any metal in to stamp out that shape, again and again.
Sand & Red Sand Casts — the cheap path

Don't want to spend gold? Press a Sand Cast or Red Sand Cast against an item to make a single-use mold — it's consumed when filled. Perfect for one-off parts before you commit to permanent gold molds.

05 — The heart of the mod

Modifiers

If parts are the body, modifiers are the soul. At the Tinker Station you drop a finished tool plus a modifier item into a slot, and the tool gains a permanent ability. Slots come in types, and you earn more of them as the tool levels or by spending special items.

Slot types

common

Upgrade Slots

The everyday slots — Sharpness, Haste, Fortune, Unbreaking and friends. Most tools start with a few.

rare

Ability Slots

Powerful actives like Auto-smelt, Silky, Expanded (AoE). Scarce — spend them carefully.

defense

Defense Slots

Armor-side — protection modifiers (used on Tinkers' armor, see §06).

soul

Soul Slots

For the rarest, gated modifiers. Earned, not crafted cheaply.

Getting more slots

  • Level the tool. Using a tool grants XP; leveling awards extra upgrade slots over time.
  • Spend special items. Silky Cloth / Silky Jewels, Ancient Hides and similar gated drops convert into extra Upgrade or Ability slots.
  • Embellishment / leveling system. Cosmetic Embellishments dress slime-line gear; the broader leveling framework is how you keep growing one tool into the endgame.
Ability slots are precious

You'll have far more Upgrade slots than Ability slots. Decide early whether a tool is your Silk-Touch tool or your Auto-smelt tool — converting later means re-earning a scarce slot. Plan the build, then commit.

06 — Wearable Tinkers

Armor

In Tinkers' Construct 3, armor is built and modified exactly like tools — from parts, with materials and modifiers — using Defense Slots in place of some upgrade slots. There are three main lines, each with its own niche.

The Tinkers' armor lines (1.19)
SetBuilt fromBest at
Travelers GearLeather-line parts, lightEarly, cheap, very modifiable; great mobility base.
Plate ArmorPlate parts cast in the SmelteryHeavy protection; the tanky endgame set.
SlimesuitSlime-line partsUtility / fun set with strong slime synergies & embellishments.

How it plays

  • Plate parts are cast. Pour molten metal into Plate Casts in a Casting Basin (large item), then assemble at the station — this is why §03–04 matter for armor too.
  • Defense slots take protection mods. Stack defense-oriented modifiers; embellishments add cosmetic color (slime line).
  • Same upgrade ecosystem. Many tool modifiers apply — Reinforced (Unbreaking), Soulbound, Magnetic and more carry over to armor pieces.
Mix and match

You aren't locked to one set. A Plate chestplate for raw defense plus lighter Travelers pieces for mobility, each individually modified, is a perfectly valid loadout — exactly the part-by-part freedom the tools enjoy.

07 — Scaling the forge

Smeltery Automation

A hand-poured Smeltery is fine for a few parts; a base wants a casting line. Tinkers gives you fluid-routing blocks and early single-block melters so the forge runs while you do other things.

Routing molten metal

pour

Faucet

The valve. Place on a Drain or Channel; a redstone pulse (or a held signal) makes it pour onto the surface below.

route

Channel

Seared Channels carry molten fluid horizontally from a Drain to several Faucets — fan one Smeltery out to many Casting Tables.

control

Redstone

Faucets respond to redstone, so a comparator/clock can drive continuous, hands-free casting of ingots or parts.

drain

Seared Drain

The wall port molten fluid leaves through. Multiple drains = multiple parallel output lines.

Single-block early melting

Before the full multiblock, or for small jobs, the smaller seared machines do real work:

The pre-Smeltery / support blocks
BlockRole
Melter + HeaterA single-block melter heated by a Seared Heater (solid fuel). Melts a couple of things at a time — great early.
AlloyerCombines molten metals into alloys without the full Smeltery (fed by tanks).
Seared HeaterBurns solid fuel to power the Melter/Alloyer. The non-lava heat source.
Seared TankBuffers molten fluid or fuel; raises Smeltery capacity when built into the wall.
Seared vs Scorched

Tinkers ships two forge "flavors". The overworld Seared Smeltery runs on lava. The Nether Scorched Smeltery (Scorched Bricks) is an alternative built around magma/blood-themed mechanics — same idea, different aesthetic and fuel path, useful if you base in the Nether.

08 — Ranged & exotic

Bows & Special Tools

Tinkers goes well past pickaxes. Ranged weapons are part-built like everything else, and a stable of "special" tools covers AoE mining and big-swing combat.

Bows & arrows

  • Longbow / Crossbow / Shortbow — assembled from limbs, a grip, and a bowstring. Limb material sets draw speed and power; grip and string tune handling.
  • Arrows are themselves modifiable Tinkers items in many setups — head + shaft + fletching — so your ammo carries traits too.
  • Like all Tinkers gear, bows accept modifiers (Power-equivalents, Haste-as-drawspeed, etc.) and never truly break.

09 — Mastery

Tips & Endgame

By now you can build, cast, and modify anything. The endgame is about picking materials per slot, keeping tools fed and repaired, and understanding why a maxed Tinkers tool simply leaves vanilla behind.

Material picks by slot (rules of thumb)

Common late-game choices (tune to your pack)
SlotReach forBecause
Head (mining)Cobalt / ManyullynCobalt = speed; Manyullyn = durability + tier.
Head (combat)Manyullyn / Queen's SlimeStrong attack & ramping traits.
HandleStat-multiplier metalsHandles multiply head durability/speed — high-leverage slot.
Binding / extraHepatizon / Rose GoldExtra-modifier traits beat raw stats here.
Bow limbsfast/strong alloysLimbs set draw speed and arrow power.

Keeping tools alive

  • Repair at the station with the head material, or pour molten metal over the tool in the Smeltery — fast and cheap once the forge is up.
  • Reinforced / Unbreaking stacks reduce durability use so far that high-tier tools rarely need topping up.
  • Ecological handles self-mend slowly — handy on a backup or rarely-used tool.

The Plate + Embossment endgame

At the top of the tree you're casting Plate parts for armor, dressing slime gear with embellishments, and embossing/leveling tools far past their starting modifier budget. A single fully-built pickaxe can carry Fortune III, max Haste, Auto-smelt, Reinforced, Expanded and more — all at once.

Why Tinkers out-scales vanilla

A diamond pickaxe is fixed: one enchant set, then it's done. A Tinkers pickaxe compounds — material traits + a dozen stacking modifiers + leveling + endless repair on one tool you keep all game. The Smeltery makes the materials cheap; the station makes the tool eternal. That's the whole game.
