The Overhauled World

Vanilla's structures are famously a bit dull. The YUNG's family quietly replaces almost all of them with hand-crafted, larger, loot-rich and far more dangerous versions — and adds a few brand-new ones. If a stronghold or temple feels grand in this pack, YUNG's is why.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

These mods don't add a new system to learn — they make the world you already explore much better. Each one replaces (or adds) a structure with a bigger, hand-built, trap-laden, loot-rich version, all sharing YUNG's API as their backbone.

They're the foundation of the Cartographer's Journal. The headline change: vanilla structures are now worth seeking out.

02 — Underfoot

Overworld Overhauls

Better Mineshafts turns the old plank tangles into sprawling, biome-themed networks worth mining out. Better Strongholds rebuilds the stronghold into a grand, multi-wing dungeon (the End portal is now a real destination). Better Dungeons upgrades the tiny spawner rooms into proper multi-room complexes with better loot and more bite. Expect new traps and denser mobs across all three.

03 — Trap puzzles

Desert & Jungle Temples

Better Desert Temples and Better Jungle Temples are the showpieces: large, multi-room temples with genuine puzzles, traps and hidden vaults instead of a single TNT pit. Move carefully — pressure plates, dispensers and dart traps are waiting — and the payoff is some of the best early/mid loot around.

Watch your step

These temples are built to catch the greedy. Disarm traps, light as you go, and don't sprint into a treasure room you haven't checked.

04 — Swamp & sea

Witch Huts & Ocean Monuments

Better Witch Huts expands the lonely swamp shack into a fuller lair, while Better Ocean Monuments reworks the underwater raid with new layouts and rewards. For the monument, bring underwater mobility and breathing (a Mekanism scuba/jetpack setup or potions) before taking on the Guardians.

05 — Other dimensions

The Nether & End

Better Nether Fortresses turns the fortress into a sprawling, vertical complex packed with blazes and wither skeletons — a richer (and more dangerous) source of blaze rods and wither skulls. Better End Island rebuilds the central End spike island into a grander arena, making the post-dragon End feel like a proper finale.

06 — The new ones

Bridges & Extras

YUNG's Bridges spans ravines and rivers with natural-looking bridges (handy for travel and a nice worldgen touch), and YUNG's Extras sprinkles small, flavorful structures across the world. Neither demands attention — they just make the map feel more lived-in.

07 — Field notes

Tips

  • These structures are more dangerous than vanilla — bring real gear (Tinkers / Apotheosis), light aggressively, and watch for traps.
  • They're excellent XP, loot and enchanted-book sources — great between tech milestones.
  • Use a map mod and mark cleared sites so you don't re-raid them (see the Operator's Handbook).
  • Keep a sleeping bag and a grave handy for the long delves.

★ — The path

Progression — a tour of the overhauls

There's no fixed order to YUNG's structures, but a rough one falls out of accessibility: start with what you can walk into early, and save the dimension-gated raids for when you have the mobility and gear to survive them. Think of this as a working tour, not a quest line. Bridges and Extras need no plan at all — they're passive worldgen that simply shows up as you travel.

  1. Surface temples & mineshafts — Better Desert & Jungle Temples and Better Mineshafts are reachable from day one. Bring: basic armour, a stack of torches, and patience for traps. Reward: some of the best early/mid loot, plus an easy XP and string/ore farm underground.
  2. Dungeons & witch huts — Better Dungeons (the spawner complexes) and Better Witch Huts step up the mob density. Bring: a real weapon, milk/antidotes for the swamp, and light to kill spawners. Reward: enchanted books, spawner loot and a foothold for mob farming.
  3. Strongholds, toward the End — Better Strongholds rebuilds the path to the End portal into a grand, multi-wing delve. Bring: Eyes of Ender, food and a way home. Reward: the End portal itself, plus library and vault loot along the way.
  4. Ocean monuments — Better Ocean Monuments need water mobility before they're worth attempting. Bring: underwater breathing and propulsion (see the map mods and gear guides), plus Mining Fatigue countermeasures. Reward: sponges, prismarine and a defended treasure core.
  5. Nether fortresses — Better Nether Fortresses are sprawling and vertical, packed with blazes and wither skeletons. Bring: fire resistance, a bow, and fall-safe routes. Reward: blaze rods for brewing and wither skulls toward the Wither.
  6. The End island — Better End Island is post-dragon content: clear the dragon first, then explore the grander central arena. Bring: ender pearls, blocks and end-game gear. Reward: a proper finale and a staging ground for the outer End.
Pace yourself

Every one of these is more dangerous than vanilla: over-gear rather than under-gear, light aggressively as you push in, and watch constantly for pressure plates and dispenser traps. For where this fits in the wider run, see the Exploration hub and The Ascent.
