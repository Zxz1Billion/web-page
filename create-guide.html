A working field manual for Create 0.5.x — the mod where nothing is "powered" and everything is turned. From your first water wheel, through cogwheels, belts and gearshifts, into the processing chain that doubles ore and forges brass, then up to assembled contraptions and full-blown trains. Build kinetic machines that visibly spin, and learn to read a network at a glance.

00 — Orientation

Start Here

Create is not an energy mod. There are no joules, no cables, no batteries. There is one currency — Rotational Force — and it travels mechanically through shafts, cogs and belts. Generators add capacity; machines spend it; if a network spends more than it has, the whole thing locks up.

Three numbers describe any kinetic network. Get them straight and the entire mod reads cleanly:

capacity

Stress Units (SU)

Generators produce stress capacity; machines impose stress. If imposed stress exceeds available capacity the network is overstressed and stops dead. Goggles show the meter.

tempo

Speed (RPM)

How fast a shaft turns, in rotations per minute. Many machines need a minimum RPM to run; faster usually means more throughput — and more stress drawn.

trade

The exchange

Gearing trades one for the other. Speeding rotation up costs more stress per machine; slowing it down frees capacity. You never get something for nothing.

How stress actually balances

Stress is measured at a reference RPM. A machine's stress impact scales with how fast you drive it — run a press at double speed and it draws roughly double the stress. A generator's capacity is what it can sustain. Your job is simply: keep total impact under total capacity on every connected network.

  • Andesite Alloy — the foundational material. Andesite + iron or zinc nuggets, mixed or crafted; nearly every early machine and casing needs it.
  • Brass — the mid-tier metal. Crush/mix zinc + copper into brass; it gates the smarter machines (Mechanical Arm, Brass Funnel, Deployer-grade automation).
  • Casings — Andesite, Copper, Brass and Railway casings wrap shafts and turn raw blocks into proper machines. The casing you can craft marks your tier.
First three things to build

A Water Wheel (free, silent SU), a Hand Crank for one-off tasks, and a Mechanical Press over a depot to start pressing sheets. Craft a pile of Andesite Alloy and an Engineer's Goggles first — you'll want to see stress before you build anything that can overstress.

01 — The prime movers

Generating Rotation

Every kinetic network starts at a generator: a block that produces both speed and stress capacity. They scale very differently, so pick by tier and by how much you need.

Generators, roughly weakest to strongest
SourceHow it spinsTierNotes
Hand CrankRight-click to turnmanualOne-shot power for a single machine. Great for testing or a deployer you trigger by hand.
Water WheelFlowing water on its bladesearlyFree, silent, reliable. Flow on opposite sides and matching direction maximises output.
Large Water WheelFlowing water, bigger radiusearlySeveral times the capacity of a small wheel for the same stream. The early workhorse.
WindmillSail blocks assembled on a Mechanical Bearingearly–midCapacity scales with sail count up to a cap; needs open sky and a bearing to assemble.
Steam Engine + BoilerHeated water → steam pressurelateThe big-league SU. Scales with boiler tank size and number/heat of Blaze Burners.
Creative MotorSet RPM directly (creative only)debugInfinite capacity, configurable speed. For testing — not survival progression.

Water Wheels

The honest beginner's generator. Place a wheel so flowing water hits its blades; line the wheel with blue and brown wheel material if you like, but the mechanics only care about flow. Run water down both sides in the same rotational direction to get full output, and remember: stacking wheels on the same shaft does not multiply a single stream — give each its own water.

Windmills

Build a cross or radial pattern of Sail (or wool/any sail-tagged) blocks, attach it to a Mechanical Bearing pointing at open sky, and right-click the bearing to assemble it into a spinning Windmill Contraption. Capacity rises with sail count up to a ceiling; speed is fixed-ish and slow, so windmills are a capacity source, not a speed source.

Steam power — the endgame SU

The late-game answer to "I need more stress than the world can give." A Fluid Tank filled with water is heated from below by Blaze Burners to become a Boiler; the boiler feeds Steam Engines mounted on its sides, each engine driving a shaft. Output scales in tiers with the tank's water volume and the number of (ideally Blaze-fed, super-heated) burners. A maxed boiler dwarfs every other generator combined.

Build capacity before consumers

Add the generator first, watch the goggles meter, then attach machines. Overstressing is not a soft warning — the network halts, contraptions freeze mid-motion, and you'll be hunting for the culprit. Always keep visible headroom.

02 — Gearing & routing

Controlling Rotation

Raw rotation off a generator is rarely the speed or direction you want. Create's gearing parts let you reroute, reverse, multiply and precisely set RPM — each with consequences for stress.

Carrying rotation

  • Shafts — straight-line transmission. Encase them for tidy builds; they carry the same speed end to end.
  • Cogwheels & Large Cogwheels — mesh side by side to transfer power. A small ↔ large pairing is a 2× gear ratio: small-to-large halves speed (and doubles torque headroom), large-to-small doubles speed.
  • Belts — connect two shafts at a distance and carry items on top. Belted shafts share the same speed.
  • Gearboxes & Vertical Gearboxes — turn rotation around corners and between axes without changing speed.
  • Chain Drives & Encased Chain Drive — link several parallel shafts at once, all at the same speed; great for driving a row of machines.
The control blocks and what they do
BlockEffectDriven by
GearshiftReverses rotation directionRedstone signal toggles it
ClutchDisconnects power (free-spins the far side)Redstone signal cuts it
Rotation Speed ControllerSets an exact target RPM on a large cogScroll-set value in its GUI
Adjustable Chain GearshiftVariable ratio on a chain-drive lineRedstone strength = ratio
Sequenced GearshiftRuns a timed program of moves (turn N°, wait, reverse…)Redstone pulse starts the sequence

Setting an exact speed

The Rotation Speed Controller (RSC) sits under a Large Cogwheel and lets you dial a precise RPM — positive or negative — regardless of input speed, as long as your network has the stress to back it. This is the clean way to feed a machine its ideal speed without a fragile tower of cogs.

Speed costs stress, not power

There is no efficiency loss in gearing, but faster shafts impose proportionally more stress on the machines they drive. The cheapest way to run a heavy line is often to gear it down to the minimum RPM the machines need, freeing capacity for more machines elsewhere.

Mind the rotation conflict

If two power sources try to spin the same shaft in opposite directions, the network seizes with a rotation conflict. Use Clutches and Gearshifts deliberately and don't accidentally close a loop back onto itself.

03 — Turning rotation into goods

Processing & Recipes

This is where rotation earns its keep. Create's processing blocks grind, press, mix, cut, wash and assemble — and most of them chain together over belts and depots into automated lines.

The core processing machines
MachineDoesHeadline use
MillstoneGrinds a single item (self-contained, slow)Early ore → crushed ore, flour, gravel
Crushing Wheels (pair)Crush items dropped between two counter-rotating wheelsBulk ore doubling, fast crushing
Mechanical PressStamps items on a belt/depotSheets, packing, compacting
Mechanical Mixer + BasinMixes/compacts multiple inputs (needs heat for some)Brass, Andesite Alloy, alloys & foods
Mechanical SawCuts logs/blocks; fells trees when mountedPlanks, stripped logs, stonecutting recipes
Encased FanProjects the block behind it (fire/water/air) onto itemsSmelting, smoking, washing, splashing
Spout & Item DrainFills items with fluid / drains filled itemsFluid-based recipes, bottling
DeployerUses a held item on a block/item in front of it"Right-click" automation, in-world crafting
Mechanical Crafter (array)Crafts a shaped recipe across a gridPrecision Mechanism, casings, multi-step crafts

The Encased Fan is secretly four machines

Place a fan and it processes items in the airflow according to the block behind it:

  • Fire / lava / campfire behind → smelting (and smoking via a campfire for food).
  • Flowing water behind → washing (Bulk Washing): crushed ore → nuggets, gravel → flint/clay, sifting drops.
  • Open air → splashing-adjacent and general air recipes.

Fan processing is the cheapest bulk smelter/washer in the mod — point a fan at a furnace-fire and run a belt through the breeze.

Ore doubling, the practical version

  1. Crushing Wheels (a counter-rotating pair, geared so they spin toward each other) crush raw ore into crushed ore, yielding extra plus a chance of nuggets.
  2. Encased Fan over water washes the crushed ore for bonus nuggets, squeezing out the last fraction of yield.
  3. Encased Fan over fire (or any furnace) smelts the result back into ingots. A single belt can run all three stages in a row.

Mixing the foundational metals

The Mechanical Mixer over a Basin compacts and alloys. Andesite Alloy comes from andesite plus iron or zinc nuggets; Brass comes from zinc + copper (often crushed first, then mixed with heat). Many mixing recipes require a Blaze Burner under the basin for heat — passive, heated, or super-heated changes what recipes are available.

Depots, belts and funnels are the conveyor

Single items process on a Depot; streams process on a Belt. A press, saw or fan straddling a moving belt processes everything that passes underneath — no per-item triggering needed. This is the difference between a toy and a factory.

04 — Moving things around

Item & Fluid Logistics

Create moves items in the open, on belts and through funnels you can watch and filter. Fluids run through a separate pipe network. Both reward thinking in flows.

Items

  • Belts — visible item transport; also the surface processing happens on.
  • Funnels (Andesite / Brass) — pull items into or push out of inventories and belts. Brass Funnels can filter by item; Andesite ones cannot.
  • Chutes & Smart Chutes — vertical item transport; Smart Chutes filter and count.
  • Mechanical Arm — a configurable arm that picks from several inputs and places to several outputs by rule; the flexible router of the mod.
  • Tunnels (Brass / Andesite) — split, merge and balance belt lines; Brass Tunnels can synchronise, round-robin, randomise and filter.

Filtering

The Filter item slots into Brass funnels, tunnels, arms and more. Two advanced variants make complex sorting trivial:

list

Smart Filter

An item with its own slots — whitelist/blacklist a set of items, even nesting other filters. Drop it into any filter slot.

rules

Attribute Filter

Filters by properties rather than exact items: "is a tool", "has fortune", "is food", "in tag". Powerful for messy sorting.

push

Brass Funnel

The everyday filtering funnel. Set one item (or a filter) and it only moves matching items.

split

Brass Tunnel

Balances and routes belt lines: round-robin to keep parallel machines evenly fed.

Fluids

Fluids are their own network and do not mix with item logistics:

  • Fluid Pipes — carry liquids; a pipe network needs flow driven by a pump and has a wind-up before it moves at full rate.
  • Mechanical Pump — kinetically powered; pushes fluid one direction (reversible with rotation direction or redstone).
  • Fluid Tanks — bulk storage; stack them into multiblocks for large volume and as boiler vessels.
  • Hose Pulley — fills or drains entire bodies of fluid; over an "ocean-sized" source it draws infinitely without draining it.
  • Smart Fluid Pipe — restricts a pipe to a single fluid type, preventing cross-contamination.
  • Portable Fluid / Storage Interface — transfer fluids/items to and from moving contraptions at a docking point.
Pipes need a reason to flow

An idle Create pipe network does nothing — it needs a pressure differential from a pump, or a tank that wants filling/draining. If fluid "won't move," check that a pump is actually powered and pointed the right way, and that there's somewhere for the fluid to go.

05 — The headline feature

Trains & Tracks

Create 0.5's banner addition: real, drivable, schedulable trains built from track, bogeys and assembled carriages. They route automatically across a network and can haul cargo, players and whole contraptions.

The railway toolkit
PartRole
Train TrackThe rail itself — laid in curves, slopes and junctions with a paving tool for long runs.
Train StationAssembly & control point: build, name, dispatch and dock trains here.
Train BogeyThe wheel-trucks placed on track; carriages mount on bogeys.
Train Casing / CarriageThe body you build over bogeys, then assemble at a station into a carriage.
Train CouplingsLink carriages into a longer train.
Train SignalsBlock-signal traffic control so multiple trains don't collide.
ScheduleA programmable itinerary item placed in the train: go to station X, wait, conditions, repeat.
Train ControlsStand-on controls to drive a train manually.

From rails to a running line

  1. Lay Train Track and place a Station against it. Use the station's "new train" mode to mark the assembly zone.
  2. Set Bogeys on the track and build your carriage body (casings, storage, seats, even mounted contraptions) over them.
  3. Assemble at the station — the build becomes a train entity. Name it.
  4. Insert a Schedule with stops and conditions, or hop on the Train Controls and drive it yourself.
  5. Add Signals on busy networks so trains hold at blocks instead of colliding.
Stations are the brains

Trains navigate between named stations. A schedule references station names, so plan a clear naming scheme early. Station blocks also let you import/export cargo automatically while a train is docked, turning a rail line into a logistics backbone.

Signals or smash-ups

Without signals, two scheduled trains on shared track will eventually meet. Place Train Signals to divide track into blocks; a train waits at a red signal until the block ahead clears.

06 — Things that move

Contraptions

A contraption is a group of blocks that Create temporarily turns into a single moving entity — a rotating windmill, a rising elevator platform, a sweeping drill. Each is built around an actuator block that you trigger to assemble and move it.

The actuators
ActuatorMotionTypical build
Mechanical BearingRotates an attached structureWindmills, rotating houses, fan/clock arms
Linear Chassis + Mechanical PistonPushes/pulls a structure in a lineDoors, drawbridges, moving walls (+ Piston Extension Poles for reach)
Rope / Linear PulleyRaises and lowers a platformElevators, lifts
Gantry Shaft + Gantry CarriageDrives a carriage along a shaft, reading positionAuto-miners, scanning machines, precise placement
Cart AssemblerMounts a contraption on a minecartMobile drills/harvesters on vanilla rail

Binding blocks: chassis & glue

A moving contraption only takes the blocks it's told to. Two ways to declare that group:

  • Radial / Linear Chassis — attach blocks within a configurable range (scroll to set how far it grips); ideal for solid platforms and pistons.
  • Super Glue — paint a box around any blocks to bind them together, even oddly-shaped builds the chassis range can't cover.

Mounting machines on the move

Drills, saws, deployers, harvesters and dispensers all keep working while mounted on a contraption. That's how you get a piston-driven tunnel borer, a bearing-mounted tree farm, or a gantry auto-quarry. Contraption Controls let you operate mounted machinery remotely, and Portable Storage/Fluid Interfaces dock the cargo when it returns home.

Assemble, then move

Most actuators need a redstone or right-click trigger to assemble the build into a contraption before it moves. If your piston "won't push," confirm the blocks are glued/chassis-bound and that the actuator has both power and a trigger.

07 — The kit

Tools & Equipment

A handful of tools make Create bearable to build with — and a few wearables turn the mod into a toolbox you carry.

essential

Wrench

Rotates blocks, configures and picks up kinetic blocks cleanly. The single most-used tool — craft it before anything else.

diagnostic

Engineer's Goggles

Worn, they overlay stress capacity vs impact and speed on any kinetic block you look at. You're flying blind without them.

reach

Extendo Grip

Extends your interaction range — invaluable for placing and wrenching across big contraptions.

combat

Potato Cannon

A goofy-but-real ranged weapon firing food ammo with different effects. Pairs with the goggles' Hat slot cosmetics.

Diving & movement gear

The Copper Backtank stores compressed air (wind it up with rotation or wear it while it fills) and powers the Diving Helmet and Diving Boots — breathe underwater and walk the seabed. Useful long before any "suit" tier in other mods.

Schematics & the Schematicannon

Create can build for you. Save a structure to a Schematic at a Schematic Table, position it in-world with the Schematic item, then load it into a Schematicannon. Fed gunpowder (as propellant) and the actual building materials, the cannon places the structure block by block — the fastest way to clone a working factory or stamp out repeated builds.

Goggles + Wrench is the whole survival kit

If you take two items into a Create build session, take these. The wrench fixes orientation mistakes instantly; the goggles tell you why a network stalled (overstressed? under minimum speed?) before you start tearing things apart.

08 — The tiering ladder

Materials & the Casing Ladder

Create's progression is a quiet material ladder. Each tier of casing and component unlocks smarter machines, with one notorious bottleneck in the middle.

Andesite CasingAndesite Alloy + stripped logEncased shafts & the early machine block

The Precision Mechanism wall

Most of Create's "smart" blocks — Mechanical Bearings, Deployers, Rotation Speed Controllers, Gearshifts, the Mechanical Arm — require Precision Mechanisms, and those are built in a Mechanical Crafter array from cogwheels, brass and an Electron Tube. Setting up a Precision Mechanism factory early is the best investment in the mid-game: it converts a recurring tedious craft into a hands-off supply.

Rose Quartz & Electron Tubes

Rose Quartz (from washing crushed materials, then crafting) is "charged" via a Deployer/recipe into Polished Rose Quartz and combined with brass into Electron Tubes — the little glowing component that gates the smart tier. It's a small chain, but automating it removes the only real grind in Create progression.

Read your tier off the casing

If you can craft Brass Casing on demand, you're mid-game and everything below it should already be automated. If you're still hand-crafting Andesite Alloy, build a Mixer + Basin line for it before anything fancier — it's the bottleneck under everything.

09 — The decorative half

Aesthetics & Building

Half of Create's reputation is looks. The mod ships an enormous palette of building blocks and quality-of-life tools that have nothing to do with stress — and everything to do with making a factory feel like a workshop.

  • Stone & brick variants — cut, layered, polished and pillared forms of many vanilla and Create stones, processed through Create's stonecutting-style recipes for huge decorative range.
  • Copper, weathered & oxidising — copper-themed blocks, shingles, tiles and pipes that age through oxidation stages (waxable to freeze the patina).
  • Windows, Trapdoors, Ladders, Train Doors — themed in each material family so machinery and architecture share a palette.
  • Sails, banners & flaps — the moving-part decorations that double as windmill material.

Quality-of-life building tools

notes

Clipboard

A writable in-world checklist/notepad — track to-dos, material counts or station names without leaving the game.

storage

Toolbox

A placeable tool chest you can link to your hotbar; pull tools wirelessly within range and auto-return them. Build-session bliss.

align

Wrench

Doubles as your decorating chisel: rotate slabs, stairs, windows and machinery to point exactly where you want.

copy

Schematicannon

Not just for factories — clone decorative structures across your base for instant, consistent architecture.

Function and form share a kit

Because casings, copper and brass blocks come in decorative variants, you can wrap working machinery in matching trim. A pretty Create base isn't a separate project — it's the same blocks, finished.

10 — The best teacher

Learn It In-Game: Ponder

Create ships its own interactive tutorial system, and it is genuinely the fastest way to learn any block. This guide gives you the map; Ponder shows you the territory in motion.

  • Ponder scenes — animated, narrated demonstrations built into nearly every Create item. Hover the item and press W (in JEI/inventory) to watch the block assemble itself and explain its rules step by step.
  • JEI integration — for recipes (what crushes into what, what the mixer accepts), JEI is ground truth for your exact pack version, which may differ from any written guide.
  • The goggles overlay — once a network exists, the Engineer's Goggles are your live debugger for stress and speed.
When in doubt, Ponder it

If a block confuses you, don't read another paragraph — point at it in your inventory and press W. Create's Ponder scenes answer "which way does this face?" and "what feeds in here?" better than any text can.

Numbers drift; mechanics don't

Exact SU values, RPM thresholds and recipe yields are config-tunable and shift between Create versions and modpacks. Trust the mechanics described here — generators add capacity, machines impose stress, gearing trades speed for headroom — and trust your in-game goggles and JEI for the precise figures.
