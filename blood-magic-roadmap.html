How the roadmap works

Each Act is a self-contained chunk of progress with the same five parts: a one-line goal, what you need first, a tickable to-do list (your boxes are saved in this browser), a red milestone gate that tells you exactly when you're done and may move on, and a watch-out for the trap that kills most people at that stage. The chips at the foot of each Act jump to the matching deep-dive in the reference grimoire.

Do the Acts in order. Every one unlocks the tool that makes the next one painless — skip ahead and you'll spend an hour bleeding for something you could have automated.

Act 0 — Before the first cut

Provisions

Set up a room where bleeding yourself won't get you killed.

Self-sacrifice drains real health. The single most common Blood Magic death is tapping the altar one heart too low. Two minutes of prep here saves you a lost inventory later.

Milestone — move on when

You have a safe, lit room, food/regen on hand, and a diamond + stone in your pocket. Never sacrifice below ~7 hearts.

Why so careful

Early on you have no LP buffer and no living armour. A creeper or a mis-click at 2 hearts ends the run. Discipline now; shortcuts later.

Act I — The opening

First Blood

Stand up a working altar, bind your first Blood Orb, and ring it to Tier 2.

Need first: the Act 0 provisions.

Orb craft · 2,000 LP Slate · 1,000 LP ea Runes · ×8 for T2
Milestone — Tier 2 reached

The Divination Sigil reads your altar as Tier 2 and you carry a bound Weak Blood Orb. You can now craft Apprentice-tier items.

Watch out

Don't bleed your whole health bar chasing a craft. The altar keeps LP it has already absorbed — you can top it up across many small, safe sacrifices.

Act II — Stop hurting yourself

The Apprentice's Bench

Get an LP-fuelled workbench and your first quality-of-life sigils so you bleed less and craft more.

Need first: Tier 2 altar + bound Weak Blood Orb.

Apprentice orb · 5,000 LP T3 · 28 runes + 4 pillars
Milestone — Tier 3 reached

Altar reads Tier 3 (66,000-ish cap once runed), you have an Alchemy Table, and at least the Water + Fast Miner sigils. Life is already easier.

Watch out

Sigils are bound on a ground array, not in the altar — the altar only forges the slate. If a sigil recipe "won't craft," you're probably looking in the wrong place.

Act III — Reach & comfort

The Magician's Reach

Climb to the Magician's orb, read your altar's live progress, and start the Tranquility build that makes bleeding fast.

Need first: Tier 3 altar + Alchemy Table.

Magician orb · 25,000 LP Incense · up to +300% self-sac
Milestone — comfortable bleeding

You hold a Magician's orb, the Seer Sigil, and an Incense Altar pushing your self-sacrifice well past +100%. Big crafts no longer mean dozens of taps.

Watch out

The charged "shining dagger" sacrifice takes ~90% of your health in one go. Only use it at full health with regen ready — it is the second-most-common Blood Magic death.

Act IV — Make LP without you

Hands Free

Stop bleeding by hand. Build the ritual system and put a Well of Suffering to work.

Need first: Magician's orb, a Tier-3 altar, and a mob source (a dark room or vanilla spawner).

Well · 40,000 LP activate Master orb · 40,000 LP T4 · 56 runes
Milestone — passive LP

A Well of Suffering is filling your altar while you do other things, and you carry a Master Blood Orb on a Tier-4 altar. From here, LP is a faucet, not a chore.

Watch out

Activation LP is pulled all at once — your network must already hold the full 40,000 before the Well will light. Build capacity first, then activate.

Tier 4 · Will

Act V — The other economy

The Second Currency

Open Demon Will: gather it, store it, and build a garden that grows it while you sleep.

Need first: a Tier-4 altar and a working mob farm (your Well's grinder doubles as Will income).

Greater gem · 4,096 Will Garden · passive crystals
Milestone — self-feeding Will

A Crucible + Crystallizer garden is growing crystals on its own, and a Sentient Sword + Greater gem keep you stocked in the field. Both currencies now flow without you grinding.

Watch out

Chicken-and-egg: crystallizers need a full aura they can't fill themselves. Prime the Crucible with a few charged gems first, then let the loop run.

Act VI — Stop dying

Flesh & Steel

Wear armour that levels itself, then arm up with LP- and Will-powered tools.

Need first: a Tier-4 altar, Arcane Ash + Binding Reagent (Alchemy Table), and a Will gem if you want sentient gear.

Armor budget · 100 → 200 Lava Crystal · LP fuel
Milestone — kitted out

You're wearing trained Living Armor (cap raised by the Draft), carrying a powered weapon and pickaxe, and smelting on a Lava Crystal. You can take a hit and dig a base fast.

Watch out

Living Armor points are shared across all abilities. Don't smear them thin — pick a build, and re-spec rather than over-spending.

★ — If you only remember five things

The Five-Step Path

Lost in the detail? The entire mod, boiled down to the five moves that matter. Do these in order and everything else slots in around them.

  1. Bleed a Weak Orb, ring to Tier 2. Altar + dagger + Divination Sigil + diamond. Now you're playing.
  2. Build the Alchemy Table + an Incense Altar. Cheaper sigils and far faster self-sacrifice.
  3. Light a Well of Suffering over a mob farm. This is the moment LP stops being work.
  4. Open Demon Will: Sentient Sword + a Crucible/Crystallizer garden. The second economy, self-feeding.
  5. Reach Tier 5, make Living Armor, automate with Routing Nodes. You're done — go build the Crypt.
