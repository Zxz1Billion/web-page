The Ascent

A whole-pack roadmap for To the Moon 2.0: seven stages that carry you from a stone pickaxe and a cooking pot to fission reactors, a full mage's wardrobe, and a rocket standing on the launch pad. It doesn't replace the individual guides — it sequences them, telling you what to build next and where to read the detail.

00 — Orientation

How to Use This Roadmap

To the Moon 2.0 is an open pack — there is no single quest gate forcing one order (though the questline companion offers one if you want structure). Most players braid several tracks at once: tech, magic, combat, logistics and building.

The stages below are capability tiers, not a strict checklist. Each stage lists the milestone that "unlocks" the next, the mods you'll lean on, and a tip for not getting stuck. When a stage points at a system, the linked guide has the full detail.

The golden rule

Whenever a boss or dungeon out-scales you, drop back a stage and tech up — better tools (Tinkers), an Apotheosis-affixed weapon, or a spell — then return. The pack is balanced around bouncing between tracks.

Before you do anything dangerous

Set up your safety net first: a Corail Tombstone grave keeps your gear on death, and a Comforts sleeping bag lets you rest in the field without moving your spawn. Both pay for themselves the first time a Mutant Creeper finds you.

01 — Stage 0

Landing — the first night

Vanilla survival, but with better food and a softer death penalty. Get fed, armed, and insured.

  1. Eat well. Stand up a Farmer's Delight kitchen — a Cooking Pot over a stove gives hearty, long-lasting meals that beat raw steak for exploration.
  2. First real tools. Build a Tinkers' Construct Part Builder + Tinker Station and assemble a pick and a sword you can repair and upgrade forever.
  3. Carry capacity. Craft a Sophisticated or Traveler's backpack so loot doesn't pin you to base.
  4. Insurance. Sort out Tombstone graves and a Comforts sleeping bag before your first cave dive.

Gate to Stage 1: a steady food supply, a Tinkers tool set, and enough iron/osmium/tin to start machines.

02 — Stage 1

First Machines — doubling everything

The moment ore stops being 1:1. Pick a tech entry point (you can run more than one):

recommended

Mekanism

Metallurgic Infuser + Enrichment Chamber + Energized Smelter = 2× ore immediately, and the alloys/circuits that unlock the whole pack. See the Assembly Line for the exact build order.

kinetic

Create

A water wheel + Millstone/Crushing Wheels doubles ore with no power grid — pure mechanical charm.

classic

Tinkers Smeltery

Even a small Smeltery melts ore into extra metal and casts your tool parts — a magic-free multiplication path.

Store the output

Bulk single items into Storage Drawers or Functional Storage; keep an Iron Chest or two for odds and ends.

Gate to Stage 2: a working ore-doubling line and a basic generator you can scale.

03 — Stage 2

Power & Logistics — make it flow

Stop hand-carrying items and hand-cranking power. Build a grid and let it run.

Power

  • Mekanism Gas-Burning Generator on home-brewed ethylene is the mid-game workhorse; IC2 Classic offers a parallel EU tech tree (mind the voltage tiers!).
  • Move it without cables using Flux Networks — wireless FE across your whole base.

Logistics

  • Pipez for simple tiered item/fluid/energy/gas runs; XNet when you want a cheap, fast router with filters; Modular Routers to replace a wall of hoppers with one block.
  • For searchable storage, graduate from drawers to a Tom's Simple Storage terminal, then to a full Applied Energistics 2 network with autocrafting.
  • Need conditional logic (do X only when Y)? That's Integrated Dynamics.

Gate to Stage 3: automated power feeding automated machines, and a storage system you can search.

04 — Stage 3

Magic & Gear — sharpen the edge

Now arm yourself for what's out there. These tracks run in parallel with tech.

blood

Blood Magic

Build the altar, ring it with runes, and start the LP economy. The Long Bleed roadmap sequences it act by act.

arcane

Iron's Spells

Pick a school, stack spell power + cooldown gear, and carry a spell-book offhand for ranged punch.

loot

Apotheosis

Reforge affixed gear, socket gems, and master the Eterna/Quanta/Arcana enchanting rework for top-tier enchants.

forge

Tinkers Modifiers

A full Smeltery lets you cast Manyullyn/Hepatizon parts and stack modifiers into a weapon that out-scales vanilla netherite.

Gate to Stage 4: a weapon and armour set you trust against a mini-boss, plus healing, totems and a grave net.

05 — Stage 4

Exploration — raid the world

The pack is packed with structures and bosses that drop gear you can't craft. Go take it.

  • Structures: YUNG's overhauled dungeons/strongholds/temples, When Dungeons Arise keeps and galleons, and the multi-floor Dungeon Crawl. Use the Cartographer's Journal as the overview map.
  • A whole dimension: open the Twilight Forest and start its locked boss ladder (Naga first).
  • Hunt bosses: Mowzie's lair bosses and Mutant Monsters for unique gear — read the Wilds Bestiary for tactics.
  • Shop: trade with Goblin Traders while you're underground anyway.

Gate to Stage 5: boss-tier loot and the resources (uranium, advanced alloys) to go nuclear.

06 — Stage 5

Heavy Industry — the big multiblocks

Scale everything. This is where Mekanism's late game and AE2's autocrafting take over your base.

  1. Go nuclear. A Mekanism Boiler + Industrial Turbine on a Fission Reactor produces orders of magnitude more power — store the surplus in an Induction Matrix. (Respect the meltdown warnings.)
  2. Autocraft it all. A full AE2 network with Crafting CPUs and Pattern Providers turns "go make 64 of X" into one click.
  3. Suit up. Assemble the MekaSuit and Meka-Tool and load them with modules — flight, auto-feed, radiation immunity, vein mining. This is your endgame body.
  4. Bridge your grids. If you ran IC2 too, use the EU/FE Bridge so every system shares power.

Gate to Stage 6: abundant power, a MekaSuit, and the alloys/plates a rocket demands.

07 — Stage 6

To the Moon — and the apex fights

The pack's namesake. With a tech base humming and a wardrobe of boss gear, leave the planet.

  • Build a rocket. Follow Ad Astra through the Tier 1–4 rockets, oxygen distributors and space suits, and reach the Moon → Mars → Venus → Mercury → Glacio, setting up sealed off-world bases.
  • Finish the boss ladders. Clear the Twilight Forest up to the Snow Queen, and take on L_Ender's Cataclysm's structure-bosses across every dimension for the strongest gear in the pack.
  • The final flex. Mekanism's Fusion Reactor and SPS turn fission waste into antimatter, feeding the Antiprotonic Nucleosynthesizer — the literal endgame fabricator.
There is no "end"

Once you're off-world with a fusion plant and antimatter, the pack becomes a sandbox: perfect your builds, automate the last holdouts, and decorate that space station. The journey was the point.
